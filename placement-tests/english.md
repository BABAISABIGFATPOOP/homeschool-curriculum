# English Placement Test

**Instructions:** Answer each question as completely as you can. Stop when it becomes too difficult.

## Section A (Grade 4–5 Level)

**1.** Read the sentence and identify the **noun**, **verb**, and **adjective**:
*"The small dog chased the red ball across the yard."*

**2.** Rewrite this sentence with correct capitalization and punctuation:
*"my friend sarah went to new york last tuesday"*

**3.** What is the **main idea** of this paragraph?
*"Bees are very important to our world. They help flowers grow by carrying pollen from one flower to another. Without bees, many of the fruits and vegetables we eat would not exist."*

**4.** Write the **past tense** of each word: run, eat, go, think, swim

**5.** What does the word "enormous" mean? Use it in a sentence.

**6.** Read the passage and answer the question:
*"Jake looked out the window. The sky was dark, and the trees were bending sideways. Leaves flew past the glass. Mom said they should move away from the windows."*
What is happening outside? How do you know?

**7.** Is this a **complete sentence** or a **fragment**?
- a) "Running quickly through the park."
- b) "The cat sat on the mat."

## Section B (Grade 6–7 Level)

**8.** Identify the **subject** and **predicate** in this sentence:
*"The group of students from the third floor won the science competition."*

**9.** What is the difference between **their**, **there**, and **they're**? Write a sentence using each one correctly.

**10.** Read the passage:
*"Marcus didn't want to admit it, but the old house fascinated him. Every creak of the floorboards sounded like a whisper. He told himself it was just the wind, but his hands trembled as he reached for the doorknob."*
What **mood/tone** does the author create? What words help create it?

**11.** What is a **thesis statement**? Write one for this topic: "Should students have homework?"

**12.** Identify the **prefix** and **root word** in each: unlock, prehistoric, misunderstand

**13.** Rewrite this paragraph to fix the errors:
*"Me and my friend goes to the store. We buyed some chips, and than we gone home. Their was nothing on TV so we played games."*

**14.** What is the difference between a **simile** and a **metaphor**? Give an example of each.

## Section C (Grade 8 Level)

**15.** Read the passage:
*"The company claimed its new drink was 'all natural,' but a quick look at the ingredient list revealed artificial sweeteners, preservatives, and dyes. The label featured images of fresh fruit, though the drink contained less than 2% real juice."*
Is the author being **objective or critical**? How do you know? What is the author's implied argument?

**16.** Write a paragraph (4–6 sentences) arguing **for or against** social media for teenagers. Include a **topic sentence**, **evidence/reasoning**, and a **concluding sentence**.

**17.** Define **active voice** and **passive voice**. Rewrite this sentence from passive to active:
*"The homework was completed by the students before lunch."*

**18.** What is **parallel structure**? Fix this sentence:
*"She likes hiking, to swim, and playing tennis."*

**19.** What does it mean to **cite a source**? Why is it important?

## Section D (Grade 9–10 Level)

**20.** Read the excerpt:
*"It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife."* — Jane Austen, *Pride and Prejudice*
What **literary device** is Austen using? What is she really saying about society?

**21.** What is a **logical fallacy**? Identify the fallacy in this statement:
*"Everyone in my class has a smartphone, so smartphones must be essential for learning."*

**22.** Write a **thesis statement** and a **brief outline** (intro, 3 body points, conclusion) for this prompt:
*"How does the environment a person grows up in shape who they become?"*

**23.** Explain the difference between **ethos**, **pathos**, and **logos**. Give a one-sentence example of each.

**24.** Read this poem excerpt and analyze it:
*"Two roads diverged in a wood, and I— / I took the one less traveled by, / And that has made all the difference."* — Robert Frost
What is the **theme**? Is the speaker confident or uncertain? What evidence supports your reading?
