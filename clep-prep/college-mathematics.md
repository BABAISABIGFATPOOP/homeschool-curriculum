# College Mathematics CLEP Study Guide

## Exam Overview

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Format** | Multiple choice |
| **Number of Questions** | 60 |
| **Time Limit** | 90 minutes |
| **Passing Score** | ~50 out of 80 (scaled); approximately 50% correct |
| **College Credit** | Typically 6 semester hours |
| **Calculator** | On-screen scientific calculator provided |

This exam covers material typically taught in a general education college math course (non-calculus). It is broader but shallower than College Algebra, covering a wide range of topics.

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Sets (10%)

- **Set notation**: roster method, set-builder notation
- **Types of sets**: empty set, universal set, finite, infinite
- **Subsets and proper subsets**
- **Set operations**: union, intersection, complement, difference
- **Venn diagrams**: two-set and three-set problems
- **Cardinality**: counting elements, inclusion-exclusion principle
- **Cartesian product**

**Key Concepts:**
- Union (A U B): all elements in A or B or both
- Intersection (A ∩ B): only elements in both A and B
- Complement (A'): everything in the universal set not in A
- Inclusion-Exclusion: |A U B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B|

### 2. Logic (10%)

- **Statements and truth values**
- **Logical connectives**: and (∧), or (∨), not (~), conditional (→), biconditional (↔)
- **Truth tables**: constructing and reading them
- **Conditional statements**: converse, inverse, contrapositive
- **Logical equivalence and tautologies**
- **Quantifiers**: universal (∀) and existential (∃)
- **Valid arguments and fallacies**

**Key Concepts:**
- A conditional p → q is logically equivalent to its contrapositive (~q → ~p)
- A conditional is NOT equivalent to its converse (q → p)
- De Morgan's Laws: ~(p ∧ q) ≡ (~p ∨ ~q) and ~(p ∨ q) ≡ (~p ∧ ~q)
- A tautology is always true regardless of truth values

### 3. Real Number System (10%)

- **Properties of real numbers**: commutative, associative, distributive, identity, inverse
- **Types of numbers**: natural, whole, integers, rational, irrational, real
- **Absolute value**
- **Order of operations**
- **Exponents and radicals**: rules and simplification
- **Scientific notation**
- **Factors, multiples, primes, divisibility**

**Key Concepts:**
- Rational numbers can be written as a/b where b ≠ 0
- Irrational numbers have non-repeating, non-terminating decimals (e.g., sqrt(2), pi)
- Every integer is rational (e.g., 5 = 5/1)

### 4. Functions and Their Graphs (20%)

- **Definition of a function**: domain, range, vertical line test
- **Function notation**: f(x), evaluating functions
- **Types**: linear, quadratic, polynomial, absolute value, piecewise
- **Composition of functions**: (f ∘ g)(x) = f(g(x))
- **Inverse functions**: f⁻¹(x), horizontal line test
- **Graphing basics**: intercepts, symmetry, transformations (shifts, reflections, stretches)
- **Linear functions**: slope, slope-intercept form, point-slope form
- **Quadratic functions**: vertex, axis of symmetry, parabola direction

**Key Concepts:**
- Slope = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)
- Vertex form: f(x) = a(x - h)² + k, vertex at (h, k)
- For inverse functions: f(f⁻¹(x)) = x and f⁻¹(f(x)) = x
- Domain of f ∘ g: all x in domain of g where g(x) is in domain of f

### 5. Probability and Statistics (25%)

- **Counting principles**: multiplication rule, addition rule
- **Permutations**: P(n, r) = n! / (n - r)!
- **Combinations**: C(n, r) = n! / [r!(n - r)!]
- **Basic probability**: P(E) = favorable outcomes / total outcomes
- **Compound events**: independent, dependent, mutually exclusive
- **Conditional probability**: P(A|B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B)
- **Measures of central tendency**: mean, median, mode
- **Measures of spread**: range, variance, standard deviation
- **Normal distribution**: 68-95-99.7 rule, z-scores
- **Data interpretation**: bar graphs, histograms, pie charts, scatter plots, box plots
- **Expected value**

**Key Concepts:**
- P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
- For independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B)
- Standard deviation is the square root of variance
- z = (x - mean) / standard deviation
- Expected value = sum of [value * probability] for all outcomes

### 6. Geometry (5%)

- **Perimeter, area, and volume formulas** for common shapes
- **Pythagorean theorem**: a² + b² = c²
- **Properties of triangles**: angle sum = 180°, similar triangles, congruence
- **Properties of parallel lines cut by a transversal**
- **Circles**: circumference = 2πr, area = πr²
- **Coordinate geometry**: distance formula, midpoint formula

**Key Concepts:**
- Distance: d = sqrt[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]
- Midpoint: ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)
- Volume of a cylinder: V = πr²h
- Volume of a sphere: V = (4/3)πr³

### 7. Financial Mathematics (20%)

- **Simple interest**: I = Prt, A = P(1 + rt)
- **Compound interest**: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
- **Continuous compounding**: A = Pe^(rt)
- **Effective annual rate**
- **Annuities**: future value and present value
- **Loans and amortization**
- **Percent increase/decrease**
- **Markup and discount**

**Key Concepts:**
- P = principal, r = annual rate, t = time in years, n = compounding periods per year
- Effective rate = (1 + r/n)^n - 1
- Future value of annuity: FV = PMT * [(1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1] / (r/n)
- The more frequently interest compounds, the more total interest accrues

---

## Study Tips

1. Focus heavily on probability/statistics and financial math (together 45% of the exam).
2. Know your formulas cold, especially interest formulas and counting principles.
3. Practice reading Venn diagrams and truth tables quickly.
4. Use the provided calculator but don't depend on it for conceptual questions.
5. Process of elimination is powerful on this exam.

## Practice Questions

**1.** If A = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5} and B = {3, 4, 5, 6, 7}, what is |A U B|?

A) 5
B) 3
C) 7
D) 10

**2.** What is the contrapositive of "If it rains, then the ground is wet"?

A) If the ground is wet, then it rains.
B) If it does not rain, then the ground is not wet.
C) If the ground is not wet, then it does not rain.
D) It rains and the ground is not wet.

**3.** Which of the following numbers is irrational?

A) 0.75
B) sqrt(16)
C) sqrt(7)
D) -3/4

**4.** If f(x) = 2x + 3 and g(x) = x², what is (f ∘ g)(2)?

A) 11
B) 49
C) 7
D) 14

**5.** A committee of 3 is to be chosen from 8 people. How many different committees are possible?

A) 336
B) 56
C) 24
D) 512

**6.** A data set has a mean of 70 and a standard deviation of 5. What z-score corresponds to a value of 82?

A) 2.0
B) 2.4
C) 1.6
D) 12.0

**7.** You invest $2,000 at 6% annual interest compounded monthly. What is the balance after 3 years? (Round to the nearest cent.)

A) $2,360.00
B) $2,393.36
C) $2,388.10
D) $2,390.00

**8.** If p is true and q is false, what is the truth value of p → q?

A) True
B) False
C) Cannot be determined
D) Both true and false

**9.** A bag contains 4 red, 3 blue, and 5 green marbles. If one marble is drawn at random, what is the probability it is NOT green?

A) 5/12
B) 7/12
C) 1/3
D) 7/11

**10.** What is the simple interest earned on $5,000 at 4% annual interest for 2.5 years?

A) $500
B) $200
C) $400
D) $1,000

---

## Answer Key

1. **C) 7** — A U B = {1,2,3,4,5,6,7}, which has 7 elements. By inclusion-exclusion: 5 + 5 - 3 = 7.
2. **C) If the ground is not wet, then it does not rain.** — The contrapositive negates and reverses both parts.
3. **C) sqrt(7)** — 7 is not a perfect square, so its square root is irrational.
4. **A) 11** — g(2) = 4, then f(4) = 2(4) + 3 = 11.
5. **B) 56** — C(8,3) = 8! / (3! * 5!) = 56. Order doesn't matter for committees.
6. **B) 2.4** — z = (82 - 70) / 5 = 12 / 5 = 2.4.
7. **B) $2,393.36** — A = 2000(1 + 0.06/12)^(12*3) = 2000(1.005)^36 ≈ 2393.36.
8. **B) False** — A conditional p → q is only false when p is true and q is false.
9. **B) 7/12** — Total = 12. Not green = 4 + 3 = 7. P = 7/12.
10. **A) $500** — I = Prt = 5000 * 0.04 * 2.5 = 500.
