# CLEP Biology — Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** Which organelle is responsible for producing ATP through cellular respiration?

A) Nucleus
B) Ribosome
C) Mitochondria
D) Golgi apparatus

**2.** What is the basic unit of life?

A) Atom
B) Molecule
C) Cell
D) Organ

**3.** During which phase of mitosis do chromosomes line up at the cell's equator?

A) Prophase
B) Metaphase
C) Anaphase
D) Telophase

**4.** Which macromolecule stores genetic information?

A) Proteins
B) Lipids
C) Carbohydrates
D) Nucleic acids

A) Covalent bonds
B) Ionic bonds
C) Hydrogen bonds
D) Peptide bonds

A) Krebs cycle
B) Glycolysis
C) Electron transport chain
D) Calvin cycle

A) Energy production
B) Protein synthesis
C) Cell division
D) Lipid storage

A) Having two identical alleles for a trait
B) Having two different alleles for a trait

D) Having more than two alleles for a trait

A) Animalia
B) Fungi

D) Protista

**10.** What is the role of enzymes in biological reactions?

A) They provide energy for reactions.
B) They act as catalysts, lowering the activation energy.

D) They increase the activation energy.

**11.** Which type of RNA carries amino acids to the ribosome during translation?

A) mRNA
B) tRNA
C) rRNA
D) snRNA

**12.** What is natural selection?

A) The random change in allele frequencies in a population
B) The process by which organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce
C) The deliberate breeding of organisms by humans
D) The migration of organisms to new environments

**13.** Which of the following is NOT a function of the cell membrane?

A) Regulating what enters and exits the cell
B) Providing structural support
C) Storing genetic information
D) Cell signaling

A) Carotenoid

**15.** Which biome is characterized by permafrost and very low temperatures?

A) Taiga
B) Tundra
C) Desert
D) Savanna

A) Producers
B) Primary consumers
C) Decomposers
D) Tertiary consumers

A) Prokaryotic cells have a nucleus; eukaryotic cells do not.
B) Eukaryotic cells have a membrane-bound nucleus; prokaryotic cells do not.
C) Prokaryotic cells are larger than eukaryotic cells.
D) Eukaryotic cells lack ribosomes.

A) Glucagon
B) Insulin
C) Cortisol
D) Epinephrine

**19.** What is the genotype ratio of a cross between two heterozygous parents (Aa × Aa)?

A) 1:2:1
B) 3:1
C) 1:1
D) 1:3:1

**20.** Which structure in the plant cell provides rigidity and support?

A) Cell membrane
B) Chloroplast
C) Cell wall
D) Vacuole

**21.** What is the process by which water moves across a semipermeable membrane from an area of low solute concentration to high solute concentration?

A) Diffusion
B) Osmosis
C) Active transport
D) Endocytosis

**22.** Which of the following is an example of a density-dependent limiting factor?

A) Earthquake
B) Disease
C) Volcanic eruption
D) Flood

**23.** What is the function of the lymphatic system?

A) To transport oxygen
B) To filter blood and support immune function
C) To digest food
D) To produce hormones

**24.** In meiosis, how many daughter cells are produced from one parent cell?

A) 2
B) 4

D) 1

A) Parasitism
B) Commensalism
C) Mutualism
D) Predation

A) Oxygen
B) Glucose
C) Carbon dioxide

A) Cerebrum
B) Cerebellum
C) Medulla oblongata
D) Hypothalamus

A) Secondary succession
B) Primary succession

A) Huntington's disease
B) Sickle cell anemia
C) Marfan syndrome

A) Kingdom, Phylum, Class, Order, Family, Genus, Species

A) Photosynthesis
B) Synthesis and transport of proteins and lipids
C) DNA replication
D) Cell division

A) All populations evolve over time.
B) Allele frequencies in a population remain constant when no evolutionary forces act upon it.

B) Blood clotting
C) Fighting infection and disease
D) Nutrient transport

A) Primary consumers
B) Secondary consumers
C) Producers
D) Tertiary consumers

**38.** What is the function of the Golgi apparatus?

A) Energy production
B) Modifying, packaging, and shipping proteins
C) DNA storage
D) Cell division

**39.** Which of the following best describes "codominance"?

A) One allele is completely dominant over the other.
B) Both alleles are expressed equally in the phenotype.
C) The traits blend together.
D) One allele is lethal.

**40.** What is the greenhouse effect?

A) The trapping of heat by gases in Earth's atmosphere
B) The release of oxygen by plants
C) The cooling of Earth's surface
D) The depletion of the ozone layer

A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin K

**42.** What is the role of helicase in DNA replication?

A) It joins Okazaki fragments.
B) It unwinds the double helix.
C) It adds nucleotides to the new strand.
D) It proofreads the new DNA.

**43.** Which of the following is an example of convergent evolution?

A) Darwin's finches developing different beak shapes
B) Wings in bats and wings in birds
C) Humans and chimpanzees sharing a common ancestor
D) Different breeds of dogs

**44.** What is the function of stomata in plants?

A) Water absorption
B) Gas exchange (CO₂ and O₂)
C) Nutrient storage
D) Reproduction

**45.** Which blood type is considered the universal donor?

A) Type A
B) Type B
C) Type AB
D) Type O negative

**46.** What is a keystone species?

A) The most abundant species in an ecosystem
B) A species whose presence has a disproportionately large effect on the ecosystem
C) The largest species in an ecosystem
D) A species that is always at the top of the food chain

**47.** Which of the following best describes "gene flow"?

A) The random change in allele frequency due to chance
B) The transfer of alleles between populations through migration
C) The selection of favorable traits by nature
D) The mutation of genes during DNA replication

**48.** What is the function of the pancreas?

A) Only insulin production
B) Production of digestive enzymes and hormones (insulin and glucagon)
C) Bile production
D) Filtering blood

A) Nitrification
B) Denitrification
C) Nitrogen fixation
D) Ammonification

A) An epidemic is more severe than a pandemic.
B) An epidemic is widespread in a region; a pandemic spans multiple countries or continents.
C) A pandemic affects only animals; an epidemic affects humans.
D) There is no difference.

A) They are autotrophic.
B) They have cell walls made of cellulose.
C) They are heterotrophic and have cell walls made of chitin.
D) They lack cell walls.

B) Acting as the natural pacemaker that initiates heartbeat

A) Mutualism
