# College Composition CLEP Practice Exam 3

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 50 (multiple choice section)
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.

> **Important Note:** The actual CLEP College Composition exam also includes TWO essay sections. This practice exam covers only the multiple-choice portion. Be sure to practice timed essay writing as well.

---

## Questions

### Section I: Grammar and Mechanics (Questions 1–15)

**1.** Choose the sentence that correctly uses the subjunctive mood.

A) If I was the president, I would change the policy.
B) If I were the president, I would change the policy.
C) If I am the president, I would change the policy.
D) If I be the president, I would change the policy.

**2.** Which sentence avoids a split infinitive?

A) She wanted to quickly finish the assignment.
B) She wanted to finish the assignment quickly.
C) She wanted quickly to finish the assignment.
D) Quickly she wanted to finish the assignment.

**3.** Choose the correct sentence.

A) Him and me went to the concert.
B) He and I went to the concert.
C) Me and him went to the concert.
D) Him and I went to the concert.

**4.** Identify the sentence with correct comma usage after an introductory element.

A) After the long exhausting meeting the employees left.
B) After the long exhausting meeting, the employees left.
C) After, the long exhausting meeting the employees left.
D) After the long, exhausting meeting the employees left.

**5.** Which word correctly completes the sentence? "______ going to regret this decision."

A) Your
B) You're
C) Youre
D) Yore

**6.** Which sentence uses the semicolon incorrectly?

A) She loves hiking; her brother prefers swimming.
B) We visited Paris, France; London, England; and Rome, Italy.
C) He studied hard; because he wanted to pass.
D) The project is due Friday; however, extensions may be granted.

**7.** Choose the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement.

A) The news are very disturbing today.
B) The news is very disturbing today.
C) The news were very disturbing today.
D) The news have been very disturbing today.

**8.** Which sentence has a vague pronoun reference?

A) When Sarah told Maria the news, Maria was shocked.
B) When Sarah told Maria the news, she was shocked.
C) Sarah told the news to Maria, who was shocked.
D) The news shocked Maria when Sarah told her.

**9.** Select the sentence with the correct use of "fewer" vs. "less."

A) There were less students in class today.
B) There were fewer students in class today.
C) There was fewer students in class today.
D) There was less students in class today.

**10.** Which sentence correctly uses an ellipsis?

A) According to the report, "the policy ... will take effect in January."
B) According to the report "the policy...will take effect in January."
C) According to the report, "the policy,... will take effect in January."
D) According to the report, the policy "will ... take" effect in January.

**11.** Choose the correct word: "The teacher ______ the students to study for the final exam."

A) adviced
B) adviced
C) advised
D) advized

**12.** Which sentence demonstrates correct use of the possessive with a compound noun?

A) My brother-in-laws car is in the driveway.
B) My brother-in-law's car is in the driveway.
C) My brother's-in-law car is in the driveway.
D) My brothers-in-law's car is in the driveway.

**13.** Identify the sentence with a faulty comparison.

A) The climate in Florida is warmer than that of Alaska.
B) The climate in Florida is warmer than Alaska.
C) Florida's climate is warmer than Alaska's climate.
D) Compared to Alaska, Florida has a warmer climate.

**14.** Which sentence uses "that/which" correctly in a restrictive clause?

A) The book, that I borrowed from the library, was excellent.
B) The book which I borrowed from the library was excellent.
C) The book that I borrowed from the library was excellent.
D) The book, which I borrowed from the library was excellent.

**15.** Choose the correct form: "The data clearly ______ that the hypothesis was wrong."

A) indicates
B) indicate
C) is indicating
D) has indicated

### Section II: Sentence Structure and Revision (Questions 16–30)

**16.** "We went to the beach. Despite the cold weather."

Which revision best corrects the error?

A) We went to the beach; despite the cold weather.
B) We went to the beach despite the cold weather.
C) We went to the beach, despite, the cold weather.
D) Despite. The cold weather we went to the beach.

**17.** "The volunteers cleaned the park. They planted new trees. They also painted the benches."

What is the most effective combination?

A) The volunteers cleaned the park, planted new trees, and painted the benches.
B) The volunteers cleaned the park and they planted new trees and they also painted the benches.
C) Cleaning the park, planting trees, and painting benches was what the volunteers did.
D) The volunteers, who cleaned the park and planted new trees, and also painting the benches.

**18.** Which of the following contains a sentence fragment?

A) She decided to leave early. Because of the storm.
B) She decided to leave early because of the storm.
C) Because of the storm, she decided to leave early.
D) The storm caused her to leave early.

**19.** "Video games can improve hand-eye coordination. Many parents believe video games are harmful. Some games teach problem-solving skills."

Which revision creates the most effective paragraph?

A) Although many parents believe video games are harmful, research suggests they can improve hand-eye coordination and teach problem-solving skills.
B) Video games can improve hand-eye coordination and many parents believe video games are harmful and some games teach problem-solving skills.
C) Many parents believe video games are harmful, however, hand-eye coordination and problem-solving skills can be improved.
D) Video games, which many parents believe are harmful, improve hand-eye coordination, and some games teach problem-solving skills, which is good.

**20.** Read the paragraph:

"(1) The Amazon rainforest produces about 20% of the world's oxygen. (2) Deforestation threatens this vital ecosystem. (3) The Amazon River is the largest river by discharge volume. (4) Conservation efforts are essential to preserve the rainforest's biodiversity."

Which sentence disrupts the paragraph's focus?

A) Sentence 1
B) Sentence 2
C) Sentence 3
D) Sentence 4

**21.** "Not only is exercise good for the body, ______ it also benefits the mind."

A) and
B) but
C) so
D) or

**22.** Which revision eliminates the passive voice?

Original: "The windows were broken by the hailstorm."

A) The hailstorm broke the windows.
B) The windows got broken by the hailstorm.
C) It was the hailstorm that the windows were broken by.
D) Broken by the hailstorm were the windows.

**23.** "After completing the internship, the company offered Sarah a full-time position."

What is the issue with this sentence, and what is the best revision?

A) No issue; the sentence is correct.
B) Dangling modifier; revision: After Sarah completed the internship, the company offered her a full-time position.
C) Comma splice; revision: After completing the internship; the company offered Sarah a position.
D) Run-on; revision: After completing the internship. The company offered Sarah a full-time position.

**24.** Identify the correctly punctuated sentence:

A) The recipe requires, sugar flour and butter.
B) The recipe requires sugar, flour, and butter.
C) The recipe requires sugar flour, and butter.
D) The recipe, requires sugar flour and butter.

**25.** Read the paragraph:

"(1) Learning a second language has cognitive benefits. (2) Bilingual individuals often outperform monolinguals on tasks requiring attention and multitasking. (3) Many schools have cut foreign language programs due to budget constraints. (4) Research also shows that bilingualism may delay the onset of dementia."

Sentence 3 would best function as:

A) The opening sentence of the paragraph
B) A counterpoint that should be introduced with a transition like "However"
C) A supporting detail for sentence 4
D) The conclusion of the paragraph

**26.** "The reason he was late was because of traffic."

What is the best revision?

A) He was late because of traffic.
B) The reason he was late was on account of the traffic situation.
C) The reason why he was late was because there was traffic.
D) Because of the fact that there was traffic, he was late for the reason of it.

**27.** Which transition word best fits? "The first study showed promising results. ______, the second study failed to replicate them."

A) Furthermore
B) In addition
C) In contrast
D) As a result

**28.** "Covered in mud from the game, the shower was exactly what the players needed."

This sentence has a problem with:

A) Subject-verb agreement
B) Dangling modifier
C) Pronoun reference
D) Parallel structure

**29.** Which sentence maintains the most objective academic tone?

A) Everyone knows that climate change is the biggest problem ever.
B) Climate change poses significant challenges to global food security and coastal communities.
C) Climate change is totally going to destroy everything if we don't act fast.
D) I think climate change is really bad and we should do something about it.

**30.** "She wanted to travel the world she also needed to finish school first."

Which revision is correct?

A) She wanted to travel the world, but she also needed to finish school first.
B) She wanted to travel the world she also needed, to finish school first.
C) She wanted to travel the world, she also needed to finish school first.
D) She wanted, to travel the world she also needed to finish school first.

### Section III: Rhetorical Analysis (Questions 31–42)

Read the following passage and answer questions 31–35.

> "Social media has fundamentally altered how we communicate, yet it has also created new challenges for mental health. A 2022 study published in JAMA Pediatrics found that adolescents who spend more than three hours daily on social media face double the risk of depression and anxiety symptoms. Defenders of social media platforms point to the connections they foster across geographic boundaries and the communities they build around shared interests. While these benefits are real, they do not negate the responsibility of tech companies to design platforms that prioritize user well-being over engagement metrics. Regulation, education, and design reform must work together to create a healthier digital landscape."

**31.** The primary purpose of this passage is to:

A) Celebrate the achievements of social media companies
B) Argue that social media's mental health risks require action from multiple stakeholders
C) Describe the history of social media
D) Compare different social media platforms

**32.** The author cites JAMA Pediatrics primarily to:

A) Show that all teenagers are depressed
B) Provide credible, peer-reviewed evidence for the claimed mental health risks
C) Criticize the journal's methodology
D) Demonstrate that only adolescents are affected

**33.** The phrase "engagement metrics" implies:

A) Social media platforms measure success by how much time users spend on them
B) Social media platforms want users to be happy
C) Engagement is the same as well-being
D) Tech companies do not track user behavior

**34.** The author acknowledges the "connections" and "communities" social media provides in order to:

A) Abandon the argument against social media
B) Demonstrate fairness by conceding legitimate benefits before reinforcing the main argument
C) Prove that social media is entirely positive
D) Introduce a completely new topic

**35.** The passage's conclusion calls for:

A) Banning social media entirely
B) A multi-pronged approach involving regulation, education, and design reform
C) Allowing tech companies to self-regulate
D) More studies before any action is taken

Read the following passage and answer questions 36–39.

> "When we pave over wetlands to build shopping centers, we are not merely changing the landscape — we are dismantling an ecosystem that filters our water, absorbs floodwaters, and provides habitat for hundreds of species. The economic value of wetland services has been estimated at over $47 trillion globally per year. Yet developers often frame the choice as 'economic progress versus environmental sentimentality.' This false dichotomy ignores the economic reality: destroying wetlands costs communities billions in flood damage, water treatment, and lost fisheries."

**36.** The author's tone can best be described as:

A) Indifferent and neutral
B) Urgent and evidence-based
C) Nostalgic and sentimental
D) Hostile and accusatory

**37.** The phrase "economic progress versus environmental sentimentality" is presented as:

A) The author's own belief
B) A false dichotomy used by developers that the author refutes
C) A valid argument the author supports
D) A quote from an environmental group

**38.** The $47 trillion figure is used to:

A) Show that wetlands are expensive to maintain
B) Quantify the economic value of wetlands to counter the notion that conservation is merely sentimental
C) Prove that developers are greedy
D) Suggest that wetlands should be sold

**39.** The author's primary rhetorical strategy is:

A) Emotional storytelling without evidence
B) Reframing the environmental argument in economic terms
C) Personal attacks on developers
D) Historical narrative

Read the following passage and answer questions 40–42.

> "The courtroom fell silent as the verdict was read. Outside, rain streaked the tall windows, blurring the world beyond into gray watercolor. The defendant stood motionless, hands clasped, eyes fixed on the judge's face. His attorney shuffled papers — a useless, mechanical gesture. In the gallery, the victim's mother gripped the arm of her chair until her knuckles whitened. Everyone in the room understood that whatever words came next would reshape at least two families' futures forever."

**40.** The dominant rhetorical mode of this passage is:

A) Argumentative essay
B) Expository report
C) Descriptive narrative with dramatic tension
D) Process analysis

**41.** The detail of rain "blurring the world beyond into gray watercolor" functions as:

A) Literal weather reporting
B) Pathetic fallacy — the weather mirrors the emotional tone of the scene
C) A metaphor for the defendant's guilt
D) A contrast to the mood inside the courtroom

**42.** The attorney's shuffling papers is described as "useless, mechanical" to suggest:

A) The attorney is incompetent
B) The gesture reveals helplessness and the inability to change what is about to happen
C) The attorney is well-prepared
D) The papers contain important evidence

### Section IV: Research Skills and Source Use (Questions 43–50)

**43.** Which of the following best describes a literature review?

A) A book report on a novel
B) A survey and synthesis of existing research on a topic
C) A personal opinion essay
D) A primary research study

**44.** Which source would be MOST appropriate for an academic research paper on vaccine efficacy?

A) A blog post by an anonymous writer
B) A peer-reviewed article in The Lancet
C) A social media thread with many likes
D) A Wikipedia article on vaccines

**45.** In Chicago style (notes-bibliography), sources are cited using:

A) Parenthetical author-page citations
B) Footnotes or endnotes with a bibliography
C) Numbered references in brackets
D) Author-date parenthetical citations

**46.** Which of the following is an example of proper paraphrasing?

A) Changing a few words in the original sentence and not citing the source
B) Restating the author's idea in entirely new words and sentence structure with a citation
C) Copying the original sentence and adding quotation marks but no citation
D) Summarizing the source without reading it fully

**47.** Signal phrases (e.g., "According to Smith...") serve what purpose in academic writing?

A) To fill space and increase word count
B) To smoothly integrate source material and attribute ideas to their authors
C) To avoid using citations
D) To express the writer's personal opinion

**48.** A DOI (Digital Object Identifier) is used in citations to:

A) Show the publication date
B) Provide a permanent, unique link to the digital version of a source
C) Indicate the author's credentials
D) Replace the need for a bibliography

**49.** Which of the following is an example of bias in a source?

A) A pharmaceutical company's study claiming its own drug is the most effective
B) A meta-analysis published in a peer-reviewed journal
C) A government report based on census data
D) A systematic review with transparent methodology

**50.** The difference between a summary and an analysis in academic writing is:

A) There is no difference
B) A summary restates the main points, while an analysis evaluates, interprets, and draws conclusions
C) A summary is longer than an analysis
D) An analysis only restates facts
