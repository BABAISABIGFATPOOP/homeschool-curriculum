# CLEP College Composition — Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 120 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 50
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** Which of the following sentences contains a comma splice?

A) The dog barked loudly, and the cat ran away.
B) The dog barked loudly, the cat ran away.
C) The dog barked loudly; the cat ran away.
D) Because the dog barked loudly, the cat ran away.

**2.** Select the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement.

A) The group of students are ready for the test.
B) The group of students is ready for the test.
C) The group of students were ready for the test.
D) The group of students have been ready for the test.

**3.** Which revision best corrects the dangling modifier? "Walking through the park, the flowers were beautiful."

A) Walking through the park, the flowers were beautiful to see.
B) Walking through the park, I thought the flowers were beautiful.
C) The flowers were beautiful, walking through the park.
D) Through the park walking, the flowers were beautiful.

**4.** What is the primary purpose of a thesis statement?

A) To summarize the entire essay
B) To present the main argument or claim of the essay
C) To introduce the topic with background information
D) To provide evidence for the first body paragraph

**5.** Which sentence uses the semicolon correctly?

A) I went to the store; and bought milk.
B) I went to the store; I bought milk.
C) I went; to the store to buy milk.
D) I went to the store, I; bought milk.

**6.** Identify the sentence that uses parallel structure correctly.

A) She likes hiking, swimming, and to ride bikes.
B) She likes hiking, swimming, and riding bikes.
C) She likes to hike, swimming, and bike rides.
D) She likes hiking, to swim, and riding bikes.

**7.** Which transitional word best indicates a contrast?

A) Furthermore
B) Similarly
C) Nevertheless
D) Additionally

**8.** In the sentence "The teacher who was strict but fair retired last year," what is the function of "who was strict but fair"?

A) Independent clause
B) Adverbial clause
C) Relative clause
D) Noun clause

**9.** Which of the following is a sentence fragment?

A) Running through the field at full speed.
B) She ran through the field at full speed.
C) The athlete ran through the field quickly.
D) Running quickly, she crossed the field.

**10.** What type of appeal does the following statement use? "Nine out of ten doctors recommend this product."

A) Ethos
B) Pathos
C) Logos
D) Kairos

**11.** Which sentence correctly uses an apostrophe?

A) The dog's were playing in the yard.
B) The dogs' owner left the gate open.
C) The dog's are friendly.
D) Its' leash was on the ground.

**12.** What is the most effective placement for a topic sentence in a paragraph?

A) At the end of the paragraph
B) In the middle of the paragraph
C) At the beginning of the paragraph
D) Topic sentences are unnecessary

**13.** Which of the following represents a logical fallacy known as "ad hominem"?

A) Everyone is buying this product, so it must be good.
B) You can't trust his argument because he's a terrible person.
C) If we allow students to use phones, soon they'll be using them during exams.
D) Either you support the policy or you don't care about children.

**14.** Select the correctly punctuated sentence.

A) "I'm leaving now" she said "don't wait for me."
B) "I'm leaving now," she said, "don't wait for me."
C) "I'm leaving now", she said, "don't wait for me".
D) "I'm leaving now," she said. "Don't wait for me."

**15.** In MLA format, where should the page number appear?

A) Bottom left corner
B) Bottom center
C) Top right corner
D) Top left corner

**16.** Which sentence demonstrates correct use of the colon?

A) The recipe calls for: eggs, milk, and flour.
B) She had one goal: to finish the race.
C) He went to: the store, the bank, and the gym.
D) The students: worked on their projects all day.

**17.** What is the purpose of a counterargument in a persuasive essay?

A) To weaken your own position
B) To acknowledge and refute opposing viewpoints
C) To change the topic of discussion
D) To conclude the essay

**18.** Which of the following is an example of passive voice?

A) The committee approved the budget.
B) The budget was approved by the committee.
C) The committee will approve the budget.
D) The committee has been approving budgets.

**19.** Identify the sentence with a misplaced modifier.

A) She almost drove the car for two hours.
B) She drove the car for almost two hours.
C) Almost, she drove the car for two hours.
D) For two hours, she almost drove the car.

**20.** Which of the following best describes "audience analysis" in writing?

A) Counting the number of readers
B) Considering the knowledge, attitudes, and expectations of readers
C) Choosing a topic that everyone will agree with
D) Writing in the simplest vocabulary possible

**21.** Select the sentence with correct pronoun-antecedent agreement.

A) Each student must bring their textbook.
B) Each student must bring his or her textbook.
C) Each student must bring our textbook.
D) Each students must bring their textbooks.

**22.** What is the function of a concluding paragraph?

A) To introduce new evidence
B) To restate the thesis and synthesize main points
C) To present the strongest argument
D) To ask a series of questions

**23.** Which rhetorical device is used in "government of the people, by the people, for the people"?

A) Metaphor
B) Anaphora
C) Hyperbole
D) Alliteration

**24.** Which of the following sentences is punctuated correctly?

A) The three largest cities are New York New York, Los Angeles California, and Chicago Illinois.
B) The three largest cities are New York, New York; Los Angeles, California; and Chicago, Illinois.
C) The three largest cities are: New York, New York, Los Angeles, California and Chicago, Illinois.
D) The three largest cities are New York New York; Los Angeles California; and Chicago Illinois.

**25.** What does it mean to "synthesize" sources in a research paper?

A) To quote each source separately
B) To combine ideas from multiple sources to develop a new understanding
C) To summarize each source in order
D) To choose the best source and ignore the rest

**26.** Which sentence contains a split infinitive?

A) She decided to quickly finish her homework.
B) She decided quickly to finish her homework.
C) She quickly decided to finish her homework.
D) She decided to finish her homework quickly.

**27.** What is the difference between "affect" and "effect" in standard usage?

A) They are interchangeable.
B) "Affect" is typically a verb; "effect" is typically a noun.
C) "Affect" is typically a noun; "effect" is typically a verb.
D) "Affect" is formal; "effect" is informal.

**28.** Which of the following is an example of a compound-complex sentence?

A) She ran quickly.
B) She ran quickly, and he followed slowly.
C) Although she ran quickly, he caught up, and they finished together.
D) Although she ran quickly, he caught up.

**29.** In an argumentative essay, what is "ethos"?

A) An appeal to the audience's emotions
B) An appeal to logic and reasoning
C) An appeal based on the credibility of the speaker or writer
D) An appeal based on timing

**30.** Which revision eliminates the wordiness? "Due to the fact that it was raining, the game was canceled."

A) Due to it raining, the game was canceled.
B) The fact that it was raining caused the game to be canceled.
C) Because it was raining, the game was canceled.
D) Owing to the fact that it was raining, the game was canceled.

**31.** What type of essay organization presents events in the order they occurred?

A) Compare and contrast
B) Cause and effect
C) Chronological
D) Spatial

**32.** Which sentence uses "who" correctly?

A) The man who I met yesterday was friendly.
B) The man who met me yesterday was friendly.
C) The man who him met yesterday was friendly.
D) The man who them met was friendly.

**33.** What is the purpose of an annotated bibliography?

A) To list sources in alphabetical order only
B) To provide a summary and evaluation of each source
C) To write footnotes for each source
D) To create endnotes for a paper

**34.** Which logical fallacy involves assuming that because two things happened sequentially, one caused the other?

A) Straw man
B) Post hoc ergo propter hoc
C) Red herring
D) Circular reasoning

**35.** Select the sentence with correct use of "its" vs. "it's."

A) Its a beautiful day outside.
B) The cat licked it's paw.
C) It's important to study for the exam.
D) The company changed it's policy.

**36.** Which of the following is the best example of a specific, arguable thesis statement?

A) Pollution is bad for the environment.
B) Many people have opinions about pollution.
C) This essay will discuss pollution.
D) Federal regulations on carbon emissions should be strengthened because current standards fail to address the primary sources of air pollution.

**37.** What is the correct way to introduce a long quotation (block quote) in MLA format?

A) Use quotation marks and indent the first line
B) Indent the entire quotation one inch from the left margin without quotation marks
C) Put the quotation in italics
D) Single-space the quotation

**38.** Which sentence demonstrates correct use of the subjunctive mood?

A) If I was you, I would study harder.
B) If I were you, I would study harder.
C) If I am you, I would study harder.
D) If I be you, I would study harder.

**39.** What is the primary difference between a summary and a paraphrase?

A) A summary is longer than a paraphrase.
B) A summary condenses the main ideas; a paraphrase restates a specific passage in your own words.
C) A paraphrase uses direct quotation marks.
D) There is no difference.

**40.** Which transition word signals addition?

A) However
B) Therefore
C) Moreover
D) Instead

**41.** Which of the following is an example of begging the question (circular reasoning)?

A) You should exercise because exercise is good for you because it improves health.
B) Everyone exercises, so you should too.
C) If you don't exercise, you'll become unhealthy and die young.
D) My doctor says exercise is important, so it must be.

**42.** Select the correct sentence.

A) Between you and I, the project is behind schedule.
B) Between you and me, the project is behind schedule.
C) Between you and myself, the project is behind schedule.
D) Between I and you, the project is behind schedule.

**43.** What is the rhetorical purpose of a rhetorical question?

A) To get a direct answer from the reader
B) To make a point or provoke thought without expecting an answer
C) To confuse the audience
D) To end an essay

**44.** Which revision corrects the faulty parallelism? "The company values integrity, innovation, and being efficient."

A) The company values integrity, innovation, and to be efficient.
B) The company values integrity, innovation, and efficiency.
C) The company values being integral, innovation, and being efficient.
D) The company values to have integrity, innovation, and being efficient.

**45.** In academic writing, what does "tone" refer to?

A) The volume of the writing
B) The writer's attitude toward the subject and audience
C) The length of sentences
D) The number of vocabulary words used

**46.** Which of the following correctly uses a dash?

A) She was determined—nothing could stop her—to finish the race.
B) She was determined nothing—could stop her to—finish the race.
C) She—was determined nothing could stop her to finish—the race.
D) She was—determined nothing could stop—her to finish the race.

**47.** What is the purpose of a "hook" in an introductory paragraph?

A) To state the thesis
B) To capture the reader's attention
C) To provide background information
D) To outline the essay's structure

**48.** Which sentence avoids gender bias?

A) A doctor should always listen to his patients.
B) A doctor should always listen to their patients.
C) A doctor should always listen to his or her patients.
D) Both B and C

**49.** What is the primary purpose of peer review in the writing process?

A) To grade the writing
B) To get feedback and suggestions for improvement
C) To check for plagiarism
D) To publish the writing

**50.** Which of the following best describes the purpose of a works cited page in MLA format?

A) To list every source you consulted during research
B) To list only the sources directly cited in your paper
C) To provide a summary of each source
D) To rank sources by importance
