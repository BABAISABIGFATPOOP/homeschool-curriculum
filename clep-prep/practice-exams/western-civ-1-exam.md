### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **5.** The Rosetta Stone, discovered in 1799, contained the same text in hieroglyphics, Demotic, and Greek, enabling Jean-Francois Champollion to decode hieroglyphics.
- **17.** Athenian democracy was direct (citizens voted on laws themselves) but excluded the majority of the population.
- **48.** "Crossing the Rubicon" became an idiom for passing a point of no return — Caesar's march on Rome triggered civil war.
- **71.** Justinian's Code organized centuries of Roman law and became the foundation of civil law in many countries.
- **86.** The Magna Carta (1215) was forced on King John by English barons — a foundational document for constitutional law.
- **95.** The Black Death killed roughly one-third of Europe's population (about 25 million people).
- **108.** Gutenberg's press revolutionized communication, making the Reformation and Scientific Revolution possible.
- **112.** Luther's "sola fide" and "sola scriptura" challenged the Catholic Church's authority and doctrines.
- **120.** The Peace of Westphalia (1648) is considered a turning point in establishing the modern state system.
