# CLEP Western Civilization II (1648 to Present) — Full Practice Exam

## Questions

### Absolutism and the Enlightenment (Questions 1–25)

**1.** Absolutism is a form of government in which:

A) Power is shared between the monarch and parliament
B) The monarch holds unlimited and unchecked power, often claiming divine right
C) Citizens elect their ruler
D) The church controls the state

**2.** Louis XIV of France exemplified absolutism with his statement:

A) "Let them eat cake"
B) "L'etat, c'est moi" ("I am the state")
C) "Liberte, egalite, fraternite"
D) "To be or not to be"

**3.** Louis XIV's Palace of Versailles served to:

A) House the poor
B) Centralize power by keeping nobles at court under the king's watchful eye and demonstrate royal grandeur
C) Serve as a military fortress
D) Function as a university

A) England and France
B) Royalists (Cavaliers) supporting King Charles I and Parliamentarians (Roundheads) under Oliver Cromwell

D) England and Spain

A) Ended monarchy in England permanently

C) The restoration of Catholic rule
D) The French conquest of England

A) Absolute monarchy

C) A republic
D) Religious uniformity

A) Isolate Russia from Europe

C) Destroy the Russian military
D) Convert Russia to Islam

A) Rejection of all knowledge

A) Disproved the heliocentric model

A) Blind faith and tradition

A) Land, money, and power

A) Kings rule by divine right

A) Individuals should submit to absolute rulers

A) Was a religious text
