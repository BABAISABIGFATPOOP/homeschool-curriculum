# CLEP Spanish Language — Full Practice Exam

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 80
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.
**Note:** The actual CLEP Spanish exam includes a listening section. This practice exam covers only the reading portion (reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary). Practice listening separately with Spanish audio resources.

### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **1.** The narrator has "dos hermanos y una hermana" = 3 siblings total.
- **31.** "Yo estudio" — first person singular present tense of "estudiar."
- **32.** "Fuimos" — first person plural preterite of "ir." "El verano pasado" indicates past tense.
- **35.** "Me gustaría que tú vinieras" — subjunctive is required after "gustaría que."
- **36.** "Iba" — imperfect tense for habitual past actions ("when I was a child, I always used to go").
- **37.** "Es importante que... hagan" — subjunctive required after impersonal expressions of necessity.
- **38.** "Si yo tuviera" — imperfect subjunctive in the "if" clause of a contrary-to-fact conditional.
- **41.** "Que sea interesante" — subjunctive after indefinite antecedent ("a book that IS interesting" — unknown book).
- **47.** "Había" — imperfect of "haber" used impersonally to mean "there was/were."
- **48.** "No creo que... diga" — subjunctive after expressions of doubt/disbelief.
- **49.** "Tan... como" = "as... as" for equal comparisons.
- **54.** "Mejor" is the irregular comparative of "bueno" (good → better).
- **55.** "Antes de que... llegaran" — subjunctive required after "antes de que."

### Grammar Concepts Tested

- **Present tense** (regular and irregular verbs)
- **Preterite vs. Imperfect** (completed vs. habitual/ongoing past actions)
- **Future tense**
- **Subjunctive mood** (after expressions of doubt, desire, emotion, impersonal expressions, indefinite antecedents, and certain conjunctions)
- **Conditional tense** and contrary-to-fact "si" clauses
- **Ser vs. Estar**
- **Reflexive verbs**
- **Comparatives and superlatives**
- **Prepositions**
- **Present perfect tense**
- **Passive voice**
