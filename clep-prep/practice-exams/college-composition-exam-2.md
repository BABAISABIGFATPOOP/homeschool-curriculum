# College Composition CLEP Practice Exam 2

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 50 (multiple choice section)
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.

> **Important Note:** The actual CLEP College Composition exam also includes TWO essay sections (one requiring a position/argument and one requiring a revision of a given passage). This practice exam covers only the multiple-choice portion. Be sure to practice timed essay writing as well.

---

## Questions

### Section I: Grammar and Mechanics (Questions 1–15)

**1.** Choose the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement.

A) Neither the students nor the teacher were prepared for the fire drill.
B) Neither the students nor the teacher was prepared for the fire drill.
C) Neither the students or the teacher were prepared for the fire drill.
D) Neither the students nor the teacher have been prepared for the fire drill.

**2.** Which sentence contains a fused (run-on) sentence?

A) The sun set behind the mountains, and the sky turned orange.
B) The sun set behind the mountains the sky turned orange.
C) After the sun set, the sky turned orange.
D) The sun set; the sky turned orange.

**3.** Choose the correct sentence.

A) The data shows that the treatment is effective.
B) The data show that the treatment is effective.
C) The datas show that the treatment is effective.
D) The data showing that the treatment is effective.

**4.** Identify the sentence that correctly uses a colon.

A) The recipe calls for: eggs, flour, and sugar.
B) The recipe calls for three ingredients: eggs, flour, and sugar.
C) The recipe: calls for eggs, flour, and sugar.
D) The recipe calls for three: ingredients eggs, flour, and sugar.

**5.** Which word correctly completes the sentence? "The team celebrated ______ victory with a parade."

A) it's
B) its
C) their
D) they're

**6.** Which sentence correctly uses a dash?

A) The report—which was submitted late—contained several errors.
B) The report, which was submitted late, contained—several errors.
C) The—report which was submitted late contained several errors.
D) The report which was submitted—late contained several errors.

**7.** Choose the sentence that avoids a double negative.

A) She couldn't hardly believe the news.
B) She could hardly believe the news.
C) She couldn't believe nothing about the news.
D) She didn't have no reason to doubt the news.

**8.** Which sentence uses the correct form of the comparative adjective?

A) This is the most easiest problem on the test.
B) This problem is more easier than the last one.
C) This problem is easier than the last one.
D) This problem is more easy than the last one.

**9.** Select the sentence with correct pronoun-antecedent agreement.

A) Every student must bring their own laptop.
B) Every student must bring his or her own laptop.
C) Every student must bring our own laptop.
D) Every students must bring their own laptops.

**10.** Which sentence correctly uses a comma with a coordinating conjunction?

A) She studied all night, and passed the exam.
B) She studied all night and, passed the exam.
C) She studied all night, and she passed the exam.
D) She studied all night and she, passed the exam.

**11.** Choose the correct word: "The loud noise had a bad ______ on the baby."

A) affect
B) effect
C) affectation
D) effective

**12.** Which sentence is punctuated correctly?

A) My three favorite cities are Paris, France, London, England, and Rome, Italy.
B) My three favorite cities are Paris, France; London, England; and Rome, Italy.
C) My three favorite cities are: Paris France, London England, and Rome Italy.
D) My three favorite cities are, Paris, France, London, England, and Rome, Italy.

**13.** Identify the sentence with correct parallel structure.

A) The coach told the players to stretch, practice drills, and they should run laps.
B) The coach told the players to stretch, to practice drills, and to run laps.
C) The coach told the players stretching, practicing drills, and to run laps.
D) The coach told the players to stretch, practice drills, and running laps.

**14.** Which sentence uses "lay/lie" correctly?

A) I need to lay down for a nap.
B) Please lie the book on the table.
C) The cat was lying in the sun.
D) She has been laying in bed all day.

**15.** Choose the correct form: "Neither the manager ______ the employees knew about the change."

A) or
B) nor
C) and
D) but

### Section II: Sentence Structure and Revision (Questions 16–30)

**16.** "Although she was tired. She continued to work on the project."

Which revision best corrects the error?

A) Although she was tired, she continued to work on the project.
B) Although she was tired; she continued to work on the project.
C) Although, she was tired she continued to work on the project.
D) Although she was tired: she continued to work on the project.

**17.** "The city invested in public transportation. The city built new bike lanes. The city also added more parks."

What is the most effective way to combine these sentences?

A) The city invested in public transportation, built new bike lanes, and added more parks.
B) The city invested in public transportation and the city built new bike lanes and the city also added more parks.
C) The city invested in public transportation, and they built new bike lanes, and also adding more parks.
D) The city, which invested in public transportation, built new bike lanes, and also they added more parks.

**18.** Which of the following is a comma splice?

A) The train was late; we missed our connection.
B) The train was late, we missed our connection.
C) Because the train was late, we missed our connection.
D) The train was late, so we missed our connection.

**19.** "The professor explained the theory clearly. Students found it easy to understand. They performed well on the exam."

Which revision is most effective?

A) Because the professor explained the theory clearly, students found it easy to understand and performed well on the exam.
B) The professor explained the theory clearly and students found it easy to understand and they performed well on the exam.
C) The professor who explained the theory clearly and students who found it easy to understand performed well on the exam.
D) Students performed well on the exam, and the professor explained the theory clearly, and they found it easy to understand.

**20.** Read the paragraph:

"(1) Regular exercise has many health benefits. (2) It reduces the risk of heart disease. (3) My gym membership costs $40 a month. (4) It also improves mental health and reduces stress."

Which sentence should be removed to improve coherence?

A) Sentence 1
B) Sentence 2
C) Sentence 3
D) Sentence 4

**21.** "The more you practice, the ______ you will become."

A) good
B) better
C) best
D) most good

**22.** Which revision eliminates redundancy most effectively?

Original: "The basic fundamentals of the program are essential and necessary for success."

A) The fundamentals of the program are essential for success.
B) The basic fundamentals of the program are essential and necessary.
C) The basic, essential, and necessary fundamentals are required for success.
D) The basic fundamentals are essentially and necessarily fundamental for success.

**23.** "After reviewing the data, a new hypothesis was proposed by the scientist."

Which revision improves this sentence?

A) After reviewing the data, the scientist proposed a new hypothesis.
B) A new hypothesis, after reviewing the data, was proposed by the scientist.
C) The data was reviewed, and a new hypothesis was proposed by the scientist.
D) After reviewing the data, it was proposed a new hypothesis by the scientist.

**24.** Identify the sentence that is NOT a run-on or fragment:

A) Going to the store for groceries and other supplies.
B) The rain stopped we went outside to play.
C) Even though the storm was severe, no one was injured.
D) Finished the project early submitted it on Monday.

**25.** Read the paragraph:

"(1) Renewable energy sources are critical for the future. (2) Wind farms are being built at an increasing rate. (3) Solar panels have become more affordable for homeowners. (4) Coal remains a significant source of energy in many countries. (5) Government incentives have accelerated the adoption of green technologies."

For the best logical flow, sentence 4 should be placed:

A) Before sentence 1
B) After sentence 1
C) After sentence 5
D) It should be removed as it disrupts the paragraph's focus on renewables

**26.** "The report that was written by the intern and that was reviewed by the manager needs revision."

What is the best way to make this sentence more concise?

A) The report written by the intern and reviewed by the manager needs revision.
B) The intern's report, which the manager reviewed, it needs revision.
C) The report that was written and that was reviewed needs revision.
D) The intern wrote the report and the manager reviewed it and it needs revision.

**27.** Which transition word best fits? "The experiment yielded unexpected results. ______, the researchers decided to replicate it."

A) However
B) Therefore
C) Meanwhile
D) Similarly

**28.** "Exhausted from the long hike, the couch looked inviting to the hikers."

This sentence has a problem with:

A) Subject-verb agreement
B) Dangling modifier
C) Pronoun reference
D) Comma splice

**29.** Which sentence uses the most precise language?

A) The thing about the study was that it was good.
B) The study produced significant and replicable results.
C) The study was nice and had some interesting stuff in it.
D) The study was basically pretty decent overall.

**30.** "The CEO announced layoffs, the employees were upset."

Which revision is correct?

A) The CEO announced layoffs; the employees were upset.
B) The CEO announced layoffs the employees were upset.
C) The CEO announced, layoffs the employees were upset.
D) The CEO; announced layoffs, the employees were upset.

### Section III: Rhetorical Analysis (Questions 31–42)

Read the following passage and answer questions 31–35.

> "In an era when screen time dominates children's daily routines, the value of outdoor play has never been more apparent. Research from the American Academy of Pediatrics shows that children who spend at least one hour outdoors daily exhibit better focus, improved social skills, and lower rates of anxiety. Yet many schools have reduced recess to make room for standardized test preparation. Critics of extended recess argue that academic minutes are too precious to waste. However, the evidence overwhelmingly suggests that physical activity and unstructured play actually enhance academic performance, not hinder it. Schools that prioritize recess are investing in the whole child."

**31.** The primary purpose of this passage is to:

A) Describe how children spend their time
B) Advocate for maintaining or increasing outdoor play and recess in schools
C) Criticize standardized testing
D) Compare outdoor and indoor activities

**32.** The reference to the "American Academy of Pediatrics" serves primarily to:

A) Show the author is a doctor
B) Provide authoritative, research-based support for the argument
C) Appeal to the reader's emotions
D) Introduce a counterargument

**33.** The phrase "investing in the whole child" suggests:

A) Schools should spend more money on playgrounds
B) Education should address physical, social, and emotional development alongside academics
C) Children are financial assets
D) Recess is more important than academics

**34.** The author addresses the counterargument about "academic minutes" in order to:

A) Concede that the critics are correct
B) Acknowledge the opposing view before presenting evidence that refutes it
C) Change the topic
D) Show that testing should be eliminated

**35.** The passage's overall structure is best described as:

A) Narrative chronology
B) Compare and contrast
C) Claim supported by evidence with counterargument and rebuttal
D) Classification and division

Read the following passage and answer questions 36–39.

> "The minimum wage debate has intensified in recent years. Proponents argue that raising the minimum wage would lift millions out of poverty and stimulate the economy through increased consumer spending. Opponents counter that higher wages would force small businesses to cut jobs or raise prices, potentially hurting the very workers the policy aims to help. A 2021 Congressional Budget Office report estimated that a $15 federal minimum wage would raise wages for 17 million workers while potentially eliminating 1.4 million jobs. The trade-offs are real, and responsible policymaking requires weighing both the benefits and costs."

**36.** The author's tone can best be described as:

A) Strongly in favor of raising the minimum wage
B) Balanced and analytical
C) Dismissive of economic research
D) Emotionally charged and biased

**37.** The CBO report is cited to:

A) Prove that raising the minimum wage is a bad idea
B) Show that the issue involves both benefits and trade-offs
C) Support only the proponents' argument
D) Undermine the opponents' argument

**38.** The phrase "the very workers the policy aims to help" highlights:

A) A logical fallacy
B) The irony that the policy could harm its intended beneficiaries
C) That the workers are unimportant
D) A historical fact

**39.** The author's rhetorical approach is best described as:

A) One-sided persuasion
B) Presenting multiple perspectives to encourage informed judgment
C) Personal anecdote
D) Satirical commentary

Read the following passage and answer questions 40–42.

> "The old lighthouse stood at the edge of the world — or so it seemed to me as a child. Its beam swept the dark waters like a tireless sentinel, warning ships of the rocks below. My father would take me there on Saturday evenings, and we would sit on the cold concrete steps, listening to the waves and saying very little. Those evenings taught me that silence can be its own form of conversation, and that some of the strongest bonds are forged not in words but in shared presence."

**40.** The dominant rhetorical mode of this passage is:

A) Persuasive argument
B) Reflective personal narrative
C) Expository analysis
D) Process analysis

**41.** The simile "like a tireless sentinel" compares the lighthouse beam to:

A) A soldier or guard keeping watch
B) A searchlight
C) A clock
D) A weapon

**42.** The final sentence conveys the theme that:

A) Lighthouses are important for navigation
B) Meaningful relationships do not always require words
C) Fathers should spend more time with their children
D) Silence is always awkward

### Section IV: Research Skills and Source Use (Questions 43–50)

**43.** Which of the following is considered a tertiary source?

A) A journal article reporting original research
B) A diary from the Civil War era
C) An encyclopedia entry summarizing a topic
D) A biography analyzing a historical figure's life

**44.** When evaluating a source's credibility, which factor is LEAST important?

A) The author's qualifications
B) The length of the article
C) Whether the source cites its own references
D) The publication's reputation

**45.** Which citation format is most commonly used in social sciences?

A) MLA
B) APA
C) Chicago
D) IEEE

**46.** In APA format, an in-text citation for a book by Jones published in 2020 would appear as:

A) (Jones 42)
B) (Jones, 2020)
C) (Jones, p. 42, 2020)
D) [Jones 2020]

**47.** Which of the following actions would constitute self-plagiarism?

A) Citing your own previous work
B) Submitting the same paper to two different classes without permission
C) Paraphrasing another author with a citation

**48.** A peer-reviewed journal article is considered reliable primarily because:

A) It is published online
B) It has been evaluated by experts in the field before publication
C) It is written by a professor
D) It contains a bibliography

**49.** The purpose of a works cited or references page is to:

A) Impress the reader with the number of sources used
B) Provide complete information for readers to locate each source
