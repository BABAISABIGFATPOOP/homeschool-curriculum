# CLEP Analyzing and Interpreting Literature — Full Practice Exam

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 80
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.
**Note:** This exam tests your ability to analyze poetry, prose fiction, and drama. No specific works are required reading — the test is based on close reading of provided passages.

### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **1.** The rhyme scheme is AABA: know/though/here/snow (know, though, snow rhyme; here does not).
- **6.** The poem uses iambic tetrameter (four iambic feet per line): "Whose WOODS these ARE I THINK I KNOW."
- **15.** The School = childhood, Gazing Grain = the fullness of life/adulthood, Setting Sun = the end of life.
- **19.** Three quatrains and a couplet (ABAB CDCD EFEF GG) = Shakespearean sonnet.
- **22.** In a Shakespearean sonnet, the volta typically comes at line 9 ("But thy eternal summer shall not fade"), where the argument shifts.
- **37.** "The flag of permanent defeat" compares the sail directly to a flag (no "like" or "as"), making it a metaphor.
- **54.** Free indirect discourse blends the character's voice with third-person narration, allowing us to hear Miss Brill's thoughts without direct "she thought" tags.
- **64.** Romeo is speaking alone on stage, unaware that Juliet is above — this is a soliloquy.
- **74.** Allegory: an extended metaphor where the entire narrative has a secondary symbolic meaning (e.g., Pilgrim's Progress, Animal Farm).
