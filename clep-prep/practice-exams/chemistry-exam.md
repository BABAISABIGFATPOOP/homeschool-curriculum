### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **4.** Oxygen has 8 electrons: 1s² 2s² 2p⁴.
- **18.** CO₂ is linear and symmetric, so the dipoles cancel, making it nonpolar.
- **21.** Ion-dipole forces (in solutions with ions) are strongest; among purely molecular IMFs, hydrogen bonding is strongest.
- **30.** 36 g / 18 g/mol = 2 mol.
- **32.** 4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃. Check: 4 Fe on each side, 6 O on each side.
- **33.** 4 mol H₂ needs 2 mol O₂ (2:1 ratio), but only 1 mol O₂ is available. O₂ is limiting.
- **46.** Boyle's Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂ → (2)(4) = (4)(V₂) → V₂ = 2 L.
- **56.** 2 mol / 0.5 L = 4 M.
- **71.** ΔG = ΔH − TΔS. Reaction is spontaneous when ΔG < 0.
- **75.** Reactant side: 4 moles of gas; product side: 2 moles. Increasing pressure shifts toward fewer moles (right).
