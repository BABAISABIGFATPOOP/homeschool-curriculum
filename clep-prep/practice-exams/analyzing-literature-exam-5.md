# CLEP Analyzing and Interpreting Literature — Practice Exam 5

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 80
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** Which of the following best defines "imagery" in literature?

A) The use of figurative language only
B) Vivid descriptive language that appeals to the senses
C) The arrangement of events in a story
D) The author's biography

**2.** What is the rhyme scheme of a Petrarchan sonnet's octave?

A) ABAB CDCD
B) ABBAABBA
C) AABB CCDD
D) ABCABC

**3.** A story told entirely through diary entries and letters is an example of:

A) Picaresque novel
B) Epistolary novel
C) Bildungsroman
D) Roman a clef

**4.** What literary term describes the central struggle in a narrative?

A) Theme
B) Plot
C) Conflict
D) Resolution

**5.** In the line "The world is too much with us; late and soon," the speaker is expressing:

A) Joy about modern progress
B) Discontent with materialism and disconnection from nature
C) Excitement about travel
D) Nostalgia for childhood

**6.** Which of the following is an example of "internal conflict"?

A) A character fighting against a villain
B) A character struggling with a moral dilemma
C) A character battling a natural disaster
D) A character rebelling against society

**7.** The term "connotation" refers to:

A) The dictionary definition of a word
B) The emotional or cultural associations of a word
C) The spelling of a word
D) The grammatical function of a word

**8.** What is "dramatic monologue" in poetry?

A) A poem in which the speaker is the poet
B) A poem in which a single character speaks to a silent listener, revealing their personality
C) A poem recited by multiple speakers
D) A poem written entirely in dialogue

**9.** Which of the following authors is associated with the Harlem Renaissance?

A) Mark Twain
B) Langston Hughes
C) Ernest Hemingway
D) Edgar Allan Poe

**10.** What is the effect of using short, clipped sentences in prose?

A) It slows down the pacing
B) It creates a sense of urgency or tension
C) It indicates the narrator is unreliable
D) It shows the setting is peaceful

**11.** A "vignette" is best described as:

A) A full-length novel
B) A brief, evocative description or episode
C) A type of rhyme scheme
D) A dramatic monologue

**12.** What is "dramatic irony" in the context of a play?

A) When the dialogue is humorous
B) When the audience knows something the characters on stage do not
C) When a character speaks in soliloquy
D) When the play has an unexpected ending

**13.** The literary term "diction" refers to:

A) The author's choice of words
B) The rhythm of a poem
C) The plot structure
D) The setting of the story

**14.** Which of the following is a characteristic of a "tall tale"?

A) Realistic characters and settings
B) Exaggerated feats and humorous, larger-than-life events
C) A moral or spiritual lesson
D) Complex psychological characters

**15.** In narrative structure, what is "falling action"?

A) The events leading up to the climax
B) The events that occur after the climax and lead to the resolution
C) The introduction of the setting
D) The moment of highest tension

**16.** What does the term "pastoral" refer to in literature?

A) Stories set in cities
B) Works that idealize rural life and the countryside
C) Dark, gothic fiction
D) War poetry

**17.** "O Captain! My Captain!" by Walt Whitman is an example of:

A) An allegory
B) An apostrophe
C) A limerick
D) A haiku

**18.** What is the function of a "narrator" in fiction?

A) To serve as the antagonist
B) To tell the story and guide the reader through events
C) To provide the moral of the story
D) To represent the author's exact views

**19.** Which literary movement rejected conventional narrative and used fragmented structure?

A) Realism
B) Romanticism
C) Modernism
D) Naturalism

**20.** What is a "paradox"?

A) A statement that seems contradictory but reveals a deeper truth
B) An extreme exaggeration
C) A direct comparison between two things
D) A reference to another work

**21.** In "The Great Gatsby," the green light at the end of Daisy's dock symbolizes:

A) Envy and jealousy
B) Gatsby's hopes and the unattainable American Dream
C) The color of money
D) A traffic signal

**22.** What is "syntax" in the study of literature?

A) The meaning of individual words
B) The arrangement of words and phrases to create sentences
C) The emotional impact of a scene
D) The use of metaphor

**23.** A "tragic flaw" is also known as:

A) Catharsis
B) Hamartia
C) Hubris
D) Nemesis

**24.** Which of the following is an example of a "flat character"?

A) Elizabeth Bennet in "Pride and Prejudice"
B) A stereotypical villain who is evil for no reason
C) Hamlet in Shakespeare's "Hamlet"
D) Jay Gatsby in "The Great Gatsby"

**25.** What is the purpose of "dialogue" in fiction?

A) To replace the need for a narrator
B) To reveal character, advance the plot, and provide information
C) To fill space between action scenes
D) To always provide comic relief

**26.** The term "elegy" refers to:

A) A humorous poem about everyday life
B) A poem of mourning, typically lamenting the dead
C) A poem celebrating nature
D) A love poem

**27.** What is a "red herring" in a mystery or narrative?

A) A clue that leads to the correct answer
B) A misleading clue intended to distract from the truth
C) The climax of the story
D) The protagonist's ally

**28.** Which author is most associated with the stream-of-consciousness technique?

A) Charles Dickens
B) James Joyce
C) Jane Austen
D) Mark Twain

**29.** What is "blank verse"?

A) Poetry with no words
B) Unrhymed iambic pentameter
C) Free verse with irregular meter
D) Poetry that uses only visual imagery

A) The resolution of the conflict
B) The event that sets the main conflict in motion
C) The climax of the story
D) The final scene

**31.** What does the term "ambiguity" mean in literature?

A) A clear, definitive meaning
B) The presence of multiple possible interpretations
C) A logical fallacy
D) A type of rhyme

**32.** Which of the following is an example of "verbal irony"?

A) A character says "Great weather!" during a hurricane.
B) A fire station burns down.
C) The audience knows the character is in danger.
D) A character finds treasure in a place they always avoided.

A) Joy and celebration
B) Loss and mourning
C) Adventure and exploration
D) Nature and beauty

**34.** What is a "couplet"?

A) A poem with two stanzas
B) Two consecutive rhyming lines of verse
C) A poem with two speakers
D) A story with two protagonists

**35.** The term "third-person omniscient" means the narrator:

A) Is a character in the story
B) Knows the thoughts and feelings of all characters
C) Only knows one character's thoughts
D) Addresses the reader as "you"

**36.** What is the literary significance of the "green-eyed monster" in Shakespeare's "Othello"?

A) A literal monster
B) A metaphor for jealousy
C) A symbol of wealth
D) A representation of nature

**37.** Which of the following is a characteristic of Postmodern literature?

A) Strict adherence to traditional narrative structure
B) Metafiction, unreliable narrators, and blurred boundaries between fiction and reality
C) Focus on idealized rural settings
D) Exclusively tragic endings

**38.** What is "assonance"?

A) The repetition of consonant sounds
B) The repetition of vowel sounds within words
C) The repetition of entire words
D) A type of end rhyme

**39.** In poetry, what is a "volta"?

A) The first line of a poem
B) A turn or shift in thought, especially in a sonnet
C) The final stanza
D) A type of meter

**40.** What is the purpose of an "epilogue"?

A) To introduce the main characters
B) To provide closure or additional information after the main story ends
C) To present the inciting incident
D) To establish the setting

**41.** Which of the following best describes "naturalism" in literature?

A) Writing that celebrates the beauty of nature
B) A literary movement depicting characters as shaped by heredity and environment, often with a deterministic view
C) A style focused on supernatural events
D) Poetry about animals and plants

**42.** What is "foreshadowing"?

A) A flashback to an earlier event
B) Hints or clues that suggest future events in a narrative
C) The climax of the story
D) A character's internal monologue

A) Metaphor
B) Epistrophe
C) Anaphora
D) Chiasmus

**44.** What is a "picaresque novel"?

A) A novel about a rogue or low-born adventurer who survives by their wits
B) A novel set in a single room
C) A novel that takes place in one day
D) A novel written in verse

**45.** Which literary term refers to the time and place in which a story occurs?

A) Plot
B) Setting
C) Theme
D) Tone

**46.** What is "consonance"?

A) The repetition of vowel sounds
B) The repetition of consonant sounds, especially at the ends of words
C) A type of end rhyme
D) The use of alliteration

**47.** In Shakespeare's plays, what is the purpose of a "prologue"?

A) To resolve the conflict
B) To introduce the play's themes and set expectations for the audience
C) To provide comic relief
D) To present the climax

**48.** What is the significance of the "stream of consciousness" technique?

A) It provides a clear, linear narrative
B) It mimics the natural flow of a character's thoughts, often in an associative manner
C) It is used only in poetry
D) It always involves multiple narrators

**49.** Which term describes a reference to a well-known work of art, literature, person, or event?

A) Allegory
B) Allusion
C) Analogy
D) Anecdote

**50.** What is the function of "repetition" in poetry?

A) To show the poet is out of ideas
B) To create emphasis, rhythm, or reinforce meaning
C) To make the poem longer
D) To confuse the reader

A) A character's physical journey
B) A sudden moment of insight or revelation experienced by a character

A) The back wall of the stage
B) The imaginary barrier between the performers and the audience
C) A wall mentioned in the script

A) An unintentional shift from the serious or sublime to the trivial or ridiculous
B) An extended metaphor

D) The resolution of a comedy

A) A character who undergoes a major transformation
B) A character who remains essentially unchanged throughout the story
C) A character who appears only in dreams
