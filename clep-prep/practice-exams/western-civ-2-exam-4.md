# CLEP Western Civilization II — Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.
**Covers:** 1648 to the present

---

## Questions

**1.** The Enlightenment was a philosophical movement that emphasized:

A) Blind obedience to authority
B) Reason, individualism, and skepticism of traditional institutions
C) A return to medieval values
D) Religious orthodoxy

**2.** John Locke's philosophy influenced modern democracy through his ideas about:

A) The divine right of kings
B) Natural rights (life, liberty, and property) and government by consent
C) Absolute monarchy
D) Theocratic government

**3.** The French Revolution began in 1789 with:

A) Napoleon's rise to power
B) The storming of the Bastille
C) The execution of Louis XVI
D) The Reign of Terror

**4.** Napoleon Bonaparte's impact on Europe included:

A) Restoring feudalism
B) Spreading the ideals of the French Revolution, reforming legal codes, and reshaping European borders
C) Creating a permanent European peace
D) Establishing democracy in France

**5.** The Congress of Vienna (1814-1815) aimed to:

A) Spread revolutionary ideas
B) Restore the balance of power in Europe after Napoleon's defeat
C) Establish the United Nations
D) Divide Africa among European powers

**6.** The Industrial Revolution began in:

A) France
B) Germany
C) Great Britain
D) The United States

**7.** The primary energy source that powered the early Industrial Revolution was:

A) Electricity
B) Oil
C) Steam (coal)
D) Nuclear power

**8.** Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels wrote "The Communist Manifesto" (1848), which argued that:

A) Capitalism was the ideal economic system
B) History is driven by class struggle, and workers should overthrow the bourgeoisie
C) Religion should guide economic policy
D) Monarchy is the best form of government

**9.** The unification of Italy (Risorgimento) was largely achieved by:

A) Napoleon
B) Giuseppe Garibaldi, Count Cavour, and King Victor Emmanuel II
C) The Pope
D) Austria

**10.** The unification of Germany in 1871 was primarily orchestrated by:

A) Kaiser Wilhelm I alone
B) Otto von Bismarck through a policy of "blood and iron"
C) The German people through revolution
D) The Austrian Empire

**11.** Imperialism in the 19th century was driven by:

A) A desire to help colonized peoples
B) Economic interests, nationalism, and a belief in European superiority
C) Scientific curiosity alone
D) Religious conversion only

**12.** The Berlin Conference (1884-1885) established rules for:

A) European trade
B) The partition of Africa among European powers
C) German unification
D) The end of slavery

**13.** World War I began in 1914 due to:

A) A single cause
B) A combination of militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism, triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand
C) A trade dispute
D) A religious conflict

**14.** Trench warfare on the Western Front was characterized by:

A) Rapid movement and quick victories
B) Stalemate, massive casualties, and brutal conditions
C) Naval battles
D) Air warfare only

**15.** The Treaty of Versailles (1919) imposed on Germany:

A) Generous peace terms
B) Harsh reparations, territorial losses, and military restrictions
C) No significant penalties
D) A colonial empire

**16.** The Russian Revolution of 1917 resulted in:

A) A constitutional monarchy
B) The establishment of a communist government under the Bolsheviks
C) A democratic republic
D) The restoration of the Romanov dynasty

**17.** The League of Nations failed primarily because:

A) It had too many members
B) Major powers (especially the U.S.) did not join, and it lacked enforcement power
C) It was too aggressive
D) It focused only on trade

**18.** Fascism, as practiced by Mussolini in Italy, was characterized by:

A) Democratic principles
B) Extreme nationalism, authoritarian rule, and suppression of opposition
C) Communist economics
D) Religious government

**19.** The Weimar Republic in Germany was weakened by:

A) Strong democratic traditions
B) Economic crises, political instability, and the humiliation of the Treaty of Versailles
C) Too much military power
D) Allied support

**20.** Adolf Hitler's rise to power was aided by:

A) A strong German economy
B) Economic desperation, nationalist sentiment, and propaganda
C) Democratic elections alone
D) Support from the League of Nations

**21.** The Holocaust was:

A) A military campaign
B) The systematic genocide of six million Jews and millions of others by Nazi Germany
C) An economic policy
D) A diplomatic failure

**22.** The Atlantic Charter (1941) outlined:

A) Germany's war aims
B) Allied goals for a post-war world, including self-determination and free trade
C) Japan's surrender terms
D) Soviet territorial demands

**23.** The Cold War was primarily a conflict between:

A) The U.S. and China
B) The United States and the Soviet Union, based on ideological differences
C) Western Europe and Eastern Europe
D) NATO and the Warsaw Pact only

**24.** The Marshall Plan (1948) aimed to:

A) Militarize Western Europe
B) Rebuild Western European economies to prevent the spread of communism
C) Punish Germany
D) Create a European army

**25.** NATO was established in 1949 as:

A) An economic organization
B) A collective defense alliance against Soviet aggression
C) A cultural exchange program
D) A colonial administration

**26.** The Warsaw Pact (1955) was:

A) A Western alliance
B) A military alliance of communist Eastern European states led by the Soviet Union
C) A trade agreement
D) A peace treaty

**27.** Decolonization after World War II was driven by:

A) European willingness to give up colonies
B) Nationalist movements, weakened European powers, and changing international norms
C) Soviet conquest of colonies
D) American imperialism

**28.** The Indian independence movement led by Gandhi used:

A) Military force
B) Nonviolent civil disobedience
C) Terrorism
D) Diplomatic pressure only

**29.** The creation of Israel in 1948 led to:

A) Peace in the Middle East
B) Ongoing Arab-Israeli conflicts
C) The end of British colonialism
D) A unified Palestine

**30.** The Berlin Wall was built in 1961 to:

A) Protect West Berlin
B) Prevent East Germans from fleeing to West Berlin
C) Celebrate German unity
D) Serve as a trade barrier

**31.** The Cuban Revolution (1959) brought to power:

A) A democratic government
B) Fidel Castro and a communist regime
C) A military junta
D) An American-backed government

**32.** The European Economic Community (EEC), forerunner to the EU, was established to:

A) Create a military alliance
B) Promote economic integration and prevent future wars among European nations
C) Establish a single European currency immediately
D) Replace NATO

**33.** The Prague Spring (1968) was:

A) A successful democratic revolution
B) A period of political liberalization in Czechoslovakia crushed by Soviet intervention
C) A cultural festival
D) A military exercise

**34.** Detente in the 1970s referred to:

A) Increased Cold War tensions
B) A period of easing tensions between the U.S. and the Soviet Union
C) A military buildup
D) The end of the Cold War

**35.** The Solidarity movement in Poland (1980s) was:

A) A communist organization
B) An independent trade union that challenged communist rule
C) A military alliance
D) A Soviet-backed movement

**36.** Mikhail Gorbachev's policies of glasnost and perestroika were:

A) Military strategies
B) Reforms promoting openness and economic restructuring in the Soviet Union
C) Plans for world conquest
D) Cultural exchanges

**37.** The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 symbolized:

A) The beginning of the Cold War
B) The end of communist control in Eastern Europe
C) German division
D) Soviet expansion

**38.** The dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991 resulted in:

A) A stronger communist state
B) The independence of former Soviet republics and the end of the Cold War
C) A military conflict
D) The reunification of the Soviet Union under a new name

**39.** The Maastricht Treaty (1992) established:

A) NATO
B) The European Union
C) The United Nations
D) The Warsaw Pact

**40.** The Rwandan genocide (1994) involved:

A) A border dispute
B) The mass killing of Tutsis by Hutu extremists
C) A colonial war
D) An international conflict

**41.** The Bosnian War (1992-1995) was part of:

A) The Cold War
B) The breakup of Yugoslavia and included ethnic cleansing
C) A trade dispute
D) NATO expansion

**42.** The Scientific Revolution's key figures included:

A) Machiavelli and Luther
B) Galileo, Newton, and Kepler
C) Marx and Engels
D) Bismarck and Cavour

**43.** Voltaire was known for advocating:

A) Absolute monarchy
B) Freedom of speech, religious tolerance, and the separation of church and state
C) Communism
D) Imperialism

**44.** Jean-Jacques Rousseau's "The Social Contract" argued that:

A) Kings have divine authority
B) Legitimate political authority rests on the consent of the governed
C) Democracy is impossible
D) Society should return to feudalism

**45.** The Reign of Terror (1793-1794) during the French Revolution was led by:

A) Napoleon
B) Maximilien Robespierre and the Committee of Public Safety
C) Louis XVI
D) The Directory

**46.** The Concert of Europe after 1815 was:

A) A musical tradition
B) A system of diplomacy to maintain the balance of power and prevent revolution
C) A military alliance
D) An economic union

**47.** The Revolutions of 1848 across Europe were primarily driven by:

A) Religious conflicts
B) Nationalism, liberalism, and demands for democratic reform
C) Colonial disputes
D) Natural disasters

**48.** Social Darwinism was:

A) A scientific theory
B) The misapplication of evolutionary ideas to justify social inequality and imperialism
C) A political party
D) A religious movement

**49.** The Dreyfus Affair in France exposed:

A) Military brilliance
B) Anti-Semitism and divisions in French society
C) Colonial ambitions
D) Scientific fraud

**50.** The Scramble for Africa resulted in:

A) African nations becoming stronger
B) The near-total colonization of Africa by European powers by 1914
C) African independence
D) European withdrawal from Africa

**51.** The Balfour Declaration (1917) expressed support for:

A) Indian independence
B) A Jewish homeland in Palestine
C) Arab independence
D) French colonialism

**52.** Collectivization under Stalin meant:

A) Distributing land to farmers
B) Forcing small farms into large, state-controlled agricultural units
C) Privatizing industry
D) Encouraging free trade

**53.** The Spanish Civil War (1936-1939) was significant because:

A) It had no international impact
B) It served as a testing ground for WWII weapons and ideologies
C) Democracy triumphed
D) It led to Spanish democracy immediately

**54.** The Nuremberg Laws (1935) in Nazi Germany:

A) Protected Jewish rights
B) Stripped Jews of German citizenship and prohibited marriage between Jews and non-Jews
C) Promoted equality
D) Were repealed immediately

**55.** The United Nations was established in 1945 to:

A) Replace the League of Nations and promote international cooperation and peace
B) Create a world government
C) Divide Europe
D) Manage colonial territories

**56.** The Suez Crisis (1956) demonstrated:

A) British and French dominance
B) The declining influence of Britain and France and the rising power of the U.S. and Soviet Union
C) Egyptian weakness
D) NATO unity

**57.** The Hungarian Uprising (1956) was:

A) A successful revolution
B) A revolt against Soviet control crushed by Soviet forces
C) A peaceful transition
D) A trade dispute

**58.** The Helsinki Accords (1975) addressed:

A) Nuclear disarmament only
B) Security, cooperation, and human rights in Europe
C) Trade relations
D) Military alliances

**59.** The Chernobyl disaster (1986) was:

A) An earthquake
B) A catastrophic nuclear power plant accident in Soviet Ukraine
C) A military attack
D) A chemical spill

**60.** The Good Friday Agreement (1998) brought:

A) An end to the Cold War
B) A peace agreement to the conflict in Northern Ireland
C) German reunification
D) Balkan peace
