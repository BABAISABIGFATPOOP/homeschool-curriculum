# Western Civilization II CLEP Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Passing Score:** Approximately 50 (scaled score of 50 out of 80)

**Instructions:** Choose the best answer (A, B, C, or D) for each question. This exam covers Western civilization from approximately 1648 to the present.

---

**1.** The Enlightenment was an intellectual movement that emphasized:

A) Blind obedience to religious authority
B) Reason, science, and individual rights as the basis for understanding and improving society
C) A return to medieval values
D) The divine right of kings

**2.** John Locke's political philosophy argued that:

A) Monarchs had absolute power from God
B) Governments derive their power from the consent of the governed and must protect natural rights
C) Democracy was impossible to achieve
D) Only the wealthy should participate in government

**3.** Voltaire is best known for his advocacy of:

A) Absolute monarchy
B) Freedom of speech, religious tolerance, and criticism of institutional authority
C) Strict censorship
D) Military expansion

**4.** Montesquieu's *The Spirit of the Laws* advocated for:

A) A single all-powerful ruler
B) Separation of powers into legislative, executive, and judicial branches
C) The elimination of all government
D) Theocratic governance

**5.** Jean-Jacques Rousseau's concept of the "social contract" proposed that:

A) Individuals have no rights
B) Legitimate political authority rests on an agreement among the people
C) Only kings can create laws
D) Religion should govern all aspects of life

**6.** The French Revolution of 1789 was triggered by:

A) A foreign invasion
B) Financial crisis, social inequality, and Enlightenment ideas challenging the ancien régime
C) A religious conflict
D) A natural disaster

**7.** The Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen (1789) proclaimed:

A) The divine right of the French king
B) Liberty, property, security, and resistance to oppression as natural rights
C) The abolition of all private property
D) The establishment of a military dictatorship

**8.** The Reign of Terror (1793-1794) under Robespierre was characterized by:

A) A period of peaceful reform
B) Mass executions of perceived enemies of the Revolution
C) The restoration of the monarchy
D) Economic prosperity

**9.** Napoleon Bonaparte rose to power in France primarily through:

A) Democratic elections
B) Military success and a coup d'état in 1799
C) Inheriting the throne
D) A popular religious movement

**10.** The Napoleonic Code was significant because it:

A) Restored feudal privileges
B) Established a uniform legal code that influenced legal systems across Europe
C) Abolished all forms of government
D) Was only applied in France

**11.** The Congress of Vienna (1814-1815) aimed to:

A) Spread the ideals of the French Revolution across Europe
B) Restore the balance of power in Europe and return to conservative monarchical order
C) Create a unified European government
D) Abolish all monarchies

**12.** The Industrial Revolution began in:

A) France in the 1600s
B) Great Britain in the late 1700s
C) Germany in the 1850s
D) The United States in the 1900s

**13.** The factory system during the Industrial Revolution led to:

A) Improved working conditions for all
B) Urbanization, long working hours, and often dangerous conditions for workers
C) A return to agricultural economies
D) The elimination of child labor

**14.** Adam Smith's *The Wealth of Nations* (1776) argued for:

A) Government control of all industry
B) Free markets and limited government intervention in the economy
C) The abolition of private property
D) Mercantilism as the best economic system

**15.** Karl Marx's *The Communist Manifesto* (1848) predicted:

A) The triumph of capitalism
B) A workers' revolution that would overthrow capitalism and establish a classless society
C) The continued dominance of the aristocracy
D) A peaceful transition to socialism through elections

**16.** The revolutions of 1848 across Europe were driven primarily by:

A) Religious conflicts
B) Demands for liberal reforms, nationalism, and workers' rights
C) Colonial disputes
D) Natural disasters

**17.** Italian unification (the Risorgimento) was achieved largely through the efforts of:

A) Napoleon III
B) Garibaldi, Cavour, and Victor Emmanuel II
C) The Pope
D) The Austrian Empire

**18.** German unification was accomplished primarily under the leadership of:

A) Kaiser Wilhelm I alone
B) Otto von Bismarck through diplomatic maneuvering and wars
C) The German Parliament
D) A popular democratic movement

**19.** The Scramble for Africa in the late 19th century was driven by:

A) African nations inviting European colonizers
B) European competition for resources, markets, strategic territory, and national prestige
C) A humanitarian desire to improve African lives
D) The spread of democracy

**20.** The Berlin Conference of 1884-1885 established:

A) An independent African government
B) Rules for European colonization of Africa, dividing the continent without African input
C) Trade agreements between Africa and Asia
D) A military alliance among European powers

**21.** Social Darwinism was used to justify:

A) Equal rights for all peoples
B) European imperialism and racial hierarchies as natural outcomes of competition
C) Government welfare programs
D) International cooperation

**22.** The Dreyfus Affair in France revealed deep divisions over:

A) Economic policy
B) Anti-Semitism, militarism, and the role of justice in a republic
C) Colonial expansion
D) Industrial reform

**23.** The alliance system before World War I consisted of:

A) A single alliance of all European powers
B) Two major blocs: the Triple Alliance and the Triple Entente
C) Alliances between European and Asian powers
D) No formal alliances

**24.** The immediate cause of World War I was:

A) Germany's invasion of France
B) The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary
C) A naval confrontation between Britain and Germany
D) A dispute over African colonies

**25.** Trench warfare on the Western Front during World War I was characterized by:

A) Rapid territorial gains
B) Stalemate, massive casualties, and horrific conditions
C) Frequent cavalry charges
D) Decisive naval battles

**26.** The Russian Revolution of 1917 resulted in:

A) A strengthened Russian monarchy
B) The overthrow of the tsar and eventually the establishment of a communist government under the Bolsheviks
C) A democratic republic
D) A military dictatorship without ideological basis

**27.** The Treaty of Versailles (1919) imposed on Germany:

A) Generous peace terms
B) Harsh reparations, territorial losses, and military restrictions
C) No penalties
D) Territorial gains

**28.** The League of Nations failed primarily because:

A) It was too powerful
B) It lacked enforcement power and key nations like the United States did not join
C) It started a war
D) It was replaced immediately by the United Nations

**29.** The Weimar Republic in Germany was weakened by:

A) Too much economic prosperity
B) Hyperinflation, political extremism, and the Great Depression
C) A strong military tradition
D) Lack of any democratic institutions

**30.** Fascism, as it developed in Italy under Mussolini, emphasized:

A) Individual rights and democracy
B) Totalitarian state control, nationalism, and suppression of opposition
C) Communist economic principles
D) Religious governance

**31.** Adolf Hitler and the Nazi Party rose to power in Germany largely because of:

A) A strong economy
B) Economic despair, resentment of the Versailles Treaty, and effective propaganda
C) A military coup without public support
D) Support from the League of Nations

**32.** The policy of appeasement, exemplified by the Munich Agreement of 1938, involved:

A) Military confrontation with Germany
B) Giving in to Hitler's territorial demands in hopes of avoiding war
C) An economic embargo against Germany
D) A military alliance with the Soviet Union

**33.** The Holocaust was:

A) A natural disaster
B) The systematic genocide of six million Jews and millions of others by Nazi Germany
C) A military campaign
D) A political scandal

**34.** The Allied invasion of Normandy on D-Day (June 6, 1944) was significant because it:

A) Ended the war immediately
B) Opened a second front in Western Europe, leading to the liberation of France
C) Was a German victory
D) Occurred in the Pacific theater

**35.** The atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945:

A) Had no effect on the war
B) Led to Japan's surrender and the end of World War II
C) Were carried out by the Soviet Union
D) Were dropped on Germany

**36.** The Cold War was primarily a conflict between:

A) Britain and France
B) The United States and the Soviet Union over ideology, influence, and power
C) China and Japan
D) North and South America

**37.** The Iron Curtain, a term coined by Winston Churchill, referred to:

A) A physical wall across all of Europe
B) The ideological and physical boundary dividing communist Eastern Europe from democratic Western Europe
C) A trade barrier between Britain and France
D) A military fortification in Germany

**38.** The Marshall Plan was significant in the Cold War because it:

A) Provided military weapons to Western Europe
B) Helped rebuild Western European economies, preventing the spread of communism
C) Was a Soviet aid program
D) Created a European military alliance

**39.** The Berlin Blockade and Airlift (1948-1949) demonstrated:

A) Soviet willingness to negotiate
B) Western determination to resist Soviet expansion and maintain access to West Berlin
C) American withdrawal from Europe
D) The unification of Germany

**40.** The creation of the state of Israel in 1948 led to:

A) Immediate peace in the Middle East
B) Conflict with neighboring Arab states
C) The dissolution of the United Nations
D) The end of British colonialism worldwide

**41.** Decolonization after World War II was driven by:

A) European nations voluntarily releasing colonies
B) Nationalist movements, weakened European powers, and changing international attitudes toward colonialism
C) American military intervention in all colonies
D) A United Nations mandate to maintain empires

**42.** The Hungarian Revolution of 1956 was:

A) A successful overthrow of communism
B) A failed uprising against Soviet-backed communist rule, crushed by Soviet forces
C) A peaceful transition to democracy
D) A conflict between Hungary and Austria

**43.** The European Economic Community (EEC), established in 1957, aimed to:

A) Create a European military alliance
B) Promote economic integration among Western European nations
C) Spread communism across Europe
D) Dissolve national borders permanently

**44.** The Cuban Missile Crisis of 1962 brought the world closest to:

A) Economic collapse
B) Nuclear war between the United States and the Soviet Union
C) A European war
D) The dissolution of NATO

**45.** The Prague Spring of 1968 was:

A) A cultural festival
B) A period of political liberalization in Czechoslovakia, crushed by Soviet invasion
C) A successful revolution
D) An economic reform program in Poland

**46.** Détente in the 1970s refers to:

A) Increased Cold War tensions
B) A period of relaxed tensions between the United States and the Soviet Union
C) A military alliance between the superpowers
D) The end of the Cold War

**47.** The OPEC oil embargo of 1973 demonstrated:

A) Western energy independence
B) The power of oil-producing nations to influence global economics and politics
C) The decline of the oil industry
D) American self-sufficiency in energy

**48.** Margaret Thatcher's economic policies in Britain during the 1980s emphasized:

A) Nationalization of industries
B) Privatization, deregulation, and reduction of the welfare state
C) Increased government spending on social programs
D) Central economic planning

**49.** The Solidarity movement in Poland was significant because it:

A) Supported communist rule
B) Was the first independent labor union in a Soviet-bloc country and helped lead to the fall of communism
C) Was a military organization
D) Was funded entirely by the United States

**50.** Mikhail Gorbachev's policies of glasnost and perestroika:

A) Strengthened communist control
B) Introduced openness and economic restructuring that contributed to the end of the Soviet Union
C) Led to Soviet military expansion
D) Had no effect on Soviet society

**51.** The fall of the Berlin Wall in November 1989 symbolized:

A) The beginning of the Cold War
B) The collapse of communist rule in Eastern Europe
C) German division
D) Soviet military strength

**52.** The dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991 resulted in:

A) A stronger communist bloc
B) The independence of fifteen republics and the end of the Cold War
C) A unified global government
D) The expansion of the Warsaw Pact

**53.** The Maastricht Treaty of 1992 established:

A) NATO
B) The European Union with goals of political and economic integration
C) The Warsaw Pact
D) The United Nations

**54.** The Rwandan genocide of 1994 highlighted:

A) The effectiveness of international intervention
B) The failure of the international community to prevent mass atrocities
C) Successful peacekeeping by the United Nations
D) European military strength

**55.** The Balkan conflicts of the 1990s involved:

A) A war between Russia and China
B) Ethnic conflicts and genocide following the breakup of Yugoslavia
C) A trade war between European nations
D) A colonial dispute

**56.** The September 11, 2001, attacks led to:

A) Decreased military spending
B) The U.S.-led War on Terror, including invasions of Afghanistan and Iraq
C) American isolationism
D) The dissolution of NATO

**57.** The 2008 global financial crisis was primarily caused by:

A) A natural disaster
B) The collapse of the housing market, risky financial practices, and insufficient regulation
C) A military conflict
D) Government austerity before the crisis

**58.** The Arab Spring beginning in 2010-2011 was:

A) A cultural festival
B) A wave of pro-democracy protests and uprisings across the Middle East and North Africa
C) An economic trade agreement
D) A military alliance

**59.** Brexit refers to:

A) A British military operation
B) The United Kingdom's decision to leave the European Union
C) A trade agreement between Britain and the United States
D) A constitutional reform within Britain

**60.** The Paris Climate Agreement of 2015 aimed to:

A) Increase fossil fuel production
B) Unite nations in efforts to limit global warming and reduce greenhouse gas emissions
C) Create a global government
D) End all industrial activity
