# Western Civilization II CLEP Practice Exam 5

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Passing Score:** Approximately 50 (scaled score of 50 out of 80)

**Instructions:** Choose the best answer (A, B, C, or D) for each question. This exam covers Western civilization from approximately 1648 to the present.

---

**1.** The Enlightenment concept of "natural rights" was most fully articulated by:

A) Thomas Hobbes
B) John Locke
C) Niccolò Machiavelli
D) Martin Luther

**2.** Denis Diderot's *Encyclopédie* was significant because it:

A) Was a religious text promoting Catholic orthodoxy
B) Compiled knowledge from many fields and spread Enlightenment ideas to a wide audience
C) Was banned in every European country
D) Focused exclusively on military strategy

**3.** The economic theory of the Physiocrats held that:

A) Manufacturing was the source of all wealth
B) Agriculture was the primary source of a nation's wealth
C) Government should control all economic activity
D) Trade with colonies was unimportant

**4.** The causes of the French Revolution included all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Financial crisis and heavy taxation of the lower classes
B) Social inequality among the three estates
C) A prosperous and contented middle class
D) Inspiration from Enlightenment ideas

**5.** The storming of the Bastille on July 14, 1789, symbolized:

A) The restoration of the monarchy
B) The people's revolt against royal authority and the beginning of revolutionary action
C) A military victory over Britain
D) The end of the French Revolution

**6.** The Committee of Public Safety during the French Revolution was led by:

A) Louis XVI
B) Maximilien Robespierre
C) Napoleon Bonaparte
D) The Marquis de Lafayette

**7.** Napoleon's Continental System was designed to:

A) Promote free trade across Europe
B) Weaken Britain economically by blocking European trade with it
C) Create a European parliament
D) Spread democracy throughout Europe

**8.** Napoleon's invasion of Russia in 1812 failed primarily because of:

A) A well-coordinated Russian military defense
B) The scorched-earth tactics of the Russians and the brutal Russian winter
C) A naval blockade by Britain
D) An uprising within Napoleon's own army

**9.** The Congress of Vienna was guided by the principle of:

A) National self-determination
B) Legitimacy, restoring traditional rulers and maintaining the balance of power
C) Democratic reform
D) Colonial expansion

**10.** The Concert of Europe, established after the Congress of Vienna, was:

A) A musical society
B) A system of cooperation among European powers to maintain peace and suppress revolutionary movements
C) A trade organization
D) A military alliance against the Ottoman Empire

**11.** The Luddite movement in early 19th century England was a reaction against:

A) Political corruption
B) The displacement of workers by industrial machinery
C) Religious persecution
D) Foreign invasion

**12.** Utopian socialists like Robert Owen and Charles Fourier proposed:

A) Violent revolution
B) Ideal communities based on cooperation and shared resources
C) A return to feudalism
D) Laissez-faire capitalism

**13.** The Chartist movement in Britain (1830s-1850s) demanded:

A) Women's suffrage exclusively
B) Political reforms including universal male suffrage and secret ballots
C) The abolition of Parliament
D) Colonial expansion

**14.** The Irish Potato Famine (1845-1852) resulted in:

A) Increased Irish prosperity
B) Massive death and emigration, reducing Ireland's population dramatically
C) British withdrawal from Ireland
D) The discovery of new farming techniques

**15.** Napoleon III's Second French Empire (1852-1870) was characterized by:

A) A return to the Revolution's radical principles
B) Authoritarian rule combined with economic modernization and urban renewal (Baron Haussmann's Paris)
C) Complete isolation from European affairs
D) A democratic republic

**16.** The Crimean War (1853-1856) was significant for:

A) Resulting in no changes to European politics
B) Exposing the weakness of the Ottoman Empire and the inadequacy of military medicine, spurring reforms
C) Unifying all of Europe against Russia
D) Being the last war fought in Europe

**17.** The emancipation of the serfs in Russia (1861) by Tsar Alexander II:

A) Immediately created a prosperous peasant class
B) Freed the serfs but left many in poverty due to inadequate land distribution and redemption payments
C) Was opposed by all Russians
D) Ended all social inequality in Russia

**18.** Charles Darwin's *On the Origin of Species* (1859) was revolutionary because it:

A) Proved the existence of God
B) Proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection, challenging traditional views of creation
C) Was a work of fiction
D) Supported the idea that species never change

**19.** The unification of Italy was completed in 1870 when:

A) The Pope voluntarily gave up Rome
B) Rome was captured and became the capital of a united Italy
C) Austria ceded all Italian territories peacefully
D) A referendum was held across all Italian states

**20.** Bismarck's "blood and iron" approach to German unification involved:

A) Peaceful diplomacy exclusively
B) Strategic wars against Denmark, Austria, and France to unite the German states
C) Economic integration without military action
D) A popular democratic revolution

**21.** The Paris Commune of 1871 was:

A) A successful permanent government
B) A radical socialist and working-class government in Paris that was violently suppressed
C) A cultural festival
D) A meeting of European diplomats

**22.** The Second Industrial Revolution (late 19th century) was characterized by advances in:

A) Agriculture only
B) Steel, chemicals, electricity, and petroleum
C) Handcraft manufacturing
D) Sailing technology

**23.** Trade unions in the late 19th century formed primarily to:

A) Support factory owners
B) Advocate for better wages, working conditions, and workers' rights
C) Promote imperialism
D) Oppose public education

**24.** The "White Man's Burden," a phrase from Rudyard Kipling's poem, was used to justify:

A) Abolition of slavery
B) European imperialism as a supposed civilizing mission
C) Workers' rights movements
D) Women's suffrage

**25.** The Boer War (1899-1902) was fought between:

A) France and Germany
B) Britain and Dutch-descended settlers (Boers) in South Africa
C) The Ottoman Empire and Russia
D) Spain and its American colonies

**26.** The arms race and naval buildup before World War I, particularly between Britain and Germany, contributed to:

A) A lasting peace
B) Increasing tensions and an atmosphere of militarism
C) Disarmament across Europe
D) Improved diplomatic relations

**27.** The Schlieffen Plan was Germany's strategy to:

A) Defend against a Russian invasion
B) Quickly defeat France through Belgium before turning east to fight Russia
C) Build a larger navy than Britain
D) Form an alliance with France

**28.** New military technologies in World War I included all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Poison gas
B) Machine guns
C) Nuclear weapons
D) Tanks

**29.** The Balfour Declaration of 1917 expressed British support for:

A) Irish independence
B) A Jewish homeland in Palestine
C) Russian democracy
D) American entry into the war

**30.** The Russian Revolution of February 1917 resulted in:

A) The strengthening of the tsar's power
B) The abdication of Tsar Nicholas II and the establishment of a provisional government
C) A communist government immediately
D) A military dictatorship

**31.** Lenin's April Theses called for:

A) Support of the provisional government
B) Immediate peace, land for peasants, and power to the soviets (workers' councils)
C) Continued participation in World War I
D) Restoration of the monarchy

**32.** The New Economic Policy (NEP) introduced by Lenin in 1921:

A) Was a purely communist economic system
B) Allowed limited private enterprise and market mechanisms to revive the economy
C) Abolished all forms of trade
D) Nationalized all farms immediately

**33.** Stalin's Five-Year Plans emphasized:

A) Consumer goods production
B) Rapid industrialization and collectivization of agriculture
C) Free market economics
D) Decentralization of power

**34.** The Nuremberg Laws of 1935 in Nazi Germany:

A) Protected Jewish rights
B) Stripped Jews of German citizenship and prohibited intermarriage
C) Were economic reform laws
D) Established democratic elections

**35.** Kristallnacht (Night of Broken Glass) in 1938 was:

A) A celebration of German culture
B) A coordinated attack on Jewish businesses, synagogues, and homes across Germany and Austria
C) A political debate in the German Parliament
D) A military operation against Poland

**36.** The Nazi-Soviet Pact of 1939 was:

A) A permanent military alliance
B) A non-aggression agreement that included the secret division of Poland and Eastern Europe
C) A trade agreement only
D) A peace treaty ending World War I

**37.** The Battle of Britain (1940) was significant because:

A) Germany successfully invaded Britain
B) The Royal Air Force's defense prevented a German invasion and was the first major defeat for Hitler
C) France was liberated
D) The United States entered the war

**38.** The Battle of Stalingrad (1942-1943) was a turning point on the Eastern Front because:

A) Germany captured the city
B) The Soviet victory halted the German advance and began the push westward
C) It was a minor skirmish
D) Both sides agreed to a ceasefire

**39.** The Nuremberg Trials (1945-1946) established the principle that:

A) War crimes could not be prosecuted
B) Individuals could be held accountable for crimes against humanity and war crimes
C) Only the losing side in a war could be tried
D) International law did not apply to wartime actions

**40.** The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948) proclaimed:

A) Rights only for citizens of Western nations
B) Fundamental human rights that should be universally protected
C) The right of nations to colonize weaker peoples
D) Economic rights only

**41.** The Truman Doctrine and the policy of containment were responses to:

A) British imperialism
B) The perceived threat of Soviet communist expansion
C) Japanese militarism
D) Chinese nationalism

**42.** The Warsaw Pact (1955) was:

A) A Western European economic agreement
B) A military alliance of communist Eastern European nations led by the Soviet Union
C) A peace treaty ending the Korean War
D) A trade organization

**43.** The Suez Crisis of 1956 demonstrated:

A) British and French colonial dominance
B) The declining power of Britain and France and the rising influence of the United States and Soviet Union
C) Egyptian military weakness
D) American support for colonialism

**44.** The construction of the Berlin Wall in 1961 was intended to:

A) Improve relations between East and West
B) Stop the flow of East Germans fleeing to West Berlin
C) Protect West Berlin from attack
D) Facilitate trade between the two Germanys

**45.** The Second Vatican Council (1962-1965) was significant because it:

A) Rejected all modern reforms
B) Modernized many practices of the Catholic Church, including allowing Mass in local languages
C) Declared the pope infallible for the first time
D) United Catholic and Protestant churches

**46.** The student protests of 1968 in France and elsewhere reflected:

A) Support for conservative governments
B) Discontent with social norms, the Vietnam War, and authoritarian institutions
C) A desire to return to traditional values
D) Opposition to women's rights

**47.** The Helsinki Accords of 1975 were significant because they:

A) Ended the Cold War
B) Included human rights provisions that dissidents in Eastern Europe used to challenge their governments
C) Created a European military alliance
D) Dissolved NATO

**48.** The Iranian Revolution of 1979 resulted in:

A) A democratic government
B) The establishment of an Islamic republic under Ayatollah Khomeini
C) A military dictatorship
D) The restoration of the Shah

**49.** The Chernobyl nuclear disaster of 1986 highlighted:

A) The safety of nuclear power
B) The dangers of nuclear energy and the secrecy of the Soviet government
C) Western European environmental problems
D) A deliberate act of sabotage

**50.** The Tiananmen Square protests of 1989 in China resulted in:

A) Democratic reforms in China
B) A violent crackdown by the Chinese government on pro-democracy demonstrators
C) The fall of the Chinese Communist Party
D) International military intervention

**51.** German reunification occurred in:

A) 1985
B) 1990
C) 1995
D) 2000

**52.** The breakup of Yugoslavia in the 1990s led to:

A) Peaceful independence for all former republics
B) Ethnic conflicts, genocide (notably in Bosnia and Kosovo), and NATO intervention
C) A stronger unified state
D) Immediate EU membership for all successor states

**53.** The Good Friday Agreement of 1998 addressed:

A) Economic reform in Britain
B) The conflict in Northern Ireland between nationalist and unionist communities
C) Scottish independence
D) Immigration policy in the EU

**54.** The euro was introduced as a common currency for many EU nations in:

A) 1989
B) 1999
C) 2005
D) 2010

**55.** The enlargement of the European Union in 2004 was significant because it:

A) Reduced the number of member states
B) Admitted ten new member states, many from former communist Eastern Europe
C) Expelled several members
D) Created a European military

**56.** The Iraq War beginning in 2003 was justified primarily by claims that:

A) Iraq was responsible for the 9/11 attacks
B) Iraq possessed weapons of mass destruction, which were not found
C) Iraq had invaded a neighboring country
D) The United Nations authorized the invasion unanimously

**57.** The global financial crisis of 2008 led to:

A) Increased prosperity worldwide
B) Widespread recession, bank bailouts, and a reassessment of financial regulation
C) No significant economic impact
D) The dissolution of the European Union

**58.** The refugee crisis in Europe beginning around 2015 was primarily driven by:

A) Economic prosperity in the Middle East
B) Conflicts in Syria, Iraq, and Afghanistan, as well as instability in parts of Africa
C) European nations actively recruiting immigrants
D) A natural disaster in Europe

**59.** The rise of populist movements in Europe in the 2010s was fueled by:

A) Universal satisfaction with existing political institutions
B) Concerns about immigration, economic inequality, and dissatisfaction with established political parties
C) Declining nationalism
D) Increased support for the European Union

**60.** The COVID-19 pandemic beginning in 2020 impacted Western civilization by:

A) Having no significant social or economic effects
B) Causing widespread illness and death, economic disruption, and accelerating debates over public health policy and government authority
C) Strengthening international cooperation without any disagreements
D) Affecting only developing nations
