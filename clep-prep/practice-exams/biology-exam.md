# CLEP Biology — Full Practice Exam

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 115
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.

- Molecular and Cellular Biology: ~33%

### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **11.** Osmosis specifically refers to water moving through a selectively permeable membrane toward higher solute concentration.
- **18.** Glycolysis produces 4 ATP but uses 2, for a net gain of 2 ATP per glucose.
- **36.** Semiconservative: each daughter DNA molecule has one original (conserved) strand and one newly synthesized strand, as proven by the Meselson-Stahl experiment.
- **40.** Tt × tt → Tt, Tt, tt, tt. Half are tt (short).
- **55.** Natural selection requires variation, heritability, and differential survival/reproduction.
- **59.** Hardy-Weinberg: large population, random mating, no mutation, no migration, no selection.
- **67.** AaBb × AaBb produces the classic 9:3:3:1 ratio.
- **97.** The "10% rule": only about 10% of energy is transferred between trophic levels; the rest is lost as heat.
- **109.** Carrying capacity (K) is the maximum sustainable population size given available resources.
