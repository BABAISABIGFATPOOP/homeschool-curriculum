# CLEP Biology — Practice Exam 5

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** Which of the following best describes the structure of a phospholipid bilayer?

A) Two layers of proteins
B) A single layer of lipids
C) Two layers of phospholipids with hydrophilic heads facing outward and hydrophobic tails facing inward
D) A layer of carbohydrates between two protein layers

**2.** What is the function of DNA polymerase?

A) Unwinding the DNA double helix
B) Synthesizing new DNA strands by adding nucleotides
C) Transcribing DNA into mRNA
D) Translating mRNA into protein

**3.** Which of the following organisms is a prokaryote?

A) Mushroom
B) Amoeba
C) E. coli bacterium
D) Fern

**4.** What is the role of oxygen in aerobic respiration?

A) It is a reactant in glycolysis.
B) It acts as the final electron acceptor in the electron transport chain.
C) It is produced as a waste product.
D) It catalyzes the Krebs cycle.

**5.** Which of the following is a disaccharide?

A) Glucose
B) Fructose
C) Sucrose
D) Galactose

**6.** What happens during the S phase of the cell cycle?

A) The cell divides
B) DNA is replicated
C) Chromosomes condense
D) The nuclear envelope breaks down

**7.** Which of the following describes an organism's complete set of DNA?

A) Proteome
B) Genome
C) Phenotype
D) Karyotype

**8.** What is the function of the Calvin cycle?

A) To produce ATP from sunlight
B) To fix carbon dioxide into glucose
C) To split water molecules
D) To transport electrons

**9.** Which structure is responsible for filtering waste from the blood in the human body?

A) Liver
B) Lungs
C) Kidneys
D) Spleen

**10.** What is the process by which a cell engulfs large particles?

A) Exocytosis
B) Pinocytosis
C) Phagocytosis
D) Osmosis

**11.** In a Punnett square, if both parents are heterozygous (Bb × Bb), what percentage of offspring are expected to be homozygous recessive?

A) 0%
B) 25%
C) 50%
D) 75%

**12.** Which of the following is an example of a negative feedback mechanism?

A) Blood clotting after a wound
B) Contractions during labor increasing in intensity
C) Body temperature regulation through sweating
D) Ripening of fruit triggering more ripening

**13.** What is the main function of the rough endoplasmic reticulum?

A) Lipid synthesis
B) Protein synthesis and processing
C) Energy production
D) Detoxification

**14.** Which of the following best describes "adaptive radiation"?

A) The extinction of a species
B) The rapid diversification of a single lineage into many species that fill different ecological niches
C) The gradual change of one species into another
D) The movement of species across continents

**15.** What is the function of hemoglobin?

A) Fighting infections
B) Carrying oxygen in red blood cells
C) Clotting blood
D) Producing antibodies

**16.** Which of the following is true about mitosis?

A) It produces four genetically unique daughter cells.
B) It produces two genetically identical daughter cells.
C) It occurs only in reproductive cells.
D) It involves crossing over.

**17.** What is an ecological niche?

A) The physical location where an organism lives
B) The role an organism plays in its environment, including its habitat and resource use
C) The number of organisms in a population
D) A type of food web

**18.** Which of the following is a characteristic of the phylum Arthropoda?

A) Backbone
B) Exoskeleton and jointed appendages
C) Radial symmetry
D) Notochord

**19.** What is the primary function of the small intestine?

A) Water absorption
B) Mechanical digestion
C) Nutrient absorption
D) Bile production

**20.** Which process produces genetic variation through the exchange of DNA segments between homologous chromosomes?

A) Mitosis
B) Binary fission
C) Crossing over during meiosis
D) DNA replication

**21.** What is the role of ATP in cellular processes?

A) It stores genetic information.
B) It provides energy for cellular work.
C) It catalyzes biochemical reactions.
D) It transports oxygen.

**22.** Which of the following is an abiotic factor in an ecosystem?

A) Predators
B) Parasites
C) Temperature
D) Competitors

**23.** What is the function of transfer RNA (tRNA)?

A) To carry genetic information from the nucleus to the ribosome
B) To bring specific amino acids to the ribosome during translation
C) To form the structure of the ribosome
D) To regulate gene expression

**24.** Which type of cell division reduces the chromosome number by half?

A) Mitosis
B) Meiosis
C) Binary fission
D) Budding

**25.** What is the function of myelin sheaths in the nervous system?

A) To produce neurotransmitters
B) To insulate nerve fibers and speed up nerve impulse transmission
C) To connect neurons to muscles
D) To store memories

**26.** Which of the following best describes "genetic drift"?

A) The deliberate selection of organisms for breeding
B) Random changes in allele frequency in a population, especially in small populations
C) The movement of individuals between populations
D) The survival of the fittest

**27.** What is the function of the vas deferens in the male reproductive system?

A) Producing sperm
B) Transporting sperm from the epididymis to the urethra
C) Producing testosterone
D) Filtering urine

**28.** Which of the following is a density-independent limiting factor?

A) Competition for food
B) Disease outbreaks
C) A hurricane
D) Predation

**29.** What is the role of NADPH in photosynthesis?

A) It absorbs light energy.
B) It provides reducing power (electrons) for the Calvin cycle to fix carbon.
C) It breaks down glucose.
D) It releases oxygen.

**30.** Which of the following best describes a "clade" in phylogenetics?

A) A group of unrelated organisms
B) A group consisting of an ancestor and all of its descendants
C) A group of organisms in the same habitat
D) A group defined by physical appearance only

**31.** What is the difference between a ligament and a tendon?

A) Ligaments connect bones to bones; tendons connect muscles to bones.
B) Ligaments connect muscles to bones; tendons connect bones to bones.
C) They are the same thing.
D) Ligaments are only found in the skull; tendons are only in limbs.

**32.** Which of the following describes "incomplete dominance"?

A) One allele completely masks the other.
B) Both alleles are expressed equally.
C) The heterozygous phenotype is intermediate between the two homozygous phenotypes.
D) Multiple alleles determine the trait.

**33.** What is the function of the alveoli in the lungs?

A) Filtering air
B) Producing mucus
C) Gas exchange between air and blood
D) Warming inhaled air

**34.** Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of living organisms?

A) Growth and development
B) Response to stimuli
C) Constant body temperature
D) Metabolism

**35.** What is the role of restriction enzymes in biotechnology?

A) They join DNA fragments together.
B) They cut DNA at specific recognition sequences.
C) They copy DNA during PCR.
D) They translate mRNA into protein.

**36.** Which of the following best describes "endosymbiotic theory"?

A) All cells evolved from a single ancestor.
B) Mitochondria and chloroplasts were once free-living prokaryotes that were engulfed by larger cells.
C) Viruses evolved from bacteria.
D) Eukaryotic cells evolved from archaea.

**37.** What is the function of the hypothalamus?

A) Voluntary muscle control
B) Linking the nervous system to the endocrine system and regulating homeostasis
C) Processing visual information
D) Coordinating balance

**38.** Which of the following is an example of an X-linked recessive trait?

A) Sickle cell anemia
B) Color blindness
C) Cystic fibrosis
D) Huntington's disease

**39.** What is the carbon cycle?

A) The process by which carbon moves through the atmosphere, biosphere, geosphere, and hydrosphere
B) The process of carbon dating fossils
C) The breakdown of carbon in the body
D) The production of carbon by plants

**40.** Which type of immunity is acquired through vaccination?

A) Natural passive immunity
B) Artificial active immunity
C) Natural active immunity
D) Artificial passive immunity

**41.** What is the purpose of gel electrophoresis?

A) To amplify DNA
B) To separate DNA, RNA, or protein fragments by size
C) To sequence an entire genome
D) To clone organisms

**42.** Which of the following is a function of the liver?

A) Producing insulin
B) Detoxification and bile production
C) Filtering blood cells
D) Producing white blood cells

**43.** What is bioaccumulation?

A) The accumulation of organisms in a food web
B) The buildup of toxic substances in organisms as they move up the food chain
C) The increase in population size over time
D) The growth of coral reefs

**44.** Which of the following best describes "coevolution"?

A) One species evolving without influence from others
B) Two or more species reciprocally affecting each other's evolution
C) The extinction of one species due to another
D) The random evolution of two species at the same time

**45.** What is the structure and function of a lysosome?

A) A membrane-bound organelle containing digestive enzymes that break down waste and cellular debris
B) A structure that produces energy
C) A structure that stores genetic material
D) A structure that synthesizes proteins

**46.** Which of the following is a renewable resource?

A) Coal
B) Natural gas
C) Solar energy
D) Petroleum

**47.** What is the role of the diaphragm in respiration?

A) It filters air before it enters the lungs.
B) It contracts and relaxes to change thoracic volume, enabling breathing.
C) It produces surfactant.
D) It exchanges gases in the blood.

**48.** Which of the following is an example of sexual selection?

A) Camouflage in a moth
B) A peacock's elaborate tail feathers
C) Antibiotic resistance in bacteria
D) Mimicry in butterflies

**49.** What is the function of the corpus callosum?

A) Regulating hormones
B) Connecting the left and right hemispheres of the brain
C) Controlling balance
D) Processing emotions

**50.** Which molecule serves as the primary short-term energy storage in animals?

A) Starch
B) Glycogen
C) Cellulose
D) Triglycerides

**51.** What is the difference between a food chain and a food web?

A) They are the same thing.
B) A food chain shows a single pathway of energy flow; a food web shows interconnected food chains.
C) A food web is simpler than a food chain.
D) A food chain includes decomposers; a food web does not.

A) Bilateral symmetry
B) Radial symmetry and stinging cells (cnidocytes)
C) An exoskeleton
D) A complete digestive system

**53.** What is the function of the pituitary gland?

A) Producing insulin
B) Regulating other endocrine glands by producing controlling hormones
C) Filtering blood
D) Producing bile

A) Photosynthesis
B) Cellular respiration
C) Nitrogen fixation
