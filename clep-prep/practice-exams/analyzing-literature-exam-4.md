# CLEP Analyzing and Interpreting Literature — Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 80
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** A fourteen-line poem written in iambic pentameter with a specific rhyme scheme is called a:

A) Ballad
B) Sonnet
C) Haiku
D) Limerick

**2.** What literary device is used in "The wind howled through the empty streets"?

A) Simile
B) Metaphor
C) Personification
D) Hyperbole

**3.** In a narrative, the "climax" refers to:

A) The introduction of the characters
B) The turning point or moment of highest tension
C) The resolution of the conflict
D) The background information

**4.** Which point of view uses "I" and "me" to tell the story?

A) Third-person omniscient
B) Third-person limited
C) Second person
D) First person

**5.** The repetition of initial consonant sounds in a series of words is called:

A) Assonance
B) Alliteration
C) Consonance
D) Onomatopoeia

**6.** What is the term for a character who contrasts with the protagonist, highlighting particular qualities?

A) Antagonist
B) Foil
C) Narrator
D) Archetype

**7.** Read the following lines: "Shall I compare thee to a summer's day? / Thou art more lovely and more temperate." This is an example of:

A) Hyperbole
B) Simile
C) Metaphor
D) Apostrophe

**8.** An extended narrative poem that tells the story of a hero's adventures is called:

A) A lyric poem
B) An epic
C) A ballad
D) An ode

**9.** What does the term "denouement" refer to in plot structure?

A) The rising action
B) The inciting incident
C) The final resolution after the climax
D) The exposition

**10.** Which literary movement emphasized emotion, individualism, and nature?

A) Realism
B) Naturalism
C) Romanticism
D) Modernism

**11.** "He was a walking encyclopedia" is an example of:

A) Simile
B) Personification
C) Metaphor
D) Oxymoron

**12.** The mood of a literary work refers to:

A) The author's attitude toward the subject
B) The emotional atmosphere felt by the reader
C) The central message of the work
D) The setting of the story

**13.** What is "dramatic irony"?

A) When a character says the opposite of what they mean
B) When the audience knows something that a character does not
C) When the outcome of events is opposite to what was expected
D) When words are used in a humorous way

**14.** A "soliloquy" in drama is:

A) A conversation between two characters
B) A speech delivered by a character alone on stage, revealing their thoughts
C) Stage directions for the actors
D) A song performed by the chorus

**15.** What is the primary purpose of "exposition" in a story?

A) To create suspense
B) To provide background information about characters and setting
C) To resolve the conflict
D) To present the moral of the story

**16.** Which figure of speech combines contradictory terms?

A) Paradox
B) Oxymoron
C) Irony
D) Litotes

**17.** "To be, or not to be, that is the question" is an example of what type of speech in drama?

A) Aside
B) Monologue
C) Soliloquy
D) Dialogue

**18.** The "setting" of a story includes:

A) Only the time period
B) Only the physical location
C) The time, place, and social environment
D) The characters' backgrounds

**19.** What literary technique is used when a story provides hints about future events?

A) Flashback
B) Foreshadowing
C) Stream of consciousness
D) In medias res

**20.** A "flat character" is one who:

A) Undergoes significant change throughout the story
B) Has complex, multi-dimensional traits
C) Has only one or two personality traits and does not change
D) Serves as the main character

**21.** Which of the following is an example of situational irony?

A) A fire station burns down.
B) A character says "What lovely weather" during a storm.
C) The audience knows the murderer's identity, but the detective does not.
D) A politician gives a speech about honesty while lying.

**22.** What is a "motif" in literature?

A) The central message or lesson
B) A recurring element that has symbolic significance
C) The main character's goal
D) The author's purpose for writing

**23.** In poetry, "enjambment" refers to:

A) The use of end rhyme
B) A pause at the end of a line
C) The continuation of a sentence beyond the end of a line
D) The repetition of vowel sounds

**24.** Which of the following best defines "allegory"?

A) A brief reference to a well-known person, place, or event
B) A story in which characters and events symbolize broader themes or moral lessons
C) A humorous imitation of a serious work
D) A comparison using "like" or "as"

**25.** What narrative technique begins a story in the middle of the action?

A) Foreshadowing
B) Flashback
C) In medias res
D) Frame narrative

**26.** The "tone" of a literary work refers to:

A) The emotional feeling the reader experiences
B) The author's attitude toward the subject matter
C) The volume at which the work should be read
D) The time period in which the work was written

**27.** Which of the following is a characteristic of free verse poetry?

A) Strict rhyme scheme
B) Fixed meter throughout
C) No regular meter or rhyme scheme
D) Exactly fourteen lines

**28.** A "dynamic character" is one who:

A) Remains the same throughout the story
B) Has few personality traits
C) Undergoes a significant internal change
D) Appears only briefly in the story

**29.** What is "verbal irony"?

A) When the outcome is the opposite of what is expected
B) When a character says the opposite of what they mean
C) When the audience knows something the character does not
D) When actions contradict words

**30.** In poetry, a "stanza" is:

A) A single line of verse
B) A grouping of lines separated by a space
C) The rhythm of the poem
D) A type of rhyme scheme

**31.** What literary device is used in "My love is like a red, red rose"?

A) Metaphor
B) Simile
C) Personification
D) Synecdoche

**32.** The term "protagonist" refers to:

A) The villain of the story
B) The main character of the story
C) The narrator of the story
D) A minor character

**33.** What is a "frame narrative"?

A) A story within a story
B) A story told in reverse chronological order
C) A story with multiple protagonists
D) A story told entirely through letters

**34.** Which literary period is characterized by a focus on ordinary people and everyday life, rejecting idealized portrayals?

A) Romanticism
B) Realism
C) Transcendentalism
D) Gothic literature

**35.** The term "catharsis" in literature refers to:

A) The introduction of the conflict
B) The emotional release experienced by the audience
C) The protagonist's tragic flaw
D) A comedic resolution

**36.** What is "stream of consciousness" as a narrative technique?

A) A technique where the narrator addresses the reader directly
B) A continuous flow of a character's thoughts and feelings
C) A technique of telling the story in reverse
D) A method of using multiple narrators

**37.** In the phrase "All hands on deck," the word "hands" is an example of:

A) Metaphor
B) Synecdoche
C) Simile
D) Hyperbole

**38.** Which term describes a character's tragic flaw that leads to their downfall?

A) Catharsis
B) Hubris
C) Hamartia
D) Pathos

**39.** What is the "unreliable narrator"?

A) A narrator who tells the story in third person
B) A narrator whose account of events cannot be fully trusted
C) A narrator who breaks the fourth wall
D) A narrator who only reveals their own thoughts

**40.** "The pen is mightier than the sword" is an example of:

A) Simile
B) Alliteration
C) Metonymy
D) Onomatopoeia

**41.** What is "satire" in literature?

A) A tragic story about a hero's downfall
B) The use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize or mock
C) A long narrative poem
D) A type of romantic poetry

**42.** Which of the following describes a "stock character"?

A) A character who develops throughout the story
B) A stereotypical, conventional character type
C) The main character of the story
D) A character who narrates the story

**43.** In Shakespeare's works, an "aside" is:

A) A long speech given by one character to another
B) A brief remark by a character intended for the audience but not heard by other characters
C) Stage directions written in the margins
D) A song performed between acts

**44.** What is the "theme" of a literary work?

A) The summary of the plot
B) The underlying message or central idea
C) The main character's motivation
D) The setting and time period

**45.** Which poetic form consists of three lines with syllable counts of 5, 7, and 5?

A) Limerick
B) Sonnet
C) Haiku
D) Couplet

**46.** A "bildungsroman" is:

A) A mystery novel
B) A coming-of-age story that focuses on a character's growth from youth to adulthood
C) A satirical novel about society
D) An epic poem

**47.** What literary device is used when rain appears in a scene to reflect a character's sadness?

A) Foreshadowing
B) Pathetic fallacy
C) Allegory
D) Flashback

**48.** The rhyme scheme ABAB CDCD EFEF GG belongs to which type of sonnet?

A) Petrarchan (Italian)
B) Shakespearean (English)
C) Spenserian
D) Free verse

**49.** What is "anaphora"?

A) A reference to another work of literature
B) The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive lines or clauses
C) A contradiction in terms
D) The omission of words from a sentence

**50.** Which term describes the use of words that imitate sounds?

A) Alliteration
B) Assonance
C) Onomatopoeia
D) Consonance

**51.** A "parable" is best described as:

A) A long narrative poem about heroic deeds
B) A short, simple story that teaches a moral or spiritual lesson
C) A speech by a single character in a play
D) A humorous imitation of another work

**52.** What is the function of a "chorus" in Greek tragedy?

A) To serve as the main character
B) To provide background information, commentary, and reflect on events
C) To create comic relief
D) To narrate flashback sequences

**53.** "She sells seashells by the seashore" is an example of:

A) Assonance
B) Alliteration
C) Onomatopoeia
D) Consonance

**54.** What does "in medias res" mean in Latin?

A) In the beginning
B) At the end
C) In the middle of things
D) From the perspective of

A) A villain who is purely evil with no other traits
B) A character with complex, multi-dimensional personality traits
C) A character who appears in only one scene
D) A character who never speaks

**56.** What is "hyperbole"?

A) An extreme exaggeration for emphasis or effect
B) A comparison using "like" or "as"
C) A mild or indirect statement
D) A reference to a famous person or event

A) Is purely evil
B) Is of noble stature and falls due to a personal flaw
C) Always survives at the end
D) Is a comedic figure

A) Simile
B) Hyperbole
C) Anthropomorphism
D) Allusion

**59.** The "point of view" in a story determines:

A) The moral of the story
B) The perspective from which the story is told
C) The physical setting
D) The conflict type

**60.** Which of the following is an example of "allusion"?

A) "He had the strength of ten men."
B) "She was an Einstein when it came to math."
C) "The leaves danced in the wind."
D) "Life is a journey."

**61.** A "couplet" in poetry is:

A) A stanza with four lines
B) Two consecutive lines that rhyme
C) A poem with no rhyme scheme
D) A line with exactly ten syllables

**62.** What is the meaning of "hubris" in Greek tragedy?

A) A sense of fate or destiny
B) Excessive pride or arrogance
C) Deep sadness or grief
D) Fear of the gods

A) Linear narrative
B) Reverse chronology
C) Epistolary
D) Picaresque

**64.** What is a "deus ex machina"?

A) A type of dramatic irony
B) An unexpectedly contrived solution to a seemingly unsolvable problem
C) A recurring symbolic element
D) A character who provides comic relief

**65.** The literary term "verisimilitude" refers to:

A) The villain's motivation
B) The appearance of being true or real
C) The use of vivid imagery
D) A story's rhyme scheme

**66.** What is the difference between "theme" and "subject"?

A) They are the same thing.
B) The subject is what the work is about; the theme is the insight or message about that subject.
C) The theme is the plot; the subject is the setting.
D) The subject is always stated directly; the theme is the author's biography.

**67.** Which of the following best defines "irony"?

A) A comparison between two unlike things
B) A contrast between expectation and reality
C) The repetition of sounds
D) An exaggeration for effect

**68.** In poetry, "caesura" refers to:

A) The last word of a line
B) A strong pause within a line of verse
C) The rhyming pattern
D) The number of syllables per line

**69.** What is an "epistolary novel"?

A) A novel told through a series of letters, diary entries, or documents
B) A novel about a journey or adventure
C) A novel with multiple narrators
D) A novel set in the future

**70.** Which of the following is a characteristic of Modernist literature?

A) Idealized depictions of nature
B) Strict adherence to traditional forms
C) Experimentation with form, fragmentation, and stream of consciousness
D) Focus on religious themes

**71.** A "monologue" differs from a "soliloquy" in that:

A) A monologue is spoken to other characters; a soliloquy is spoken alone
B) A monologue is always in poetry; a soliloquy is always in prose
C) A monologue is shorter than a soliloquy
D) There is no difference

**72.** What is "litotes"?

A) A deliberate understatement, often using double negatives
B) An extreme exaggeration
C) A comparison using "like"
D) A reference to mythology

**73.** The "antagonist" in a story is:

A) Always a person
B) The character or force that opposes the protagonist
C) The main character
D) The narrator

**74.** Which of the following best describes "magic realism"?

B) Realistic narratives that include magical or fantastical elements treated as ordinary

B) To provide a humorous break that reduces tension

A) The emotional tone of the poem
B) The pattern of stressed and unstressed syllables
C) The number of lines in a stanza
D) The subject matter

**77.** What is an "archetype" in literature?
