# CLEP College Composition — Full Practice Exam

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 50 (multiple choice section)
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); check with your institution.

> **Important Note:** The actual CLEP College Composition exam also includes TWO essay sections (one requiring a position/argument and one requiring a revision of a given passage). This practice exam covers only the multiple-choice portion. Be sure to practice timed essay writing as well. Some institutions offer "College Composition Modular" which may have a different essay format.

---

## Questions

### Section I: Grammar and Mechanics (Questions 1–15)

**1.** Choose the sentence with correct punctuation.

A) The dog, who was very old laid on the porch.
B) The dog who was very old, laid on the porch.
C) The dog, who was very old, lay on the porch.
D) The dog who was very old lay, on the porch.

**2.** Which sentence contains a comma splice?

A) Running down the street, the child tripped.
B) I wanted to go to the store, however it was closed.
C) After the rain stopped, we went outside.
D) The movie was long, but it was entertaining.

**3.** Choose the correct sentence.

A) Each of the students need to bring their textbook.
B) Each of the students need to bring his or her textbook.
C) Each of the students needs to bring their textbook.
D) Each of the students needs to bring his or her textbook.

**4.** Identify the sentence with a dangling modifier.

A) While walking to class, the rain began to fall.
B) While walking to class, she noticed the rain.
C) She noticed the rain while walking to class.
D) The rain began to fall as she walked to class.

**5.** Which word correctly completes the sentence? "The committee presented ______ findings to the board."

A) it's
B) its
C) its'
D) their's

**6.** Which sentence uses the semicolon correctly?

A) I went to the store; and bought milk.
B) I went to the store; I bought milk.
C) I went to the store; buying milk.
D) I went; to the store and bought milk.

**7.** Choose the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement.

A) The group of students are waiting in the hallway.
B) The group of students is waiting in the hallway.
C) The group of students were waiting in the hallway.
D) The group of students have been waiting in the hallway.

**8.** Which sentence has a misplaced modifier?

A) She almost drove her car for 300 miles.
B) She drove her car for almost 300 miles.
C) She drove almost 300 miles in her car.
D) Almost 300 miles were driven by her.

**9.** Select the sentence with correct pronoun usage.

B) Between you and me, the test was easy.
C) Between you and myself, the test was easy.

**10.** Which sentence correctly uses an apostrophe?

A) The teacher's lounge is on the second floor. (one teacher)
B) The teachers lounge is on the second floor.
C) The teacher's lounge is on the second floor. (multiple teachers)
D) The teachers's lounge is on the second floor.

**11.** Choose the correct word: "The weather will ______ our plans."

A) affect
B) effect
C) affective
D) effecting

**12.** Which sentence is punctuated correctly?

A) "Where are you going"? she asked.
B) "Where are you going?" she asked.
C) "Where are you going," she asked?
D) "Where are you going?" She asked.

**13.** Identify the sentence with a parallel structure error.

A) She likes reading, writing, and swimming.
B) She likes reading, writing, and to swim.

**15.** Choose the correct form: "If I ______ you, I would study harder."

A) was

### Section II: Sentence Structure and Revision (Questions 16–30)

Read each passage or sentence and answer the question that follows.

**16.** "The experiment failed. Because the temperature was too high."

Which revision best corrects the error?

A) The experiment failed; because the temperature was too high.
B) The experiment failed because the temperature was too high.
C) The experiment failed, because, the temperature was too high.
D) The experiment failed: because the temperature was too high.

**17.** "Students who study regularly tend to perform better on exams. Also, they experience less anxiety. Furthermore, they retain information longer."

What is the most effective way to combine these sentences?

A) Students who study regularly tend to perform better on exams, experience less anxiety, and retain information longer.
B) Students who study regularly tend to perform better on exams also they experience less anxiety furthermore they retain information longer.
C) Students who study regularly tend to perform better on exams; also, they experience less anxiety; furthermore, they retain information longer.
D) Studying regularly helps students perform better, and it also helps with anxiety, and also they retain information.

**18.** Which of the following is a sentence fragment?

A) Running through the rain.
B) She runs through the rain.
C) She was running through the rain.
D) Run through the rain!

**19.** "The mayor announced the new policy. The policy will reduce taxes. The policy will also increase funding for schools."

Which revision is most effective?

A) The mayor announced a new policy that will reduce taxes and increase funding for schools.
B) The mayor announced the new policy and the policy will reduce taxes and the policy will also increase funding for schools.
C) The mayor announced the new policy, which reduces taxes, and it increases funding for schools too.
D) The mayor, who announced the new policy, will reduce taxes and increase funding for schools.

**20.** Read the paragraph:

Which sentence should be removed to improve the coherence of the paragraph?

A) Sentence 1
B) Sentence 2
C) Sentence 3
D) Sentence 4

**21.** "Not only did the team win the championship, ______ they also set a new record."

A) and
B) but

**22.** Which revision eliminates wordiness most effectively?

Original: "Due to the fact that the weather was bad, we cancelled the event."

A) Because the weather was bad, we cancelled the event.
B) On account of the bad weather situation, we cancelled the event.
C) The reason we cancelled the event was because of the weather being bad.
D) It was due to bad weather that the cancellation of the event occurred.

**23.** "The researcher collected data. She analyzed the results. She published her findings."

Which is the best revision?

A) After collecting data and analyzing the results, the researcher published her findings.
B) The researcher collected data, and she analyzed the results, and she published her findings.
C) Collecting data, analyzing results, publishing findings was what the researcher did.
D) The researcher, who collected data and analyzed results, and then published findings.

**24.** Identify the run-on sentence:

A) The concert was amazing, and the band played for three hours.
B) The concert was amazing the band played for three hours.
C) Although the concert was long, it was amazing.
D) The concert was amazing; the band played for three hours.

(4) However, the initial cost can be prohibitive for some families. (5) The cost of solar panels has actually decreased by 70% over the past decade."

For the best logical flow, sentence 5 should be placed:

**27.** Which transition word best fits? "The study showed positive results. ______, further research is needed to confirm the findings."

**28.** "Having finished the book, the movie adaptation was watched by Sarah."

### Section III: Rhetorical Analysis (Questions 31–42)

Read the following passage and answer questions 31–35.

> "Every year, millions of tons of food are wasted in the United States while millions of Americans go hungry. This paradox demands our immediate attention. According to the USDA, approximately 30–40% of the food supply is wasted. Meanwhile, food banks across the country struggle to meet growing demand. Some argue that food waste is simply a byproduct of consumer choice and abundance. However, this argument ignores the moral imperative to address hunger in one of the wealthiest nations on Earth. By implementing better distribution systems, reforming 'sell-by' date labeling, and educating consumers, we can significantly reduce food waste while feeding more people."

**31.** The primary purpose of this passage is to:

A) Describe the process of food distribution
B) Argue that food waste should be reduced to address hunger
C) Explain why Americans waste food
D) Compare the U.S. food system to other countries

A) Impress the reader with a large number
B) Provide credible evidence supporting the severity of the problem
C) Criticize the USDA
D) Compare food waste across different countries

**33.** The word "paradox" in the second sentence refers to:

A) The cost of food production
B) The simultaneous existence of food waste and hunger

**34.** The author addresses the counterargument that food waste is "a byproduct of consumer choice" in order to:

**35.** The passage's organizational structure is best described as:

A) Chronological narrative
B) Compare and contrast
C) Problem-solution with counterargument

### Answer Explanations (Selected)

- **1.** "Who was very old" is a nonrestrictive clause and needs commas on both sides. The past tense of "lie" (recline) is "lay," not "laid."
- **3.** "Each" is singular, so "needs" is correct. "His or her" agrees with the singular antecedent. (Note: "their" as singular is increasingly accepted, but D is the most traditionally correct answer.)
- **4.** "While walking to class" modifies nobody in the main clause—"the rain" wasn't walking.
- **8.** "Almost drove 300 miles" implies she nearly drove but didn't. "Drove almost 300 miles" correctly modifies the distance.
- **15.** Subjunctive mood: "If I were" is correct for hypothetical/contrary-to-fact conditions.
- **25.** Sentence 5 counters the cost concern in sentence 4, so it logically follows sentence 4.
- **30.** A semicolon with a transitional word ("however") correctly joins two independent clauses.
- **37.** The idiom means saving small amounts now while losing much more in the long run.
- **46.** MLA uses author and page number with no comma: (Smith 42).
