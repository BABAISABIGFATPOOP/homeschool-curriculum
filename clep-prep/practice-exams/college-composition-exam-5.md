# CLEP College Composition — Practice Exam 5

**Time Limit:** 120 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 50
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.

---

## Questions

**1.** Which of the following sentences is a run-on?

A) The sun set, and the stars appeared.
B) The sun set the stars appeared the moon rose.
C) After the sun set, the stars appeared.
D) The sun set; the stars appeared.

**2.** What is the correct plural possessive of "children"?

A) childrens'
B) children's
C) childrens
D) children'

**3.** Which sentence best demonstrates conciseness?

A) In the event that you are unable to attend, please notify us.
B) If you cannot attend, please notify us.
C) In the circumstance that you find yourself unable to attend the meeting, please be sure to notify us.
D) Should it happen that attendance is not possible for you, notification to us would be appreciated.

**4.** Identify the type of sentence: "Although the rain stopped, the game was still delayed because the field was flooded."

A) Simple
B) Compound
C) Complex
D) Compound-complex

**5.** Which of the following is an example of a straw man fallacy?

A) Appealing to emotions rather than logic
B) Misrepresenting someone's argument to make it easier to attack
C) Attacking the person rather than their argument
D) Assuming that one event caused another

**6.** Select the sentence with correct comma usage.

A) The tall, brick, building stood at the corner.
B) The tall brick building stood at the corner.
C) The, tall, brick building stood at the corner.
D) The tall, brick building, stood at the corner.

**7.** What is the role of evidence in an argumentative essay?

A) To fill space in the body paragraphs
B) To support and prove the claims made in the thesis
C) To present the opposing viewpoint
D) To entertain the reader

**8.** Which of the following sentences uses "fewer" and "less" correctly?

A) There are less students in class today.
B) There is fewer water in the glass.
C) There are fewer students in class today.
D) There is fewer rain this year.

**9.** What is the purpose of a transition sentence at the end of a paragraph?

A) To introduce new evidence
B) To link the current paragraph to the next one
C) To restate the topic sentence
D) To provide a citation

**10.** Which sentence contains a restrictive (essential) clause?

A) My brother, who lives in Texas, is visiting next week.
B) The students who studied passed the exam.
C) Our teacher, who is very kind, gave us extra time.
D) The Grand Canyon, which is in Arizona, is beautiful.

**11.** What does "cite" mean in academic writing?

A) To see a source
B) To give credit to a source by referencing it
C) To criticize a source
D) To copy a source word for word

**12.** Which of the following represents correct APA in-text citation?

A) (Smith 42)
B) (Smith, 2020)
C) (Smith, p. 42, 2020)
D) [Smith 2020]

**13.** Which revision best fixes the pronoun reference error? "When Sarah met Jennifer, she was nervous."

A) When Sarah met Jennifer, Sarah was nervous.
B) When Sarah met Jennifer, she was very nervous.
C) She was nervous when Sarah met Jennifer.
D) When they met, she was nervous.

**14.** What type of organizational pattern compares similarities and differences between two subjects?

A) Cause and effect
B) Problem-solution
C) Compare and contrast
D) Classification

**15.** Which sentence is in active voice?

A) The cake was eaten by the children.
B) The ball was thrown by the pitcher.
C) The children ate the cake.
D) The report was written by the manager.

**16.** What is the purpose of an abstract in academic writing?

A) To replace the introduction
B) To provide a brief summary of the entire paper
C) To present the conclusion only
D) To list references

**17.** Which of the following demonstrates correct use of quotation marks with other punctuation?

A) She said "I'm leaving."
B) She said, "I'm leaving."
C) She said, "I'm leaving".
D) She said "I'm leaving",

**18.** What is a "claim" in argumentative writing?

A) A fact that cannot be disputed
B) A statement that expresses a position or opinion that can be supported with evidence
C) A question posed to the reader
D) A summary of the opposing viewpoint

**19.** Select the sentence with correct use of "who" vs. "whom."

A) Who did you give the book to?
B) To who did you give the book?
C) Whom did you give the book to?
D) To whom you gave the book?

**20.** Which of the following is an example of understatement?

A) I've told you a million times.
B) It's just a scratch. (referring to a large dent in a car)
C) The classroom was a zoo.
D) Her smile was the sun.

**21.** What is the function of a subordinating conjunction?

A) To join two independent clauses of equal importance
B) To connect a dependent clause to an independent clause
C) To connect two nouns
D) To replace a pronoun

**22.** Which sentence correctly uses a coordinating conjunction?

A) I wanted to go, so but I was too tired.
B) I wanted to go but I was too tired.
C) I wanted to go, but I was too tired.
D) I wanted, to go but, I was too tired.

**23.** What is the "rhetorical triangle"?

A) Introduction, body, conclusion
B) Ethos, pathos, logos
C) Thesis, evidence, analysis
D) Topic, support, transition

**24.** Which of the following sentences uses an appositive correctly?

A) My neighbor a retired teacher volunteers at the library.
B) My neighbor, a retired teacher, volunteers at the library.
C) My neighbor a retired teacher, volunteers at the library.
D) My neighbor, a retired teacher volunteers at the library.

**25.** What is the difference between "imply" and "infer"?

A) They mean the same thing.
B) "Imply" means to suggest; "infer" means to conclude from evidence.
C) "Imply" means to conclude; "infer" means to suggest.
D) "Imply" is formal; "infer" is informal.

**26.** Which of the following is a feature of formal academic writing?

A) Use of slang and colloquialisms
B) First-person point of view throughout
C) Objective tone and evidence-based reasoning
D) Short, incomplete sentences for emphasis

**27.** What is the purpose of a "signal phrase" when integrating quotations?

A) To signal the end of a paragraph
B) To introduce a quotation and identify the source
C) To summarize the quotation
D) To replace the need for a citation

**28.** Which sentence contains a gerund?

A) The running water was cold.
B) She was running quickly.
C) Running is excellent exercise.
D) The running back scored a touchdown.

**29.** Which logical fallacy involves offering only two options when more exist?

A) Slippery slope
B) False dilemma
C) Red herring
D) Hasty generalization

**30.** Select the correctly punctuated sentence.

A) The following students were honored, Maria, James, and Li.
B) The following students were honored: Maria, James, and Li.
C) The following students were honored; Maria, James, and Li.
D) The following students, were honored Maria, James, and Li.

**31.** What is plagiarism?

A) Paraphrasing a source and citing it
B) Using someone else's ideas or words without proper attribution
C) Quoting a source with quotation marks and a citation
D) Summarizing a source in your own words with a citation

**32.** Which sentence demonstrates the correct use of a participial phrase?

A) Exhausted from the hike, the trail seemed endless.
B) Exhausted from the hike, we thought the trail seemed endless.
C) The trail seemed endless, exhausted from the hike.
D) Exhausted from the hike was the trail seemingly endless.

**33.** What type of evidence is a statistic from a peer-reviewed journal?

A) Anecdotal evidence
B) Empirical evidence
C) Testimonial evidence
D) Circumstantial evidence

**34.** Which of the following is NOT a prewriting strategy?

A) Brainstorming
B) Outlining
C) Freewriting
D) Proofreading

**35.** Select the sentence with correct subject-verb agreement.

A) Neither the teacher nor the students was prepared.
B) Neither the teacher nor the students were prepared.
C) Neither the teacher nor the students has been prepared.
D) Neither the teachers nor the student were prepared.

**36.** What is the function of the word "however" in a sentence?

A) It shows cause and effect.
B) It signals a contrast or contradiction.
C) It introduces an example.
D) It shows addition.

**37.** Which of the following demonstrates correct MLA parenthetical citation for a source with two authors?

A) (Smith and Jones, 42)
B) (Smith & Jones 42)
C) (Smith and Jones 42)
D) (Smith, Jones, 42)

**38.** What is the purpose of varying sentence length in writing?

A) To meet a word count requirement
B) To create rhythm and maintain reader interest
C) To confuse the reader
D) To show vocabulary knowledge

**39.** Which sentence uses the word "complement" correctly?

A) She gave him a nice complement on his work.
B) The wine complements the meal perfectly.
C) He complemented her on her presentation.
D) She received many complements.

**40.** What is the difference between denotation and connotation?

A) Denotation is the emotional meaning; connotation is the literal meaning.
B) Denotation is the literal, dictionary meaning; connotation is the associated emotional meaning.
C) They mean the same thing.
D) Denotation applies to verbs; connotation applies to nouns.

**41.** Which sentence correctly uses a semicolon with a conjunctive adverb?

A) She studied hard, therefore she passed the exam.
B) She studied hard; therefore, she passed the exam.
C) She studied hard therefore; she passed the exam.
D) She studied hard; therefore she passed, the exam.

**42.** What is the "occasion" in the SOAPSTone analysis method?

A) The author of the text
B) The time and place that provoked the writing
C) The intended audience
D) The subject of the text

**43.** Which of the following contains a double negative?

A) She doesn't have any money.
B) She has no money.
C) She doesn't have no money.
D) She hardly has any money.

**44.** What is the correct way to cite a paraphrase?

A) No citation is needed since it's in your own words.
B) Use quotation marks and a citation.
C) Provide a citation without quotation marks.
D) Put the paraphrase in italics.

**45.** Which of the following best describes "coherence" in writing?

A) Using big vocabulary words
B) The logical flow and connection of ideas throughout a text
C) Writing very long paragraphs
D) Avoiding any repetition

**46.** Select the sentence that uses "lay" and "lie" correctly.

A) I need to lay down for a nap.
B) Please lie the book on the table.
C) She lay in bed all morning yesterday.
D) He has been laying in bed since noon.

**47.** What is the purpose of a "working thesis"?

A) A final, polished thesis statement
B) A preliminary thesis that may be revised during the writing process
C) A thesis that has been approved by a teacher
D) A thesis that summarizes the conclusion

**48.** Which of the following is an example of personification?

A) She ran as fast as a cheetah.
B) The wind whispered through the trees.
C) He was a lion in battle.
D) The sunset was like a painting.

**49.** What is the correct order for the writing process?

A) Drafting, prewriting, revising, editing, publishing
B) Prewriting, drafting, revising, editing, publishing
C) Prewriting, editing, drafting, revising, publishing
D) Revising, prewriting, drafting, editing, publishing

**50.** Which of the following is true about paragraph unity?

A) Each paragraph should cover multiple topics.
B) Each paragraph should focus on one main idea.
C) Paragraphs do not need topic sentences.
D) Paragraph length should always be exactly five sentences.
