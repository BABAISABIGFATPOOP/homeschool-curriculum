# CLEP U.S. History II — Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Format:** Multiple choice (A, B, C, D)
**Passing Score:** Typically 50 (on CLEP's 20–80 scale); approximately 50% correct is a reasonable target, but check with your institution.
**Covers:** 1865 to the present

---

## Questions

**1.** The Gilded Age (roughly 1870s–1900) was characterized by:

A) Economic equality and social harmony
B) Rapid industrialization, wealth inequality, and political corruption
C) Minimal economic growth
D) Isolationism from global affairs

**2.** Who was the industrialist known as the "king of steel"?

A) John D. Rockefeller
B) Andrew Carnegie
C) J.P. Morgan
D) Cornelius Vanderbilt

**3.** The Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882 was significant because it:

A) Encouraged Chinese immigration
B) Was the first major federal law restricting immigration based on nationality
C) Granted citizenship to Chinese immigrants
D) Established trade with China

**4.** The Dawes Act of 1887 aimed to:

A) Preserve Native American tribal lands
B) Break up tribal lands and assimilate Native Americans into white culture
C) Establish new reservations
D) Grant Native Americans voting rights

**5.** What was the purpose of the Sherman Antitrust Act of 1890?

A) To promote monopolies
B) To break up monopolies and trusts that restrained trade
C) To lower tariffs
D) To regulate the banking industry

**6.** The Spanish-American War of 1898 resulted in the U.S. acquiring:

A) Canada
B) Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines
C) Mexico
D) Alaska and Hawaii

**7.** The Progressive Era (1890s–1920s) was characterized by:

A) Laissez-faire economics
B) Social and political reform movements addressing corruption, inequality, and working conditions
C) A return to agricultural economy
D) Isolationism

**8.** Who wrote "The Jungle" (1906), exposing conditions in the meatpacking industry?

A) Ida Tarbell
B) Upton Sinclair
C) Jacob Riis
D) Lincoln Steffens

**9.** The 19th Amendment (1920) granted:

A) Prohibition of alcohol
B) Women the right to vote
C) Freedom of speech
D) Equal protection under the law

**10.** The 18th Amendment (1919) established:

A) Women's suffrage
B) The income tax
C) Prohibition of alcohol
D) Direct election of senators

**11.** What was the primary cause of U.S. entry into World War I in 1917?

A) The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand
B) Unrestricted German submarine warfare and the Zimmermann Telegram
C) The bombing of Pearl Harbor
D) Treaty obligations with France

**12.** President Wilson's Fourteen Points included:

A) A plan for American territorial expansion
B) A proposal for the League of Nations and self-determination for peoples
C) A demand for German territory
D) A plan for American isolationism

**13.** The "Red Scare" of 1919–1920 was a period of:

A) Celebration of communist ideals
B) Fear of communism and radical political movements in the U.S.
C) A military conflict with the Soviet Union
D) Economic prosperity

**14.** The Harlem Renaissance was:

A) A political movement for civil rights
B) A cultural and artistic movement celebrating African American identity
C) A religious revival
D) An economic reform movement

**15.** What caused the Stock Market Crash of 1929?

A) Government regulation of the stock market
B) Speculation, overproduction, and buying stocks on margin
C) A foreign invasion
D) A natural disaster

**16.** The New Deal, implemented by Franklin D. Roosevelt, aimed to:

A) Reduce government involvement in the economy
B) Provide relief, recovery, and reform during the Great Depression
C) Prepare for World War II
D) Eliminate labor unions

**17.** The Dust Bowl of the 1930s primarily affected:

A) The Northeast
B) The Great Plains
C) The Pacific Northwest
D) The Southeast

**18.** What event triggered U.S. entry into World War II?

A) The invasion of Poland
B) The attack on Pearl Harbor (December 7, 1941)
C) The fall of France
D) The sinking of the Lusitania

**19.** Executive Order 9066 (1942) authorized:

A) The desegregation of the military
B) The internment of Japanese Americans
C) The rationing of food supplies
D) The creation of the Manhattan Project

**20.** D-Day (June 6, 1944) refers to:

A) The bombing of Hiroshima
B) The Allied invasion of Normandy, France
C) The surrender of Germany
D) The liberation of Paris

**21.** The Manhattan Project was:

A) A housing initiative in New York City
B) The secret program to develop the atomic bomb
C) A plan to rebuild Europe after the war
D) A spy network during the Cold War

**22.** The Marshall Plan (1948) was designed to:

A) Contain communism in Asia
B) Rebuild Western European economies after World War II
C) Create a military alliance
D) Establish the United Nations

**23.** The Truman Doctrine (1947) pledged:

A) Neutrality in all conflicts
B) U.S. support for countries resisting communist expansion
C) Free trade with the Soviet Union
D) Dismantling of nuclear weapons

**24.** The Korean War (1950–1953) ended with:

A) A decisive U.S. victory
B) An armistice that maintained the division at the 38th parallel
C) A unified communist Korea
D) The withdrawal of all foreign troops

**25.** McCarthyism in the 1950s was characterized by:

A) Support for communist ideals
B) Aggressive investigations and accusations of communist infiltration in American society
C) Peaceful diplomacy with the Soviet Union
D) Economic reform

**26.** Brown v. Board of Education (1954) ruled that:

A) Separate but equal was constitutional
B) Racial segregation in public schools was unconstitutional
C) Voting rights could be denied based on race
D) Affirmative action was required

**27.** The Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955–1956) was sparked by:

A) Martin Luther King Jr.'s assassination
B) Rosa Parks refusing to give up her seat
C) The desegregation of schools
D) The passage of the Civil Rights Act

**28.** The Interstate Highway Act of 1956 was significant because it:

A) Created the first railroad system
B) Authorized the construction of a national highway system
C) Established air traffic control
D) Built the first bridges

**29.** The Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) involved:

A) A U.S. invasion of Cuba
B) The discovery of Soviet nuclear missiles in Cuba, bringing the world close to nuclear war
C) A trade embargo only
D) A Cuban invasion of the U.S.

**30.** The Civil Rights Act of 1964 prohibited:

A) Interracial marriage
B) Discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin
C) Public protests
D) Immigration from certain countries

**31.** The Voting Rights Act of 1965:

A) Established property requirements for voting
B) Eliminated barriers such as literacy tests that prevented African Americans from voting
C) Lowered the voting age to 18
D) Granted women the right to vote

**32.** The Gulf of Tonkin Resolution (1964) authorized:

A) The invasion of North Korea
B) The president to use military force in Vietnam without a formal declaration of war
C) Nuclear strikes against the Soviet Union
D) A trade embargo on China

**33.** The Tet Offensive (1968) was significant because:

A) It was a decisive American victory
B) It turned American public opinion against the Vietnam War
C) It ended the war
D) It resulted in a peace treaty

**34.** What was the significance of the moon landing on July 20, 1969?

A) It ended the Cold War.
B) The U.S. became the first nation to land humans on the moon.
C) It established a permanent lunar base.
D) It was a Soviet achievement.

**35.** The Watergate scandal led to:

A) The impeachment of Bill Clinton
B) The resignation of President Richard Nixon in 1974
C) The assassination of a president
D) A constitutional convention

**36.** Title IX (1972) prohibited:

A) Racial discrimination in housing
B) Sex-based discrimination in educational programs receiving federal funding
C) Age discrimination in the workplace
D) Religious discrimination

**37.** The Camp David Accords (1978) were a peace agreement between:

A) The U.S. and the Soviet Union
B) Egypt and Israel, brokered by President Carter
C) North and South Vietnam
D) The U.S. and China

**38.** The Iran Hostage Crisis (1979–1981) involved:

A) An oil embargo
B) The seizure of the American embassy in Tehran and 52 hostages held for 444 days
C) A nuclear threat from Iran
D) A military invasion of Iran

**39.** "Reaganomics" was characterized by:

A) Increased government spending on social programs
B) Tax cuts, deregulation, and reduced government spending on domestic programs
C) Nationalization of industries
D) Isolationist foreign policy

**40.** The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 symbolized:

A) The beginning of the Cold War
B) The end of communist control in Eastern Europe
C) German reunification only
D) A military defeat

**41.** The Persian Gulf War (1991) was fought to:

A) Remove Saddam Hussein from power
B) Liberate Kuwait from Iraqi invasion
C) Establish democracy in Iraq
D) Protect oil fields in Saudi Arabia

**42.** NAFTA (1994) was:

A) A military alliance
B) A free trade agreement between the U.S., Canada, and Mexico
C) An environmental treaty
D) A healthcare reform bill

**43.** The Oklahoma City bombing (1995) was:

A) An international terrorist attack
B) A domestic terrorist attack by Timothy McVeigh
C) An accident
D) A foreign military strike

**44.** The September 11, 2001, attacks targeted:

A) The Capitol Building and the Pentagon
B) The World Trade Center and the Pentagon, with a fourth plane crashing in Pennsylvania
C) Military bases only
D) The White House

**45.** The USA PATRIOT Act (2001) was enacted to:

A) Reduce government surveillance
B) Expand law enforcement powers to combat terrorism
C) Limit military spending
D) Reform immigration

**46.** The Great Recession of 2007–2009 was primarily caused by:

A) A natural disaster
B) The collapse of the housing market and risky financial practices
C) A military conflict
D) Government austerity measures

**47.** What was the significance of Barack Obama's election in 2008?

A) He was the youngest president ever elected.
B) He was the first African American elected president.
C) He was the first Democrat elected in decades.
D) He was a third-party candidate.

**48.** The Affordable Care Act (2010) aimed to:

A) Eliminate all healthcare programs
B) Expand access to health insurance and reduce healthcare costs
C) Privatize Medicare
D) Create a single-payer system

**49.** What was the primary goal of the women's liberation movement of the 1960s and 1970s?

A) Returning women to traditional roles
B) Achieving equal rights, opportunities, and pay for women
C) Abolishing marriage
D) Creating separate institutions for women

**50.** The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) was established in:

A) 1950
B) 1970
C) 1990
D) 2000

**51.** The policy of "containment" during the Cold War meant:

A) Expanding communism worldwide
B) Preventing the spread of communism beyond where it already existed
C) Isolating the United States from world affairs

A) Urban renewal projects
B) The growth of suburbia in post-WWII America

A) Funding for the Manhattan Project
