# U.S. History II CLEP Practice Exam 4

**Time Limit:** 90 minutes
**Number of Questions:** 60
**Passing Score:** Approximately 50 (scaled score of 50 out of 80)

**Instructions:** Choose the best answer (A, B, C, or D) for each question.

---

1. The Reconstruction era following the Civil War is generally dated from:

A) 1860 to 1870
B) 1865 to 1877
C) 1870 to 1890
D) 1876 to 1900

2. The 13th Amendment to the Constitution accomplished which of the following?

A) Granted citizenship to all persons born in the United States
B) Abolished slavery throughout the United States
C) Guaranteed voting rights regardless of race
D) Established equal protection under the law

3. The Black Codes enacted by Southern states in 1865-1866 were designed to:

A) Protect the civil rights of formerly enslaved people
B) Restrict the freedom and labor of African Americans
C) Provide land grants to formerly enslaved people
D) Integrate public schools in the South

4. The Freedmen's Bureau was established primarily to:

A) Redistribute plantation land to formerly enslaved people
B) Provide food, education, medical care, and legal assistance to freed people and poor whites
C) Enforce voting rights for African Americans in the South
D) Establish military rule in former Confederate states

5. Radical Republicans in Congress advocated for:

A) Lenient treatment of former Confederate states
B) Strict requirements for readmission and strong protections for African American rights
C) Immediate restoration of Southern states without conditions
D) Compensation for former slaveholders

6. The impeachment of President Andrew Johnson in 1868 was primarily related to:

A) His involvement in the assassination of Abraham Lincoln
B) His violation of the Tenure of Office Act
C) His refusal to enforce the 14th Amendment
D) Financial corruption in his administration

7. The Compromise of 1877 effectively ended Reconstruction by:

A) Passing new civil rights legislation
B) Removing federal troops from the South in exchange for Rutherford B. Hayes becoming president
C) Granting amnesty to all former Confederates
D) Establishing new Freedmen's Bureau offices across the South

8. Sharecropping in the post-Civil War South resulted in:

A) Economic independence for most formerly enslaved people
B) A cycle of debt and poverty for many African American and poor white farmers
C) The rapid industrialization of Southern agriculture
D) Equal land distribution among all Southern farmers

9. The completion of the transcontinental railroad in 1869 was significant because it:

A) Ended the need for steamboat transportation
B) Connected the eastern and western United States, facilitating trade and migration
C) Was built entirely by American-born workers
D) Was funded solely by private investment without government support

10. The Homestead Act of 1862 encouraged westward settlement by:

A) Paying settlers to move west
B) Offering 160 acres of public land to settlers who improved it over five years
C) Granting land only to veterans of the Civil War
D) Selling western land at reduced prices to railroad companies

11. The Dawes Act of 1887 attempted to:

A) Preserve Native American tribal lands and cultures
B) Assimilate Native Americans by dividing tribal lands into individual allotments
C) Create a unified Native American government
D) Relocate all Native Americans to Oklahoma Territory

12. The Gilded Age term, coined by Mark Twain, referred to a period characterized by:

A) Genuine prosperity shared equally by all Americans
B) Surface-level wealth masking underlying social problems like corruption and inequality
C) A golden age of American literature and arts
D) The expansion of democratic participation

13. Andrew Carnegie's business empire was primarily built in:

A) Oil refining
B) Steel manufacturing
C) Railroad construction
D) Banking and finance

14. The theory of Social Darwinism was used to justify:

A) Government regulation of big business
B) The wealth of industrialists as a natural result of competition
C) Labor union organizing
D) Progressive taxation

15. The Knights of Labor differed from later unions in that they:

A) Accepted only skilled white male workers
B) Welcomed workers of all skills, races, and genders
C) Focused exclusively on wage increases
D) Opposed all forms of political activism

16. The Haymarket Square incident of 1886 in Chicago damaged the labor movement because:

A) Workers won too many concessions from employers
B) A bombing was blamed on anarchists, turning public opinion against labor
C) The government passed pro-labor legislation afterward
D) It demonstrated the effectiveness of peaceful protest

17. The Sherman Antitrust Act of 1890 was intended to:

A) Promote the formation of business trusts
B) Break up monopolies and prevent restraint of trade
C) Regulate railroad rates
D) Establish minimum wage laws

18. Ellis Island served primarily as:

A) A military base defending New York Harbor
B) An immigration processing center for newcomers arriving on the East Coast
C) A quarantine station for livestock
D) A customs house for international trade

19. The Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882 was significant because it:

A) Encouraged Chinese immigration to fill labor shortages
B) Was the first federal law to restrict immigration based on nationality
C) Applied only to Chinese diplomats
D) Was repealed within two years of passage

20. The Populist Party of the 1890s drew most of its support from:

A) Urban industrial workers
B) Farmers and rural Americans struggling with debt and low crop prices
C) Wealthy Eastern bankers
D) Railroad executives

21. The Plessy v. Ferguson decision of 1896 established the legal doctrine of:

A) One person, one vote
B) Separate but equal, upholding racial segregation
C) Desegregation of public schools
D) Universal suffrage regardless of race

22. Yellow journalism, associated with William Randolph Hearst and Joseph Pulitzer, contributed to:

A) The passage of the first press censorship laws
B) Public support for the Spanish-American War through sensationalized reporting
C) The decline of newspaper readership
D) Improved journalistic standards nationwide

23. The explosion of the USS Maine in Havana Harbor in 1898 led to:

A) A diplomatic resolution with Spain
B) The United States declaring war on Spain
C) An American withdrawal from Caribbean affairs
D) A formal alliance between the U.S. and Spain

24. As a result of the Spanish-American War, the United States gained control of:

A) Cuba, Mexico, and Canada
B) The Philippines, Puerto Rico, and Guam
C) Hawaii, Alaska, and the Panama Canal Zone
D) Jamaica, Bermuda, and the Bahamas

25. The Open Door Policy (1899-1900) regarding China called for:

A) Exclusive American trading rights in China
B) Equal trading opportunities for all nations in China
C) The colonization of China by Western powers
D) Chinese independence from all foreign influence

26. Theodore Roosevelt's approach to foreign policy was often described as:

A) Isolationist
B) "Speak softly and carry a big stick"
C) Appeasement toward European powers
D) Non-interventionist

27. The Progressive Era (roughly 1890-1920) was characterized by efforts to:

A) Reduce government involvement in the economy
B) Address social problems caused by industrialization, urbanization, and corruption
C) Return to agrarian economic values
D) Expand American territorial holdings overseas

28. Muckrakers were Progressive Era journalists who:

A) Supported the interests of big business
B) Exposed corruption, unsafe conditions, and social injustice through investigative reporting
C) Campaigned for restrictions on press freedom
D) Focused exclusively on foreign affairs reporting

29. Upton Sinclair's novel *The Jungle* (1906) led most directly to:

A) The creation of the Federal Reserve
B) The passage of the Pure Food and Drug Act and the Meat Inspection Act
C) The breakup of Standard Oil
D) The establishment of the National Park Service

30. The 19th Amendment, ratified in 1920, granted:

A) Prohibition of alcohol
B) Women the right to vote
C) Direct election of senators
D) The income tax

31. The Panama Canal, completed in 1914, was strategically important because it:

A) Connected the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, reducing shipping times dramatically
B) Provided irrigation for Central American agriculture
C) Served primarily as a military fortification
D) Was the first international construction project

32. Woodrow Wilson's Fourteen Points proposed:

A) A harsh peace settlement punishing Germany
B) Principles for peace including self-determination, free trade, and a League of Nations
C) American annexation of European territories
D) A permanent military alliance with Britain and France

33. The United States entered World War I in 1917 primarily because of:

A) The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand
B) Unrestricted German submarine warfare and the Zimmermann Telegram
C) A direct attack on American territory
D) Treaty obligations to France

34. The Red Scare of 1919-1920 was fueled by:

A) The discovery of Soviet spies in the U.S. government
B) Fear of communist and anarchist influence following the Russian Revolution
C) A communist takeover of the American labor movement
D) The election of socialist candidates to Congress

35. The Harlem Renaissance of the 1920s was a cultural movement centered on:

A) Scientific innovation and technological progress
B) African American literary, artistic, and musical expression
C) European immigrant contributions to American culture
D) The revival of traditional folk music

36. Prohibition, established by the 18th Amendment, was repealed by the:

A) 19th Amendment
B) 20th Amendment
C) 21st Amendment
D) 22nd Amendment

37. The economic boom of the 1920s was characterized by:

A) Government ownership of major industries
B) Widespread consumer spending, installment buying, and stock market speculation
C) Declining industrial production
D) A return to agrarian economic principles

38. The Scopes Trial of 1925 centered on the conflict between:

A) Labor unions and management
B) Teaching evolution in public schools versus religious fundamentalism
C) Free press and government censorship
D) States' rights and federal authority

39. The stock market crash of October 1929 was triggered in part by:

A) A sudden increase in international trade
B) Excessive speculation, overproduction, and buying stocks on margin
C) Government regulation of the stock market
D) A natural disaster destroying major industrial centers

40. During the Great Depression, approximately what percentage of Americans were unemployed at its peak?

A) 10 percent
B) 15 percent
C) 25 percent
D) 40 percent

41. The Dust Bowl of the 1930s was primarily caused by:

A) Excessive rainfall and flooding
B) Drought combined with poor farming practices that stripped topsoil
C) Industrial pollution from nearby factories
D) Intentional burning of grasslands by settlers

42. Franklin Roosevelt's New Deal included all of the following programs EXCEPT:

A) The Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC)
B) The Social Security Act
C) The Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA)
D) The Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI)

43. The Wagner Act of 1935 was significant for labor because it:

A) Banned all labor strikes
B) Guaranteed workers the right to organize and bargain collectively
C) Established a federal minimum wage
D) Created the Department of Labor

44. The Supreme Court initially challenged New Deal programs on the grounds that they:

A) Were too conservative
B) Exceeded the federal government's constitutional authority
C) Did not go far enough in addressing poverty
D) Violated international trade agreements

45. The Lend-Lease Act of 1941 allowed the United States to:

A) Send American troops to fight in Europe before Pearl Harbor
B) Provide military supplies to Allied nations while remaining officially neutral
C) Impose economic sanctions on Japan
D) Establish military bases throughout Europe

46. The Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941 resulted in:

A) A negotiated peace settlement with Japan
B) The United States declaring war on Japan and entering World War II
C) American withdrawal from the Pacific
D) An immediate invasion of the Japanese mainland

47. Executive Order 9066 led to:

A) The desegregation of the armed forces
B) The internment of Japanese Americans in relocation camps
C) The creation of the Manhattan Project
D) The rationing of consumer goods

48. D-Day, June 6, 1944, refers to:

A) The bombing of Hiroshima
B) The Allied invasion of Normandy, France
C) The surrender of Italy
D) The Battle of the Bulge

49. The Manhattan Project was the secret program to:

A) Develop radar technology
B) Develop the atomic bomb
C) Break German and Japanese codes
D) Build long-range bomber aircraft

50. The decision to drop atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki was justified by President Truman primarily as:

A) Revenge for Pearl Harbor
B) A way to end the war quickly and avoid a costly invasion of Japan
C) A demonstration of power directed at the Soviet Union
D) A response to Japanese use of chemical weapons

51. The Truman Doctrine of 1947 pledged American support to:

A) All nations seeking independence from colonial rule
B) Countries resisting communist expansion or subversion
C) European nations rebuilding after World War II
D) Asian nations threatened by Japanese imperialism

52. The Marshall Plan (1948) was designed to:

A) Rebuild the American military after World War II
B) Provide economic aid to rebuild Western European economies and prevent the spread of communism
C) Create a military alliance against the Soviet Union
D) Establish the United Nations

53. The Berlin Airlift of 1948-1949 was a response to:

A) A Soviet invasion of West Berlin
B) The Soviet blockade of land routes to West Berlin
C) An American withdrawal from Germany
D) A German request for reunification

54. The Korean War (1950-1953) ended with:

A) A clear American victory and the reunification of Korea
B) An armistice that left Korea divided near the 38th parallel
C) A North Korean victory and communist control of the entire peninsula
D) A United Nations mandate for Korean independence

55. Senator Joseph McCarthy gained notoriety in the early 1950s for:

A) His opposition to the Vietnam War
B) His aggressive and often unsubstantiated accusations of communist infiltration in the government
C) His support for civil rights legislation
D) His criticism of the Marshall Plan

56. The Supreme Court's decision in Brown v. Board of Education (1954) ruled that:

A) Affirmative action in college admissions was constitutional
B) Racial segregation in public schools was unconstitutional
C) School prayer was a violation of the First Amendment
D) States could set their own education standards without federal oversight

57. The Montgomery Bus Boycott of 1955-1956 was sparked by:

A) A Supreme Court ruling on bus segregation
B) Rosa Parks' refusal to give up her seat to a white passenger
C) Martin Luther King Jr.'s first public speech
D) A violent attack on African American bus riders

58. The Interstate Highway Act of 1956, signed by Eisenhower, was motivated in part by:

A) The need to reduce railroad traffic
B) National defense considerations and the desire to evacuate cities in case of nuclear attack
C) Lobbying by environmental groups
D) A desire to connect national parks for tourism

59. The launch of Sputnik by the Soviet Union in 1957 led the United States to:

A) Withdraw from the space race
B) Increase funding for science education and create NASA
C) Negotiate a ban on space exploration
D) Focus exclusively on military satellite development

60. The U-2 incident of 1960 involved:

A) A Soviet spy caught in Washington, D.C.
B) The shooting down of an American reconnaissance plane over the Soviet Union
C) A failed American invasion of Cuba
D) A nuclear submarine accident in the Atlantic
