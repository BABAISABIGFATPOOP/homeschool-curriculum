# Spanish Language CLEP Study Guide

## Exam Overview

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Format** | Multiple choice (reading) + listening sections |
| **Number of Questions** | ~120 (approx. 80 reading, 40 listening) |
| **Time Limit** | 90 minutes |
| **Passing Score** | ~50 out of 80 (scaled); approximately 50% correct |
| **College Credit** | Typically 6-12 semester hours (depending on score) |
| **Note** | This exam tests Level I and II college Spanish; higher scores may earn more credit |

The Spanish CLEP tests reading and listening comprehension at the intermediate level. It covers grammar, vocabulary, reading passages, and audio-based questions. There is no speaking or writing component.

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Reading Comprehension (40%)

- **Passage types**: advertisements, letters, articles, narratives, instructions, dialogues
- **Skills tested**:
  - Main idea identification
  - Supporting details
  - Vocabulary in context
  - Inference and interpretation
  - Tone and purpose of the passage
  - Understanding cultural references

**Key Strategies:**
- Read the questions before the passage to know what to look for
- Look for cognates (words similar to English: familia = family, hospital = hospital)
- Use context clues for unfamiliar words
- Pay attention to verb tenses — they tell you WHEN something happens
- Don't try to translate word-by-word; focus on overall meaning
- Watch for "false cognates" (embarazada = pregnant, NOT embarrassed; actualmente = currently, NOT actually)

### 2. Grammar (35%)

#### Verb Tenses — The Core of Spanish Grammar

**Present Tense (Presente)**
- Regular conjugations: -ar (hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, hablan), -er (como, comes, come, comemos, comen), -ir (vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, viven)
- Common irregular verbs: ser, estar, ir, tener, hacer, decir, poder, saber, conocer, querer, venir, salir, dar, ver, poner
- Stem-changing verbs: e→ie (pensar, querer), o→ue (dormir, poder), e→i (pedir, servir)
- Uses: habitual actions, general truths, current actions

**Preterite (Preterito)**
- Regular: -ar (hable, hablaste, hablo, hablamos, hablaron), -er/-ir (comi, comiste, comio, comimos, comieron)
- Key irregulars: ser/ir (fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fueron), hacer (hice, hiciste, hizo...), tener (tuve...), estar (estuve...), poder (pude...), poner (puse...), saber (supe...), decir (dije...), querer (quise...), venir (vine...)
- Uses: completed actions, specific time, beginning/end of an action, a series of events

**Imperfect (Imperfecto)**
- Regular: -ar (hablaba, hablabas, hablaba, hablabamos, hablaban), -er/-ir (comia, comias, comia, comiamos, comian)
- Only 3 irregulars: ser (era), ir (iba), ver (veia)
- Uses: habitual past actions ("used to"), descriptions, background information, ongoing past actions, age, time, weather in the past

**Preterite vs. Imperfect — Critical Distinction:**
- Preterite = completed, specific, "what happened"
- Imperfect = ongoing, habitual, descriptive, "what was happening"
- Example: "Llovia (imperfect — it was raining) cuando sali (preterite — when I left)"

**Future (Futuro)**
- Regular: infinitive + e, as, a, emos, an (hablare, hablaras, hablara, hablaremos, hablaran)
- Irregular stems: tener (tendr-), poder (podr-), saber (sabr-), hacer (har-), decir (dir-), querer (querr-), salir (saldr-), venir (vendr-), poner (pondr-)
- Also: ir + a + infinitive for near future ("Voy a comer" = I'm going to eat)

**Conditional (Condicional)**
- Regular: infinitive + ia, ias, ia, iamos, ian
- Same irregular stems as future tense
- Uses: would + verb, polite requests, hypothetical situations

**Present Perfect (Preterito Perfecto)**
- haber (he, has, ha, hemos, han) + past participle (-ado/-ido)
- Irregular past participles: hecho (hacer), dicho (decir), escrito (escribir), visto (ver), puesto (poner), abierto (abrir), muerto (morir), vuelto (volver), roto (romper), cubierto (cubrir)
- Uses: actions completed recently or with relevance to the present ("I have eaten")

**Subjunctive (Subjuntivo) — High-Frequency Test Topic**
- Present subjunctive: formed from yo form of present → drop -o → add opposite endings (-ar: -e, -es, -e, -emos, -en; -er/-ir: -a, -as, -a, -amos, -an)
- Irregulars: ser (sea), ir (vaya), saber (sepa), dar (de), estar (este), haber (haya)
- **WEIRDO triggers** (when to use subjunctive):
  - **W**ishes/wants: querer que, desear que, esperar que
  - **E**motions: alegrarse de que, temer que, es triste que
  - **I**mpersonal expressions: es importante que, es necesario que, es posible que
  - **R**ecommendations: recomendar que, sugerir que, aconsejar que
  - **D**oubt/denial: dudar que, no creer que, negar que
  - **O**jala (que): "hopefully"
- Subjunctive after conjunctions: antes de que, para que, a menos que, sin que, cuando (future), hasta que (future)
- Subjunctive in adjective clauses when the antecedent is unknown or nonexistent: "Busco un libro que sea interesante"

**Commands (Imperativo)**
- Tu affirmative: 3rd person singular present (habla, come, escribe); irregulars: di, haz, ve, pon, sal, se, ten, ven
- Tu negative: no + present subjunctive (no hables, no comas)
- Usted: present subjunctive forms (hable, coma, escriba)
- Ustedes: present subjunctive forms (hablen, coman, escriban)

#### Ser vs. Estar — Essential Distinction

**Ser (to be — permanent/inherent):**
- Identity: Soy estudiante
- Origin: Es de Mexico
- Time/Date: Son las tres
- Characteristics: Es alto, es inteligente
- Material: La mesa es de madera
- Possession: Es mi libro
- Events (location of): La fiesta es en mi casa
- Remember: **DOCTOR** — Description, Occupation, Characteristic, Time, Origin, Relationship

**Estar (to be — temporary/condition/location):**
- Location: Estoy en la escuela
- Health/condition: Estoy enfermo
- Emotions: Esta triste
- Progressive: Estoy comiendo
- Result of action: La puerta esta abierta
- Remember: **PLACE** — Position, Location, Action (progressive), Condition, Emotion

**Some adjectives change meaning:**
- ser aburrido = to be boring; estar aburrido = to be bored
- ser listo = to be smart; estar listo = to be ready
- ser malo = to be bad (character); estar malo = to be sick
- ser rico = to be rich; estar rico = to be delicious

#### Por vs. Para — Key Distinction

**Por (for/by/through/because of):**
- Cause/reason: por la lluvia (because of the rain)
- Duration: por dos horas (for two hours)
- Exchange: Pague diez dolares por el libro
- Movement through: Camino por el parque
- Means: por telefono, por avion
- Per: dos veces por semana
- Passive voice agent: escrito por Cervantes
- Common expressions: por favor, por supuesto, por eso, por fin, por lo menos

**Para (for/in order to/by a deadline):**
- Purpose/goal: Estudio para aprender
- Recipient: El regalo es para ti
- Destination: Salgo para Madrid
- Deadline: para el lunes (by Monday)
- Opinion: Para mi, es facil
- Comparison: Para un nino, es muy alto
- Employment: Trabajo para Google

#### Other Important Grammar Topics

**Pronouns:**
- Subject: yo, tu, el/ella/usted, nosotros, ellos/ellas/ustedes
- Direct object: me, te, lo/la, nos, los/las
- Indirect object: me, te, le, nos, les
- Reflexive: me, te, se, nos, se
- Double object pronouns: indirect before direct (Me lo dio = He gave it to me); le/les → se before lo/la/los/las
- Prepositional: mi, ti, el/ella/usted, nosotros, ellos/ellas/ustedes (conmigo, contigo)

**Adjective Agreement:**
- Adjectives agree in gender and number with the noun they modify
- Generally placed AFTER the noun: el libro rojo, la casa grande
- Some change meaning before/after: gran (great) vs. grande (big)

**Comparisons:**
- mas...que (more...than), menos...que (less...than), tan...como (as...as)
- Irregulars: mejor (better), peor (worse), mayor (older), menor (younger)

**Negative Words:**
- nada (nothing), nadie (nobody), nunca/jamas (never), ninguno (none), tampoco (neither), ni...ni (neither...nor)
- Double negatives are correct in Spanish: No tengo nada

### 3. Vocabulary (15%)

#### High-Frequency Topics
- **Family**: padre, madre, hermano, hermana, hijo, hija, abuelo, abuela, tio, tia, primo, prima, esposo/esposa
- **Food and restaurants**: desayuno, almuerzo, cena, carne, pollo, pescado, arroz, verduras, frutas, la cuenta, propina, mesero/camarero
- **Travel and transportation**: el aeropuerto, el avion, el tren, el autobus, el boleto, el equipaje, la aduana, el pasaporte
- **Health**: el medico, la enfermedad, la medicina, doler, la fiebre, el resfriado, la cita, la farmacia
- **School**: la clase, el examen, la tarea, el maestro/profesor, la biblioteca, la nota/calificacion
- **Shopping**: la tienda, comprar, vender, el precio, barato, caro, la talla, pagar, el dinero
- **Home**: la casa, la cocina, el dormitorio, el bano, la sala, el jardin, la escalera
- **Weather**: hace calor/frio/sol/viento, llueve, nieva, esta nublado
- **Body**: la cabeza, el brazo, la mano, la pierna, el pie, el estomago, los ojos, la boca
- **Professions**: abogado, medico, ingeniero, maestro, enfermero, bombero, policia

### 4. Sentence Completion (10%)

- Fill-in-the-blank questions testing grammar and vocabulary in context
- Focus on: correct verb form, preposition choice, pronoun placement, vocabulary fit
- Strategy: read the entire sentence first, identify the tense/mood needed, then select

### 5. Listening Skills Overview

- **Dialogues**: short conversations between two people
- **Narratives**: short spoken passages
- **Skills tested**: main idea, details, inference, speaker's intent
- **Tips**: listen for key words, don't panic if you miss a word, use context, pay attention to tone of voice
- **Practice**: listen to Spanish podcasts, music, news (e.g., CNN en Espanol, Notes in Spanish, Radio Ambulante)

---

## Study Tips

1. The subjunctive mood is heavily tested — master the WEIRDO triggers.
2. Preterite vs. imperfect is the most commonly tested grammar distinction.
3. Ser vs. estar and por vs. para appear on nearly every exam.
4. Build vocabulary through reading (news articles, short stories in Spanish).
5. Practice listening daily — even 15 minutes of Spanish audio helps enormously.
6. Learn irregular verb forms by heart; they come up constantly.
7. Focus on understanding overall meaning rather than translating every word.
8. Make flashcards for false cognates — they are designed to trick you.

## Practice Questions

**1.** Complete: "Cuando era nino, yo _______ al parque todos los dias."

A) fui
B) iba
C) voy
D) ire

**2.** Which sentence correctly uses the subjunctive?

A) Es importante que tu estudias.
B) Es importante que tu estudies.
C) Es importante que tu estudiar.
D) Es importante que tu estudiaste.

**3.** Choose the correct form: "El libro fue escrito _______ Cervantes."

A) para
B) por
C) de
D) con

**4.** "Mi hermana _______ enferma ayer." (ser or estar)

A) fue
B) estuvo
C) era
D) estaba

**5.** Which is the correct negative sentence?

A) No tengo nada.
B) No tengo algo.
C) Tengo no nada.
D) Nada no tengo.

**6.** "Yo quiero que ella _______ la verdad." (decir)

A) dice
B) diga
C) dijo
D) decir

**7.** Complete: "Ayer nosotros _______ una pelicula muy buena." (ver)

A) veimos
B) viamos
C) vimos
D) vemos

**8.** "_______ estudiante" means "I am a student."

A) Estoy
B) Soy
C) Tengo
D) Hago

**9.** Which word means "success" in Spanish?

A) Exito
B) Suceso
C) Salida
D) Exitar

**10.** Complete: "Ella trabaja _______ una empresa grande."

A) por
B) para
C) de
D) a

---

## Answer Key

1. **B) iba** — The imperfect is used for habitual past actions ("used to go"). "Todos los dias" (every day) signals a repeated action. Fui (preterite) would indicate a single, completed event.
2. **B) Es importante que tu estudies.** — "Es importante que" is an impersonal expression that triggers the subjunctive. "Estudies" is the present subjunctive of "estudiar."
3. **B) por** — In passive voice constructions, "por" indicates the agent (the person who did the action). "Written BY Cervantes."
4. **B) estuvo** — Being sick is a temporary condition (estar, not ser), and "ayer" (yesterday) indicates a completed event (preterite). Estaba would also be acceptable in some contexts if describing an ongoing state, but estuvo emphasizes the completed illness.
5. **A) No tengo nada.** — Double negatives are grammatically correct in Spanish. "No" before the verb and "nada" after is the standard construction.
6. **B) diga** — "Querer que" (to want that) triggers the subjunctive. "Diga" is the present subjunctive of "decir."
7. **C) vimos** — "Ver" in the preterite, nosotros form = vimos. "Ayer" (yesterday) signals preterite tense.
8. **B) Soy** — Occupation/identity uses "ser." "Soy estudiante" = I am a student.
9. **A) Exito** — "Exito" means success. "Suceso" means event/happening. "Salida" means exit. This is a common false cognate trap.
10. **B) para** — "Para" is used for employment ("works for a company"). "Por" would indicate cause or exchange, not employer.
