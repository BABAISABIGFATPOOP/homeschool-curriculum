# Grade 9 — Full Year Plan (36 Weeks)

## Math (Algebra I)

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Real Number System | Classifying numbers, properties of real numbers |
| 2 | Order of Operations & Expressions | Evaluating, simplifying with variables |
| 3 | Solving One- & Two-Step Equations | Review with focus on fluency |
| 4 | Multi-Step Equations | Variables on both sides, distributive property |
| 5 | Literal Equations | Solving for a specific variable (rearranging formulas) |
| 6 | Solving Inequalities | One-step, two-step, multi-step; graphing solutions |
| 7 | Compound Inequalities | "And" vs. "or", graphing on number line |
| 8 | Absolute Value Equations & Inequalities | Solving, graphing |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 1 test |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Relations & Functions | Domain, range, function notation, vertical line test |
| 11 | Graphing Linear Functions | Slope-intercept form, finding intercepts |
| 12 | Slope | From graphs, two points, equations; rate of change |
| 13 | Writing Linear Equations | Slope-intercept form, point-slope form |
| 14 | Writing Linear Equations | From tables, graphs, word problems |
| 15 | Standard Form | Ax + By = C, converting between forms |
| 16 | Parallel & Perpendicular Lines | Slopes of parallel/perpendicular lines, writing equations |
| 17 | Linear Inequalities in Two Variables | Graphing, shading, real-world applications |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Systems of Equations: Graphing | Solving by graphing, identifying solution types |
| 20 | Systems of Equations: Substitution | Solving algebraically |
| 21 | Systems of Equations: Elimination | Adding/subtracting equations to eliminate variables |
| 22 | Systems: Applications | Word problems, choosing a method |
| 23 | Exponent Rules | Product, quotient, power, zero, negative exponents |
| 24 | Polynomials: Intro | Classifying, degree, standard form |
| 25 | Adding & Subtracting Polynomials | Combining like terms |
| 26 | Multiplying Polynomials | Distributive property, FOIL, binomial × trinomial |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Factoring: GCF & Grouping | Factoring out GCF, factor by grouping |
| 29 | Factoring Trinomials | x² + bx + c, finding factors |
| 30 | Factoring: Special Cases | Difference of squares, perfect square trinomials |
| 31 | Quadratic Equations: Square Roots | Solving x² = k, (x + a)² = k |
| 32 | Quadratic Equations: Factoring | Setting equal to zero, solving |
| 33 | Quadratic Formula | Deriving (overview), using, discriminant |
| 34 | Graphing Quadratics | Vertex, axis of symmetry, intercepts, direction |
| 35 | Review | Full year review, focus on weak areas |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test |

## English

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)

| 24 | Writing: Research Paper | Outlining, drafting, integrating sources |

| 26 | Writing: Research Paper | Revision, editing, final draft |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)

## Biology (High School Biology)

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Nature of Science | Scientific method, experimental design, peer review |
| 2 | Biochemistry: Water | Properties of water, pH, buffers |
| 3 | Biochemistry: Carbon & Macromolecules | Carbon bonding, organic vs. inorganic |
| 4 | Biochemistry: Carbohydrates & Lipids | Structure, function, examples |
| 5 | Biochemistry: Proteins | Amino acids, structure levels, enzymes |
| 6 | Biochemistry: Nucleic Acids | DNA & RNA structure, nucleotides |
| 7 | Lab: Enzyme Activity | Testing catalase with hydrogen peroxide, varying conditions |
| 8 | Biochemistry: Review & Connections | How macromolecules work together in organisms |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 1 test, lab report |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Cell Structure | Prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic, organelles in detail |
| 11 | Cell Membrane | Fluid mosaic model, phospholipid bilayer |
| 12 | Cell Transport | Passive (diffusion, osmosis, facilitated) vs. active transport |
| 13 | Lab: Osmosis & Diffusion | Dialysis tubing experiment |
| 14 | Photosynthesis | Light-dependent reactions, Calvin cycle overview |
| 15 | Photosynthesis | Factors affecting rate, lab (leaf disk assay) |
| 16 | Cellular Respiration | Glycolysis, Krebs cycle, electron transport chain |
| 17 | Cellular Respiration | ATP yield, aerobic vs. anaerobic, fermentation |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test, lab reports |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | DNA Structure & Replication | Watson & Crick, semiconservative replication |
| 20 | Transcription | DNA → mRNA, RNA polymerase, promoter |
| 21 | Translation | mRNA → protein, ribosomes, tRNA, codons |
| 22 | Gene Regulation | Operons (prokaryotes), gene expression basics |
| 23 | Mutations & Biotechnology | Types of mutations, genetic engineering, CRISPR intro |
| 24 | Genetics: Mendel's Laws | Dominance, segregation, independent assortment |
| 25 | Genetics: Beyond Mendel | Incomplete dominance, codominance, epistasis, polygenic traits |
| 26 | Genetics: Sex-Linked & Pedigrees | X-linked traits, reading pedigree charts |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test, genetics problem sets |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Evolution: Evidence | Fossil record, biogeography, comparative anatomy, molecular |
| 29 | Evolution: Mechanisms | Natural selection, genetic drift, gene flow, mutation |
| 30 | Evolution: Speciation | Reproductive isolation, adaptive radiation, convergent evolution |
| 31 | Ecology: Ecosystems | Energy flow, trophic levels, productivity |
| 32 | Ecology: Populations | Growth models, carrying capacity, density-dependent factors |
| 33 | Ecology: Communities & Biomes | Interactions, succession, major biomes |
| 34 | Ecology: Human Impact | Climate change, biodiversity loss, conservation |
| 35 | Review | Full year review, focus on weak areas |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test, cumulative lab practical |
