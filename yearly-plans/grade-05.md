# Grade 5 — Full Year Plan (36 Weeks)

## Math

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Place Value to Millions | Reading, writing, comparing large numbers |
| 2 | Rounding & Estimating | Rounding to any place, estimating sums/differences |
| 3 | Multi-Digit Addition & Subtraction | Up to 6 digits, word problems |
| 4 | Multiplication Review | Facts fluency, properties of multiplication |
| 5 | Multi-Digit Multiplication | 2-digit × 2-digit, standard algorithm |
| 6 | Multi-Digit Multiplication | 3-digit × 2-digit, word problems |
| 7 | Long Division | 3-digit ÷ 1-digit, interpreting remainders |
| 8 | Long Division | 4-digit ÷ 2-digit |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 1 test |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Fractions Review | Equivalent fractions, simplifying, comparing |
| 11 | Adding Fractions | Like denominators, then unlike denominators (finding LCD) |
| 12 | Subtracting Fractions | Like and unlike denominators |
| 13 | Mixed Numbers | Adding & subtracting mixed numbers |
| 14 | Multiplying Fractions | Fraction × whole number, fraction × fraction |
| 15 | Fraction Word Problems | Multi-step problems with all operations |
| 16 | Decimals: Place Value | Tenths, hundredths, thousandths; reading & writing |
| 17 | Comparing & Ordering Decimals | Using place value, number line |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Adding & Subtracting Decimals | Lining up decimal points, estimating |
| 20 | Multiplying Decimals | By whole numbers, by decimals, placing the decimal |
| 21 | Dividing Decimals | By whole numbers, intro to dividing by decimals |
| 22 | Order of Operations | PEMDAS, evaluating expressions |
| 23 | Intro to Expressions | Writing and evaluating simple expressions with variables |
| 24 | Coordinate Plane | Plotting ordered pairs in Quadrant I, reading coordinates |
| 25 | Geometry: Triangles | Classifying by sides and angles |
| 26 | Area of Rectangles & Triangles | Formulas, calculating, word problems |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Volume | Volume of rectangular prisms, cubic units |
| 29 | Volume Word Problems | Real-world volume problems, composite shapes |
| 30 | Measurement Conversions | Customary (in/ft/yd, oz/lb, cup/gal), metric (mm/cm/m/km, g/kg, mL/L) |
| 31 | Data: Line Plots | Creating and interpreting line plots with fractions |
| 32 | Data: Mean, Median, Mode | Finding averages, intro to statistics |
| 33 | Patterns & Relationships | Number patterns, graphing relationships on coordinate plane |
| 34 | Problem Solving Strategies | Draw a picture, work backwards, guess & check |
| 35 | Review | Full year review |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test |

## Biology

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Scientific Method Review | Question, hypothesis, experiment, results, conclusion |
| 2 | Cells: The Building Blocks | What cells are, all living things are made of cells |
| 3 | Plant Cells vs. Animal Cells | Cell wall, chloroplasts, cell membrane, nucleus |
| 4 | Cell Parts & Functions | Nucleus, cytoplasm, membrane, vacuole (basic organelles) |
| 5 | Looking at Cells | Microscope intro (if available), cell diagrams, labeling |
| 6 | Single-Celled Organisms | Bacteria, amoeba, paramecium — life at the micro level |
| 7 | Fungi | Mushrooms, mold, yeast — decomposers |
| 8 | Microorganisms & Health | Germs, hygiene, helpful vs. harmful microbes |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 1 test, cell diagram project |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Photosynthesis | How plants make food, sunlight + water + CO₂ → sugar + O₂ |
| 11 | Photosynthesis Experiment | Growing plants in different light conditions |
| 12 | Food Chains Review | Producers, consumers (herbivores, carnivores, omnivores), decomposers |
| 13 | Food Webs | Interconnected food chains, what happens when one part is removed |
| 14 | Energy Flow in Ecosystems | Energy pyramids, why there are fewer top predators |
| 15 | Adaptations | Structural & behavioral adaptations for survival |
| 16 | Ecosystems & Biomes Intro | Desert, forest, ocean, tundra, grassland |
| 17 | Human Impact on Ecosystems | Pollution, deforestation, endangered species |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test, ecosystem diorama/poster |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Human Body: Digestive System | Mouth to stomach to intestines, how food is broken down |
| 20 | Human Body: Digestive System Lab | Model digestive system activity, food diary |
| 21 | Human Body: Respiratory System | Lungs, breathing, oxygen exchange |
| 22 | Human Body: Circulatory System Intro | Heart, blood, blood vessels (preview for Grade 6) |
| 23 | Nutrition Deep Dive | Nutrients (carbs, protein, fat, vitamins, minerals), reading labels |
| 24 | Health: Disease & Prevention | How germs spread, immune system basics, vaccines intro |
| 25 | Animal Reproduction Basics | Egg-laying vs. live birth, caring for young |
| 26 | Plant Reproduction | Seeds, pollination, seed dispersal methods |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Classification Intro | Why scientists classify, sorting living things into groups |
| 29 | Vertebrates Deep Dive | Mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish — detailed |
| 30 | Invertebrates | Insects, spiders, worms, jellyfish, mollusks |
| 31 | Animal Behavior | Instinct vs. learned behavior, communication |
| 32 | Endangered Species & Conservation | Why species go extinct, conservation efforts |
| 33 | Science Fair Project | Choose a biology question, plan experiment |
| 34 | Science Fair Project | Conduct experiment, collect data |
| 35 | Science Fair Project | Create display, present findings |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test, science fair presentations |
