# Grade 4 — Full Year Plan (36 Weeks)

## Math

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Place Value Review | Ones through thousands, reading & writing numbers |
| 2 | Place Value Extended | Ten thousands & hundred thousands, comparing & ordering |
| 3 | Rounding | Rounding to nearest 10, 100, 1,000 |
| 4 | Addition Strategies | Multi-digit addition with regrouping, estimating sums |
| 5 | Subtraction Strategies | Multi-digit subtraction with regrouping, estimating differences |
| 6 | Addition & Subtraction Word Problems | Multi-step word problems, choosing operations |
| 7 | Intro to Multiplication | Multiplication as repeated addition, arrays, times tables 0–5 |
| 8 | Multiplication Facts | Times tables 6–12, properties of multiplication |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 1 test, fact fluency check |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Multi-Digit Multiplication | 2-digit × 1-digit, area model |
| 11 | Multi-Digit Multiplication | 3-digit × 1-digit, standard algorithm |
| 12 | Intro to Division | Division as sharing/grouping, relationship to multiplication |
| 13 | Division Facts | Dividing by 1–12, remainders intro |
| 14 | Long Division | 2-digit ÷ 1-digit, step-by-step process |
| 15 | Multiplication & Division Word Problems | Choosing operations, multi-step problems |
| 16 | Intro to Fractions | What fractions are, numerator/denominator, fractions on a number line |
| 17 | Comparing Fractions | Like denominators, unlike denominators with visuals, equivalent fractions |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Equivalent Fractions | Finding equivalents, simplifying |
| 20 | Adding & Subtracting Fractions | Like denominators only |
| 21 | Mixed Numbers | Identifying, converting between improper fractions and mixed numbers |
| 22 | Decimals Intro | Tenths and hundredths, decimal notation, money connections |
| 23 | Comparing Decimals | Using place value to compare, ordering decimals |
| 24 | Geometry: Lines & Angles | Points, lines, rays, segments, right/acute/obtuse angles |
| 25 | Geometry: Shapes | Classifying triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons |
| 26 | Perimeter | Finding perimeter of rectangles and irregular shapes |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Area | Area of rectangles, square units |
| 29 | Area & Perimeter Combined | Distinguishing area vs. perimeter, word problems |
| 30 | Measurement: Length | Customary & metric units, converting within systems |
| 31 | Measurement: Weight & Capacity | Ounces/pounds, grams/kilograms, cups/liters |
| 32 | Time & Elapsed Time | Telling time, calculating elapsed time |
| 33 | Patterns & Sequences | Number patterns, input/output tables, growing patterns |
| 34 | Data & Graphs | Bar graphs, line plots, interpreting data |
| 35 | Review | Full year review, practice test |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test |

## English

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Reading: Fiction Basics | Story elements (character, setting, plot), read-aloud chapter book |
| 2 | Nouns & Verbs | Common/proper nouns, action/linking verbs |
| 3 | Reading: Main Idea & Details | Identifying main idea in short passages |
| 4 | Adjectives & Adverbs | Descriptive words, comparative forms |
| 5 | Writing: Complete Sentences | Subjects & predicates, sentence vs. fragment |
| 6 | Reading: Sequencing | Retelling events in order, transition words (first, then, finally) |
| 7 | Spelling Patterns | Short/long vowels, common patterns (CVC, CVCe) |
| 8 | Writing: Personal Narrative | Brainstorming, drafting a personal story |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Reading comprehension test, grammar quiz |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Reading: Nonfiction | Text features (headings, captions, glossary), informational texts |
| 11 | Sentence Types | Declarative, interrogative, imperative, exclamatory |
| 12 | Reading: Compare & Contrast | Venn diagrams, comparing two texts or characters |
| 13 | Subject-Verb Agreement | Matching subjects and verbs, singular/plural |
| 14 | Writing: Paragraphs | Topic sentence, supporting details, closing sentence |
| 15 | Vocabulary Building | Context clues, using a dictionary |
| 16 | Reading: Poetry | Rhyme, rhythm, imagery; reading & discussing poems |
| 17 | Writing: Book Report | Summarizing a book, giving opinions with evidence |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test, book report due |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Reading: Chapter Book Study | Begin a novel, daily reading log, chapter discussions |
| 20 | Pronouns | Subject/object pronouns, possessives |
| 21 | Reading: Making Inferences | Using clues from text + background knowledge |
| 22 | Punctuation | Commas in lists, apostrophes, quotation marks |
| 23 | Writing: Descriptive Writing | Using sensory details, show don't tell |
| 24 | Reading: Cause & Effect | Identifying cause/effect relationships in texts |
| 25 | Spelling & Vocabulary | Prefixes (un-, re-, pre-), suffixes (-ly, -ful, -less) |
| 26 | Writing: Opinion Piece | Stating an opinion, giving reasons, concluding |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test, novel wrap-up |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Reading: Myths & Folktales | Reading traditional stories, identifying morals/lessons |
| 29 | Verb Tenses | Past, present, future; irregular verbs |
| 30 | Writing: Research Mini-Project | Choosing a topic, finding 2–3 sources, note-taking |
| 31 | Writing: Research Mini-Project | Drafting & revising a short report |
| 32 | Reading: Author's Purpose | Persuade, inform, entertain |
| 33 | Handwriting & Presentation | Cursive practice, neat final copies |
| 34 | Oral Presentation | Presenting research project to family |
| 35 | Review | Full year reading & grammar review |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test, reading log reflection |

## Biology

### Quarter 1 (Weeks 1–9)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | What is Science? | Observation, asking questions, science journals |
| 2 | Living vs. Nonliving | Characteristics of living things (growth, reproduction, response) |
| 3 | Plants: Parts & Functions | Roots, stems, leaves, flowers — what each does |
| 4 | Plants: Life Cycle | Seed → sprout → plant → flower → seed; plant a seed experiment |
| 5 | Plants: Needs | Water, sunlight, soil, air; design an experiment |
| 6 | Animal Groups: Mammals & Birds | Characteristics, examples, warm-blooded |
| 7 | Animal Groups: Reptiles & Amphibians | Characteristics, examples, cold-blooded |
| 8 | Animal Groups: Fish & Insects | Characteristics, examples, diversity |
| 9 | Review & Assessment | Animal classification quiz, journal check |

### Quarter 2 (Weeks 10–18)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 10 | Habitats: Forests & Deserts | What makes a habitat, how animals adapt |
| 11 | Habitats: Oceans & Freshwater | Aquatic ecosystems, saltwater vs. freshwater |
| 12 | Habitats: Grasslands & Tundra | Extreme environments, animal adaptations |
| 13 | Food Chains | Producers, consumers, decomposers |
| 14 | Food Webs | How food chains connect, predator/prey |
| 15 | Ecosystems | Living and nonliving parts working together |
| 16 | Caring for the Environment | Pollution, conservation, recycling |
| 17 | Nature Walk / Field Study | Observe local ecosystem, record findings |
| 18 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 2 test, ecosystem poster project |

### Quarter 3 (Weeks 19–27)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 19 | Human Body: Skeletal System | Bones, joints, why we need a skeleton |
| 20 | Human Body: Muscular System | Types of muscles, how muscles & bones work together |
| 21 | Human Body: Skin | Layers of skin, protection, touch |
| 22 | Health & Nutrition Basics | Food groups, balanced meals, why nutrition matters |
| 23 | Exercise & Rest | Why the body needs movement and sleep |
| 24 | Animal Life Cycles | Metamorphosis (butterfly, frog), mammal life cycles |
| 25 | Animal Behaviors | Migration, hibernation, camouflage |
| 26 | Inherited Traits vs. Learned Behaviors | What you're born with vs. what you learn |
| 27 | Review & Assessment | Quarter 3 test |

### Quarter 4 (Weeks 28–36)
| Week | Topic | Details |
|------|-------|---------|
| 28 | Simple Experiments | Asking a question, making a hypothesis |
| 29 | Variables & Fair Tests | Independent/dependent variables, controls (simple terms) |
| 30 | Recording & Measuring | Using rulers, thermometers, scales; recording data |
| 31 | Reading Data | Simple tables, bar graphs, drawing conclusions |
| 32 | Mini Science Project | Choose a question, plan an experiment |
| 33 | Mini Science Project | Conduct experiment, record results |
| 34 | Mini Science Project | Create a poster or presentation |
| 35 | Review | Full year review, science journal reflection |
| 36 | Final Assessment | End-of-year test, project presentations |
